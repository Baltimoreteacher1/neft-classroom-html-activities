Ingredient Conversion Station

Ingredient Mixed Number Your Improper Fraction Show Work
🍞 Flour 2 1/2 cups
🍬 Sugar 1 3/4 cups
🥛 Milk 3 2/3 cups
🧈 Butter 1 1/8 cups

📚 Support: Mixed Number to Improper Fraction Steps

Step 1: Multiply the whole number by the denominator
Step 2: Add the numerator
Step 3: Place the result over the original denominator

Example: 2 1/2 → (2 × 2 + 1) / 2 = 5/2

For 2 1/2: Multiply 2 × 2 = 4, then add 1 = 5. The improper fraction is 5/2. Follow the same pattern for each ingredient!

⚡ Chef's Challenge: Triple Batch

A banquet hall orders 3 times the recipe. Multiply each original ingredient by 3 and convert any improper fractions to mixed numbers:

Multiply numerator × numerator and denominator × denominator. For 3/4 × 1/2: top = 3×1 = 3, bottom = 4×2 = 8, so the answer is 3/8.

2.3
Portion Division — Dividing Fractions

The kitchen has limited ingredients. Figure out how many servings you can make by dividing the total amount by the amount per serving.

Situation Total Amount Amount Per Serving Division Expression Number of Servings
🍰 Cake batter 3/4 cup 1/8 cup each
🍛 Pizza dough 2/3 pound 1/6 pound each
🥦 Juice 5/6 gallon 1/3 gallon each
🍦 Frosting 7/8 cup 1/4 cup each

Keep the first fraction, Change ÷ to ×, Flip the second fraction. For 3/4 ÷ 1/8: Keep 3/4, change to ×, flip 1/8 to 8/1. Then 3/4 × 8/1 = 24/4 = 6 servings.

3.1
Party Size Scaling

The Garden Pasta recipe serves 4 people. A party of 6 is coming in. You need to multiply each ingredient by 6/4 (which simplifies to 3/2). Scale each ingredient:

Ingredient Original (serves 4) × 3/2 = Scaled Amount Simplified
🍝 Pasta 2/3 pound
🥑 Garlic 3/4 tablespoon
🥔 Olive Oil 1/3 cup
🧀 Cheese 5/8 cup

For 2/3 × 3/2: numerator = 2×3 = 6, denominator = 3×2 = 6, so 6/6 = 1. One pound of pasta! Notice that 3 appears in both the numerator and denominator — you can cross-cancel.

3.3
Kitchen Math Word Problems

Problem 1: The kitchen has 3 1/2 pounds of chicken. Each serving uses 2/3 of a pound. How many full servings can the chef make?

Problem 2: A recipe calls for 2/3 cup of oil. The chef only has a 1/4-cup measuring cup. How many scoops does the chef need?

Problem 3: The bakery uses 5/6 of a bag of flour each day. They have 4 1/6 bags in storage. How many full days will the flour last?

📚 Support: Word Problem Strategy

1. Read carefully — what operation is needed?
2. "How many ___ fit in ___?" = Division
3. "What is ___ of ___?" = Multiplication
4. Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions first
5. Simplify your final answer

4.1
Catering Order — Multi-Step Challenge

A wedding reception needs 60 servings of your signature dessert. The original recipe makes 8 servings. You need to find the scaling factor and calculate each ingredient.

Now multiply each ingredient by your scaling factor:

Ingredient Original (8 srv) × Scaling Factor Amount for 60
🍞 Flour 1 1/2 cups
🧈 Butter 3/4 cup
🍬 Sugar 2/3 cup
🥚 Eggs 5

60 ÷ 8 = 60/8 = 15/2 = 7 1/2. So multiply each ingredient by 15/2. Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions first: 1 1/2 = 3/2, then 3/2 × 15/2 = 45/4 = 11 1/4 cups.
