Statistics Vocabulary Review
Master these statistical measures before analyzing data.
📊
Mean (Average)
Add all values, then divide by the count. Data: 3, 5, 7 → Mean = (3+5+7) / 3 = 5
📉
Median (Middle)
The middle value when data is ordered. Data: 2, 5, 9 → Median = 5
📈
Mode (Most Common)
The value that appears most often. Data: 3, 5, 5, 7 → Mode = 5
📐
Range (Spread)
Maximum minus minimum. Data: 2, 5, 9 → Range = 9 - 2 = 7
First order the data: 4, 4, 6, 8, 8, 8, 10. Sum = 48, count = 7, so mean = 48/7. Median is the middle (4th) value. Mode is most frequent. Range = max - min.
📚 Support: Step 1: Order the data least to greatest. Step 2: Mean = add all then divide by how many. Step 3: Median = cross off from both ends until you reach the middle. Step 4: Mode = whichever number appears most. Step 5: Range = biggest - smallest.

Key vocabulary:

  • mean: The average: add all values, then divide by how many there are.
  • median: The middle value when the numbers are put in order.
  • mode: The value that appears most often in a data set.
  • range: The difference between the largest and smallest values.
  • outlier: A value much higher or lower than the rest of the data.
  • distribution: How the data is spread out across the values.
