Task 4: The Missing Deposit — Addition Equation
A bank reported that an account had a deposit of $28 added, bringing the total to $75. How much was originally in the account?
x + 28 Left Side
=
75 Right Side
📚 Support: To solve x + 28 = 75, subtract 28 from both sides: x = 75 − 28 = 47. Addition equations are solved with subtraction (inverse operation).
Since 28 is being added to x, use the inverse operation (subtraction). Subtract 28 from both sides.

Task 5: The Stolen Evidence — Subtraction Equation
A detective had a collection of evidence items but 14 went missing. After the theft, 23 items remained. How many items were there originally?
n − 14 Left Side
=
23 Right Side
📚 Support: To solve n − 14 = 23, add 14 to both sides: n = 23 + 14 = 37. Subtraction equations are solved with addition (inverse operation).
Since 14 is being subtracted from n, use the inverse operation (addition). Add 14 to both sides.

Task 6: The Counterfeit Ring — Multiplication Equation
Investigators discovered 5 identical counterfeit money bags worth a total of $145. How much was in each bag?
5m Left Side
=
145 Right Side
📚 Support: To solve 5m = 145, divide both sides by 5: m = 145 ÷ 5 = 29. Multiplication equations are solved with division.
5m means “5 times m.” To undo multiplication, divide both sides by 5.

Task 9: Write the Inequality — Height Requirement
To join the detective academy, a candidate must be at least 54 inches tall. Write an inequality using h for height.
📚 Support: “At least” means greater than or equal to, which uses the ≥ symbol. Written as: h >= 54.
“At least 54” means 54 or more. The candidate can be 54 inches tall (exactly) or taller. Which symbol includes the value itself?
10
Task 10: Graph the Inequality — Suspect Age Range
The suspect is older than 25. This means x > 25. Describe how you would graph this on a number line. Is the circle open or closed? Which direction does the arrow point?
📚 Support: Open circle = the value is NOT included (> or <). Closed circle = the value IS included (≥ or ≤). Since x > 25 does NOT include 25 itself, we use an open circle. The arrow points right (toward bigger numbers).
> means “greater than” but NOT equal to. So 25 itself is not a solution. What kind of circle represents “not included”?
11
Task 11: Solve and Graph — The Budget Constraint
The department has a $200 budget for an investigation. Equipment costs $40 upfront, and each hour of surveillance costs $20. How many hours of surveillance can the team afford?

40 + 20h ≤ 200
📚 Support: Step 1: Subtract 40 from both sides: 20h ≤ 160. Step 2: Divide both sides by 20: h ≤ 8. The team can afford up to 8 hours of surveillance.
🌟 Challenge: What if the budget was $300 instead? How many hours could the team afford then? Show your work.
This is a two-step inequality. First undo the addition (+40), then undo the multiplication (×20). Keep the ≤ sign pointing the same way.

Task 12: Open vs. Closed Circle
For each inequality, decide whether the boundary point uses an open circle or a closed circle.
Case Inequality Circle Type
(a) x > 7
(b) y ≤ 3
(c) n ≥ 10
(d) p < 5
📚 Support: Open circle is used for < and > (the value is NOT part of the solution). Closed circle is used for ≤ and ≥ (the value IS part of the solution).
Remember: if the symbol has a line underneath (≤ or ≥), it means “or equal to,” so the endpoint is included = closed circle.

Task 13: The Jewelry Heist
Investigators found that 4 identical jewelry cases were stolen. The total value of the stolen goods is $2,340. How much is each case worth?

4c = 2340
4c Left Side
=
2340 Right Side
📚 Support: To solve 4c = 2340, divide both sides by 4: c = 2340 ÷ 4 = 585. Each case is worth $585.
4c means 4 times c. To find one case, divide the total by 4. Try: 2340 ÷ 4.
14
Task 14: The Speeding Suspect
A suspect drove 324 miles in 6 hours. What was the suspect’s average speed?

6s = 324

Then: The speed limit is 60 mph. Was the suspect speeding?
6s Left Side
=
324 Right Side
📚 Support: The formula is distance = speed × time, or d = s × t. So 6s = 324 ⇒ s = 324 ÷ 6 = 54 mph. Since 54 < 60, the suspect was NOT speeding.
First find the speed by dividing 324 by 6. Then compare your answer to 60. Is it more or less than 60?
15
Task 15: The Ransom Note — Two-Step Equation
A ransom note demands that half of the stolen amount plus $120 equals $400. What was the total stolen amount?

x/2 + 120 = 400
x/2 + 120 Left Side
=
400 Right Side
📚 Support: This is a two-step equation.
Step 1: Subtract 120 from both sides: x/2 = 280.
Step 2: Multiply both sides by 2: x = 560.
🌟 Challenge: Write your own two-step equation with a real-world scenario. Include the equation and the solution.
Two-step equations: undo addition/subtraction first, then undo multiplication/division. Think of “reverse PEMDAS.”
16
Task 16: The Witness Timeline
A witness saw the suspect at the scene for more than 3 hours but no more than 7 hours. Write this as a compound inequality using t for time, then name three integers that satisfy it.

3 < t ≤ 7
📚 Support: “More than 3” means t > 3 (open circle at 3, not included). “No more than 7” means t ≤ 7 (closed circle at 7, included). Together: 3 < t ≤ 7. Valid integers: 4, 5, 6, 7.
“More than” = strictly greater than (>). “No more than” = less than or equal to (≤). Which whole numbers are between 3 and 7 following these rules?

Key vocabulary:

  • equation: A math sentence that says two things are equal, using an equals sign.
  • inequality: A math sentence that compares using <, >, ≤, or ≥.
  • solution: The value of the variable that makes an equation true.
  • variable: A letter that stands for a number we do not know yet.
  • inverse operation: The opposite operation used to undo another (e.g. subtract undoes add).
  • isolate: To get the variable alone on one side of the equation.
🧠 How to work through this (Level 1)

Take it one small step at a time:

  1. Read slowly. Underlined words have a meaning bubble — tap them.
  2. Find the numbers in the problem and write them down.
  3. Pick the operation (+, −, ×, ÷). Ask: am I joining, taking away, grouping, or sharing?
  4. Use the 💡 Show Hint buttons any time you feel stuck.
  5. Check your answer. Does it make sense for the story?

Tip: It is okay to use smaller, friendlier numbers to practice first. The hints walk you through each step.
