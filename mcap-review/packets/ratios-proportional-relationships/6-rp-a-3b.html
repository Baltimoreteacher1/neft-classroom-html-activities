Solve Unit-Rate Problems

Ratios & Proportional Relationships
🎯 Learning Goal: Use unit rates to solve real problems about speed, price, and steady amounts.

Vocabulary

constant speedA speed that stays the same the whole time.
unit priceThe cost of exactly 1 item or 1 unit.
totalThe full amount you get after combining all the units.

What You Need to Know

  • First find the unit rate, then multiply to get a bigger amount.
  • Distance = speed × time when speed stays constant.
  • To find the better deal, compare the unit prices (cost per 1).
  • At a steady rate, you can scale up or down to any amount.
  • Example: if 1 hour = 50 miles, then 4 hours = 50 × 4 = 200 miles.
  • Lining up the unit (per 1) makes multiplying easy.

Worked Example I Do — watch how it works

A train travels at a constant speed of 60 miles per hour. How far does it travel in 4.5 hours?
  1. Step 1. The unit rate is 60 miles in 1 hour.
  2. Step 2. Multiply the unit rate by the number of hours: 60 × 4.5.
  3. Step 3. 60 × 4.5 = 270.
Answer: 270 miles

Guided Practice We Do — try it together

  1. 1. Oranges cost $0.40 each. How much do 7 oranges cost?
    💡 Hint: Multiply the unit price by the number of oranges: 0.40 × 7.
  2. 2. A walker moves at 3 miles per hour. How far in 2.5 hours?
    💡 Hint: Multiply 3 by 2.5.
  3. 3. Store A: 6 pens for $3.00. Store B: 10 pens for $4.50. Which is cheaper per pen?
    💡 Hint: Find each unit price: 3.00 ÷ 6 and 4.50 ÷ 10.

Independent Practice You Do — show your work

  1. 1. A car goes 55 miles per hour. How far in 3 hours?
  2. 2. Bananas cost $0.25 each. How much for 12 bananas?
  3. 3. A pump moves 8 gallons per minute. How many gallons in 15 minutes?
  4. 4. A cyclist rides 14 miles per hour. How long to ride 42 miles?
  5. 5. Which is the better buy: 4 lbs for $10 or 6 lbs for $13.50?

MCAP-Style Practice

  1. 1. A runner keeps a steady pace of 8 minutes per mile. How long to run 5 miles?
  2. 2. Apples are $1.80 for 3 pounds. How much for 5 pounds at the same rate?
  3. 3. A boat travels at a constant 22 miles per hour. Fill in: in 3 hours it travels ____ miles.
