Expressions & Equations
🎯 Learning Goal: Translate words into algebraic expressions and identify their parts, such as terms and coefficients.

Vocabulary

variableA letter that stands for an unknown number, like x or n.
termA single part of an expression separated by + or − signs. In 3x + 5, the terms are 3x and 5.
coefficientThe number multiplied by a variable. In 7y, the coefficient is 7.
constantA term that is just a number with no variable, like the 5 in 3x + 5.
expressionA math phrase with numbers, variables, and operations but no equal sign.

What You Need to Know

  • 'Sum' means add, 'difference' means subtract, 'product' means multiply, 'quotient' means divide.
  • 'More than' and 'increased by' mean add; 'less than' and 'decreased by' mean subtract.
  • Watch the order: '5 less than x' is x − 5, NOT 5 − x.
  • A coefficient sits in front of a variable; 'x' alone has a coefficient of 1.
  • Terms are separated by + or − signs; count them to know how many terms there are.
  • 'Twice a number' means 2 × the number, written 2n.

Worked Example I Do — watch how it works

Write an expression for 'the cost of t tickets at $9 each, plus a $4 service fee,' then name the coefficient and constant.
  1. Step 1. Cost of tickets is $9 times the number of tickets: 9t.
  2. Step 2. Add the flat $4 service fee: 9t + 4.
  3. Step 3. The coefficient is 9 (multiplies t) and the constant is 4.
Answer: 9t + 4; coefficient 9, constant 4

Guided Practice We Do — try it together

  1. 1. Write 'a number n decreased by 7' as an expression.
    💡 Hint: 'Decreased by' means subtract, in the order given.
  2. 2. How many terms are in 4x + 2y + 9, and what is the coefficient of y?
    💡 Hint: Count parts separated by + signs; the coefficient is the number in front of y.
  3. 3. Write 'the product of 6 and a number x' as an expression.
    💡 Hint: 'Product' means multiply.

Independent Practice You Do — show your work

  1. 1. Write '8 more than a number m' as an expression.
  2. 2. Write '3 less than twice a number p' as an expression.
  3. 3. Identify the coefficient and constant in 10g + 15.
  4. 4. Write 'the quotient of a number y and 5' as an expression.
  5. 5. How many terms are in the expression 5a + 7 − b?

  2. 2. In the expression 7x + 12, which statement is true?
