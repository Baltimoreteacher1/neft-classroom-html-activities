  • I count the total number of decimal places because I am multiplying.
  • I move the decimal in the divisor to make a whole number because I am dividing.
  • My answer is reasonable because it is close to __________.

Decimal Point

The dot that separates whole numbers and parts of a whole.

Place Value

The value of a digit based on its position: ones, tenths, hundredths, thousandths.

Product

The answer to a multiplication problem.

Quotient

The answer to a division problem.

Multiply Decimals

Multiply

Problem: 2.4 × 1.3

  24
× 13
 72
240
 312
3.12
Step 1: Ignore the decimal points at first.
Step 2: Multiply 24 × 13 = 312.
Step 3: Count the decimal places in the factors: 1 place in 2.4 and 1 place in 1.3.
Step 4: Put the decimal in the product so there are 2 decimal places total: 3.12.
Visual guide:
  • Multiply like whole numbers first.
  • Then count all decimal places in both factors.
  • Move the decimal into the product.
Estimate: 2.4 × 1.3 is a little more than 2.4, so 3.12 is reasonable.

Divide Decimals

Divide

Problem: 8.64 ÷ 0.3

8.64 ÷ 0.3
86.4 ÷ 3
28.8
Step 1: Make the divisor a whole number.
Step 2: Move the decimal in 0.3 one place right to make 3.
Step 3: Move the decimal in 8.64 one place right too, so it becomes 86.4.
Step 4: Divide 86.4 by 3 to get 28.8.
Visual guide:
  • Only move decimals to make the divisor a whole number.
  • Move the decimal in both numbers the same number of places.
  • Then divide as usual.
Estimate: Dividing by 0.3 makes the answer bigger than 8.64.
