Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

Quadrant

One of the four regions the x- and y-axes split the plane into. Signs: I (+,+), II (−,+), III (−,−), IV (+,−).

Cuadrante: una de las cuatro regiones del plano. Signos: I (+,+), II (−,+), III (−,−), IV (+,−).

Reflection

A flip over an axis like a mirror. Over the x-axis: (x, y) → (x, −y). Over the y-axis: (x, y) → (−x, y).

Reflexión: un giro de espejo sobre un eje. Sobre el eje x: (x, y) → (x, −y).

Absolute Value

|n| is the distance from 0 on a number line — never negative. |−3| = 3 and |3| = 3.

Valor absoluto: |n| es la distancia desde 0 — nunca es negativo. |−3| = 3.

Coordinate / Integer

An ordered pair (x, y): x is left/right, y is up/down. Integers are whole numbers including negatives: …−2, −1, 0, 1, 2…

Par ordenado (x, y); enteros incluyen negativos: …−2, −1, 0, 1, 2…

Sea Level = 0 (reference)

Zero is the reference point. Above zero is positive, below zero is negative. Like the origin on the plane.

El nivel del mar = 0 es el punto de referencia: arriba es positivo, abajo es negativo.

Formula Bank

Distance, shared x: |y₂ − y₁| (vertical line)

Distance, shared y: |x₂ − x₁| (horizontal line)

Reflect over x-axis: (x, y) → (x, −y)

Reflect over y-axis: (x, y) → (−x, y)

Absolute value: |n| = distance from 0

Quadrant signs: I (+,+) · II (−,+) · III (−,−) · IV (+,−)

Diagonal (Level 2 preview): right triangle legs |Δx| and |Δy|; diagonal² = |Δx|² + |Δy|² (Pythagoras)

🪞 Mirror one of your places (reflection)

Reflect a place across an axis to plan a "mirrored" section of your map.

Need a hint?

Across the x-axis: keep x, negate y → (3, 4) → (3, −4). Across the y-axis: negate x, keep y → (3, 4) → (−3, 4). Both: negate both → (−3, −4).

💡 Guided Steps & Help

Reflecting a point across an axis:

  1. Reflecting across the x-axis (a horizontal flip up/down) keeps the x-value the same and changes the sign of the y-value: (x, y) → (x, −y).
  2. Reflecting across the y-axis (a horizontal flip left/right) keeps the y-value the same and changes the sign of the x-value: (x, y) → (−x, y).
  3. The reflected point is the same distance from the axis as the original — just on the opposite side.
💡 Example: Reflect (4, 3) across the x-axis → (4, −3). Across the y-axis → (−4, 3).

📏 Measure a walking distance (absolute value)

If two places share an x or a y, the distance is the absolute difference of the other coordinate.

Tip: make both points share the same x or the same y (not both). The calculator tells you if they do not.
Need a hint?

Absolute value = distance, never negative. |3 − (−5)| = |8| = 8. So (2, 3) and (2, −5) are 8 units apart because they share x = 2.

Level 2 stretch — Diagonal distance (right-triangle preview): Pick two of your places that do not share a coordinate. The legs of the right triangle are |Δx| and |Δy|. Use the grid to count them, then estimate the diagonal with Pythagoras.
Quick check: What is the distance between (2, 3) and (2, 9)?

Level 1 worked step: both points share x = 2, so distance = |9 − 3| = |6| = 6.

💡 Guided Steps & Help

Finding distance with absolute value:

  1. Distance is always positive — it is how far apart two points are, never a negative number.
  2. If the points sit on the same horizontal line, subtract the x-values. If they share the same vertical line, subtract the y-values.
  3. Take the absolute value of that difference: |a − b|. The bars mean "distance from 0," so the answer is never negative.
  4. Write your answer with units (for example, blocks or feet).
💡 Example: From (2, 3) to (2, 9): |9 − 3| = |6| = 6 units apart.
