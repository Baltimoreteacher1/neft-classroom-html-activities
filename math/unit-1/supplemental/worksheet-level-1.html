Word Bank

🔢Whole number — a counting number with no fraction or decimal (0, 5, 27).
📍Place value — what a digit is worth based on its spot (in 5,243 the 5 means 5,000).
➕Sum — the answer when you add.
✖️Product — the answer when you multiply.
➗Quotient — the answer when you divide.
🎯Estimate — a close, rounded guess (not exact).

Worked Example

Add 143 + 278

Step 1: Line up by place value (ones under ones).
Step 2: Add the ones: 3 + 8 = 11. Write 1, carry the 1.
Step 3: Add the tens: 4 + 7 + 1 = 12. Write 2, carry the 1.
Step 4: Add the hundreds: 1 + 2 + 1 = 4.
Answer: 421. Check: 140 + 280 = 420 (close, so it makes sense).

Guided Practice

Directions: Solve each problem. Use the work space and the hint cue if you need help. Show all your steps.
  1. 24 + 35 = ______
    Hint: add the ones (4 + 5), then the tens (20 + 30).
  2. 143 + 278 = ______
    Hint: line up the digits and carry when a column is 10 or more.
  3. 500 − 237 = ______
    Hint: count up from 237 to 500.
  4. 23 × 4 = ______
    Hint: break it up — (20 × 4) + (3 × 4).
  5. 12 × 5 = ______
    Hint: (10 × 5) + (2 × 5).
  6. 84 ÷ 4 = ______
    Hint: 4 × 20 = 80, then share the leftover 4.
  7. What is the place value of the 7 in 3,714?
    Hint: count from the right — ones, tens, then…
  8. Round 4,672 to the nearest hundred. ______
    Hint: look at the tens digit. Is it 5 or more?
  9. Estimate 48 × 21 by rounding each number first. ______
    Hint: round 48 to 50 and 21 to 20.
  10. Write 2,056 in expanded form. ______
    Hint: write each digit times its place value, then add.
