Factors, GCF, and Prime Numbers

GCF and LCM are foundational properties of integers. Review these prime tree guidelines and Venn diagram overlaps before starting your review checkpoints.

📈 Prime vs. Composite

A prime number has exactly two factors: 1 and itself (e.g. 2, 3, 5, 7). A composite number has more than two factors (e.g. 4, 6, 8, 9).

Números Primos: Tienen solo dos factores (1 y sí mismo). Compuestos tienen más.
Primes: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13...

⚖️ Greatest Common Factor

The GCF is the largest number that divides evenly into two or more integers. Find it by identifying overlapping prime factors in a Venn diagram.

Máximo Común Divisor (MCD): El número mayor que divide exactamente a dos o más números.
GCF = product of common primes

📊 Least Common Multiple

The LCM is the smallest multiple shared by two integers. Compute it by multiplying the unique outer prime factors by the common inner ones.

Mínimo Común Múltiplo (mcm): El múltiplo más pequeño que comparten dos números.
LCM = GCF × unique outer primes
