Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

🎁GCF — Greatest Common Factor

The biggest number that divides two numbers evenly.

MCD — Máximo Común Divisor: el número más grande que divide a dos números sin sobrar.

⏱️LCM — Least Common Multiple

The smallest number that two numbers both divide into.

mcm — mínimo común múltiplo: el número más pequeño en el que caben ambos.

💵Decimal Operations

Add or subtract money by lining up the decimal points.

Operaciones con decimales: suma o resta alineando el punto decimal.

➗Multi-Digit Division

Sharing a big number into equal groups.

División de varios dígitos: repartir un número grande en partes iguales.

Formula Bank

GCF(a, b): biggest shared factor → number of equal groups

LCM(a, b): first shared multiple → when cycles line up

Budget left: budget − total spent

Cost per guest: total cost ÷ number of guests

Distributive (6.NS.4): a + b = GCF × (a÷GCF + b÷GCF)

Sentence Frames

"The GCF of ___ and ___ is ___, so I can make ___ equal bags."

"My cost per guest is $___ because total ÷ guests = ___."

"My number is higher / lower than ___ because ___."

"El MCD de ___ y ___ es ___, así que puedo hacer ___ bolsas iguales."

🎁 Build identical goodie bags (GCF)

Level 1 tip: try amounts that share a factor, like 24 and 36.

Level 2 extension (Distributive Property · 6.NS.4): Rewrite your two supply totals as GCF × (a + b). Example: 48 + 36 = 12 × (4 + 3). Write yours below.
Need a hint?

List the factors of each number, or use the Euclid method. The GCF is the biggest factor they share. Example: GCF(48, 36) = 12, so 12 bags with 4 stickers and 3 bars each.

⏱️ Sync the entertainment (LCM)

Need a hint?

List the multiples of each until they share one. Multiples of 6: 6, 12, 18, 24… Multiples of 8: 8, 16, 24… They meet at 24 minutes.

💵 Track your budget (decimal operations)

Need a hint?

Line up the decimal points and add the four costs. Then subtract from your budget. Over budget? Trim a category.

➗ Fair price per guest (division)

Quick division check: A pack of 144 cups is shared equally among 6 tables. How many cups per table?

Level 1 worked step: 144 ÷ 6 → think "6 × 20 = 120, then 24 left, 6 × 4 = 24" → 24.
