Warm-Up 1
What does percent mean?
Vocabulary: The word percent comes from the Latin words meaning "per" (for each) and "cent" (hundred). So percent literally means per 100.
✓Correct! Percent means "per 100." For example, 25% means 25 out of every 100.
✗Not quite. "Percent" literally means "per hundred." The answer is B: Per 100.
Warm-Up 2
A 100-grid has 40 squares shaded. What percent of the grid is shaded?
Hint: A 100-grid has exactly 100 squares. Each shaded square represents 1%. Count the shaded squares to find the percent.
✓Correct! 40 out of 100 squares are shaded, so 40% of the grid is shaded.
✗Each square in a 100-grid equals 1%. If 40 squares are shaded, that is 40% (answer C).

Write 75% as a fraction with a denominator of 100.
Remember: Percent means out of 100. So 75% = 75 out of 100. Write it as a fraction: ___ / 100.

Explain your reasoning:

Sentence frame: "75% means ___ out of 100, so the fraction is ___/100."

✓Correct! 75% = 75/100. The percent tells you the numerator when the denominator is 100.
✗Since percent means "per 100," 75% is simply 75/100.
Practice 2
A store has 100 apples. If 15% of the apples are green, how many green apples are there?
Strategy: 15% means 15 out of every 100. Since there are exactly 100 apples, 15% of 100 = 15.
Show your work:

Sentence frame: "15% means ___ per 100. Since there are 100 apples, there are ___ green apples."

✓Correct! 15% of 100 = 15 green apples.
✗15% means 15 per 100. With exactly 100 apples, there are 15 green apples (answer B).

Practice 5
Marcus says that 110% is impossible because you can never have more than 100%. Is he correct?
Think about it: Can you shade more than one full 100-grid? If you shade all 100 squares and then 10 more squares on a second grid, that is 110 out of 100, or 110%.
Explain Marcus's error:

Sentence frame: "Marcus is incorrect because percents ___ than 100% are possible. 110% means ___ out of 100, which is more than ___."

✓Correct! Percents greater than 100% are possible. 110% means 110 out of 100, or more than one whole.
✗Marcus is wrong. Percents can be greater than 100%. 110% means 110 per 100 -- more than one whole. The answer is B.

Challenge
A school survey shows that 63 out of 100 students prefer pizza for lunch. Another survey of 200 students at a different school finds that 130 prefer pizza. Which school has a higher percent of pizza fans? Explain your reasoning.
Strategy: For the first school, 63 out of 100 = 63%. For the second school, find the rate per 100: 130 out of 200. To convert to percent, think: 200 is 2 groups of 100, so divide 130 by 2 to find how many per 100.
Explain how you compared the percents:

Sentence frame: "The first school has ___%. The second school has 130 out of 200 = ___%. Since ___% is greater, the ___ school has more pizza fans."

✓Correct! 130 / 200 = 65%, which is greater than 63%. The second school has the higher percent of pizza fans.
✗Convert 130/200 to a percent: 130 ÷ 2 = 65 per 100, so 65%. Since 65% > 63%, the second school has more pizza fans (answer B).
