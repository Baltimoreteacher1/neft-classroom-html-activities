Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

⚖️Unit Rate

A rate for ONE — like dollars per 1 ounce or miles per 1 hour.

Tasa unitaria: una tasa para UNO — dólares por 1 onza o millas por 1 hora.

🏷️Unit Price

Price for one unit: total price ÷ number of units. Lower wins.

Precio unitario: precio total ÷ número de unidades. Gana el más bajo.

💲Percent of a Number

A part out of 100. Find it with: number × (percent ÷ 100).

Porcentaje de un número: una parte de 100. Número × (porcentaje ÷ 100).

🔻Discount

The amount you SUBTRACT from a price during a sale.

Descuento: la cantidad que RESTAS del precio en una oferta.

🍽️Tip

A percent of the bill you give the server for service.

Propina: un porcentaje de la cuenta que das al mesero por el servicio.

🔁Conversion Factor

A rate written as a fraction used to change units.

Factor de conversión: una tasa escrita como fracción para cambiar unidades.

Formula Bank

Unit price: total price ÷ number of units

Better buy: the package with the LOWER unit price

Tip amount: bill × (tip% ÷ 100) · Total: bill + tip

Convert: value × (to-unit / from-unit) — units cancel

Stacked deal: price × (1 − disc%) , then compare unit prices

Percent decrease: (old − new) ÷ old × 100

🔁 Convert a real product-label measurement

Read a measurement off a real label (a snack's net weight, a drink's volume, a roll's length) and convert it using a rate.

Level 1: stick to length units (feet, yards, inches, miles).

Need a hint?

Multiply by the conversion factor written as a fraction. 5 feet × (12 inches / 1 foot) = 60 inches. The "feet" units cancel, leaving inches — that is rate reasoning.

Level 2 — promo stacking & percent decrease: Package B is on sale for a percent off. Apply the discount THEN recompute its unit price to see if the sale changes the better buy.
