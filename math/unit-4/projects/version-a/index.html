Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

💲Percent of a Number

A part out of 100. Find it with: number × (percent ÷ 100).

Porcentaje de un número: una parte de 100. Multiplica número × (porcentaje ÷ 100).

📈Markup

The amount you ADD to your cost to make a profit.

Margen / recargo: la cantidad que SUMAS al costo para ganar dinero.

🏷️Discount

The amount you SUBTRACT from a price during a sale.

Descuento: la cantidad que RESTAS del precio en una oferta.

🧾Sales Tax

Extra money the state adds at checkout, as a percent.

Impuesto de venta: dinero extra que el estado agrega al pagar, como porcentaje.

🔁Conversion Factor

A rate written as a fraction used to change units.

Factor de conversión: una tasa escrita como fracción para cambiar unidades.

⚖️Unit Rate

A rate for ONE — like miles per 1 hour or dollars per 1 item.

Tasa unitaria: una tasa para UNO — millas por 1 hora o dólares por 1 artículo.

Formula Bank

Percent of a number: n × (p ÷ 100)

Markup amount: cost × (markup% ÷ 100) · Selling price: cost + markup

Discount amount: price × (disc% ÷ 100) · Sale price: price − discount

Sales tax: price × (tax% ÷ 100) · Total: price + tax

Stacked promo: ((price × (1 − disc%)) × (1 + tax%))

Break-even markup %: at sale, sale price = cost

Sentence Frames

"My cost is $___, and a ___% markup gives a selling price of $___."

"A ___% discount saves $___, so the sale price is $___."

"After ___% sales tax the customer pays $___."

"My final price is higher / lower than ___ because ___."

"Mi costo es $___ y un margen de ___% da un precio de venta de $___."

📈 Set your selling price (markup)

Level 1 tip: a friendly markup is 25%. Markup amount = cost × 0.25.

Need a hint?

Markup amount = cost × (markup ÷ 100). Example: $40.00 × (25 ÷ 100) = $10.00. Selling price = $40.00 + $10.00 = $50.00.

Self-check: Type the selling price your numbers give. The tool checks it against the math.

💡 Guided Steps & Help

Calculating Percentages, Taxes, and Discounts:

  1. Convert the percentage to a decimal: Divide the percent by 100 (move the decimal point 2 places left, e.g., $6.5\% = 0.065$).
  2. Calculate tax or tip amount: Multiply the original price by the decimal value.
  3. Find the total cost: Add the tax or tip amount to the original price.
  4. For discounts: Multiply by the discount decimal, then subtract it from the original price.
💡 Example: $20.00 shirt with 8% sales tax. Tax = $20 \times 0.08 = $1.60. Total = $21.60.

🔁 Convert a real product-label measurement

Read a measurement off a real product you would sell (a candle's weight, a drink's volume, a ribbon's length) and convert it using a rate.

Level 1: stick to length units (feet, yards, inches, miles).

Need a hint?

Multiply by the conversion factor written as a fraction. 5 feet × (12 inches / 1 foot) = 60 inches. The "feet" units cancel, leaving inches — that is rate reasoning.
