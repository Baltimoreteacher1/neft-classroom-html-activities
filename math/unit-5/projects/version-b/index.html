Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

📏Base (b)

One full side of a shape that you measure along.

Base: un lado completo de la figura que mides.

📐Height (h) — perpendicular!

The straight-up distance from the base, making a right angle — NOT the slanted side.

Altura: la distancia recta (perpendicular) desde la base, no el lado inclinado.

▱Parallelogram

A 4-sided shape with both pairs of sides parallel. Area = b × h.

Paralelogramo: figura de 4 lados con lados paralelos. Área = b × h.

🔺Triangle

Exactly half a parallelogram. Area = ½ × b × h.

Triángulo: la mitad de un paralelogramo. Área = ½ × b × h.

⏢Trapezoid

A 4-sided shape with two parallel bases (b₁, b₂). Area = ½(b₁+b₂)h.

Trapecio: figura con dos bases paralelas. Área = ½(b₁+b₂)h.

🧩Composite figure

A shape made of simpler shapes. Decompose, find each, then add.

Figura compuesta: hecha de figuras simples. Descompón y suma.

🟦Square unit

How area is measured — like square feet (sq ft).

Unidad cuadrada: cómo se mide el área, como pies cuadrados.

Formula Bank

Parallelogram: Area = b × h

Triangle: Area = ½ × b × h

Trapezoid: Area = ½ × (b₁ + b₂) × h

Paint cans: ⌈ Area ÷ coverage ⌉ (always round UP)

Missing dimension: h = Area ÷ b  |  b₂ = (2·Area ÷ h) − b₁

Sentence Frames

"The area of my ___ is ___ sq ft because I used Area = ___."

"I need ___ cans of paint because ___ ÷ ___ rounds UP to ___."

"My plot is bigger / smaller than ___ by ___ sq ft."

"El área de mi ___ es ___ pies² porque usé Área = ___."

Measure & Calculate Your Spaces

Measure real spaces at your school in feet: a garden plot, a classroom wall for the mural, and a banner. No tape measure? Click Use example numbers to model the math, or count floor / wall tiles and use one tile as one unit.

Equity fallback — estimate in tiles: if you can't measure in feet, count square tiles along each side and use those numbers. Your "sq ft" then means "square tiles."

⏢ Garden plot (trapezoid)

Area = ½ × (b₁ + b₂) × h

Level 1 tip: use whole numbers, like b₁ = 8, b₂ = 12, h = 6.

Need a hint?

Add the two bases, multiply by height, then divide by 2. Example: b₁ = 8, b₂ = 12, h = 6 → ½ × (8 + 12) × 6 = ½ × 20 × 6 = 60 sq ft.

🔺 Mural panel (triangle) & paint cans

Area = ½ × b × h | Cans = ⌈ Area ÷ coverage ⌉ (round UP)

You always round up cans — you can't buy a fraction of a can.

Need a hint?

Step 1: Area = ½ × b × h. Step 2: Cans = Area ÷ coverage. Step 3: round UP (ceiling). Even 2.1 cans means buying 3. Example: 70 ÷ 50 = 1.4 → 2 cans.

▱ School banner (parallelogram)

Area = b × h
Need a hint?

Use b × h with the perpendicular height, not the slant side. Example: b = 9, h = 4 → Area = 36 sq ft.
