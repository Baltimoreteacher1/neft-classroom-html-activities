Warm-Up 1
A bag has 4 red marbles and 6 blue marbles. What is the ratio of red marbles to blue marbles?
Vocabulary: A ratio compares two quantities. A part-to-part ratio compares one part of a group to another part. You can write it as 4:6, 4 to 6, or 4/6.
✓Correct! The ratio of red to blue is 4 to 6. Order matters in a ratio — red was listed first.
✗Not quite. The ratio of red to blue compares red first, then blue: 4 to 6. The answer is B.
Warm-Up 2
There are 3 cats and 5 dogs at a pet store. Which shows the ratio of cats to total animals?
Hint: A part-to-whole ratio compares one part to the total. Total = 3 + 5 = 8.
✓Correct! 3 cats out of 8 total animals gives the part-to-whole ratio 3:8.
✗This is a part-to-whole ratio. Cats = 3, total = 3 + 5 = 8. The answer is C: 3:8.

Practice 1
A fruit bowl has 5 apples and 3 oranges. Which ratio notation shows apples to oranges?
Remember: Ratios can be written three ways: using a colon (5:3), the word "to" (5 to 3), or as a fraction (5/3).
Explain how you know:

Sentence frame: "The ratio of apples to oranges is ___ to ___ because there are ___ apples and ___ oranges."

✓Correct! Apples to oranges = 5:3. Order matters — the first quantity mentioned comes first.
✗Watch the order: apples (5) comes first, oranges (3) comes second. The answer is B: 5:3.
Practice 2
A classroom has 12 boys and 16 girls. What is the ratio of boys to girls in simplest form?
Strategy: Write the ratio 12:16, then divide both numbers by their greatest common factor (GCF). The GCF of 12 and 16 is 4.
Show your simplification work:

Sentence frame: "I divided both ___ and ___ by ___ to get the simplest form ___:___."

✓Correct! 12 ÷ 4 = 3 and 16 ÷ 4 = 4, so the simplest form is 3:4.
✗Divide both by the GCF of 4: 12 ÷ 4 = 3, 16 ÷ 4 = 4. The answer is C: 3:4.

Practice 5
A recipe calls for 2 cups of flour for every 3 cups of sugar. Jayden says the ratio of sugar to flour is 2:3. Is he correct?
Think carefully: The recipe says flour to sugar is 2:3. Jayden is writing sugar to flour. Does the order change?
Explain Jayden's error:

Sentence frame: "Jayden is incorrect because the ratio of sugar to flour is ___:___, not ___:___. Order matters in a ratio."

✓Correct! Sugar to flour = 3:2, not 2:3. Order matters in a ratio — Jayden reversed the quantities.
✗Order matters! Sugar = 3, flour = 2. Sugar to flour = 3:2. The answer is B.
