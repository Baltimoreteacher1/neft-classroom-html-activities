Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

⚖️Ratio

A compare of two amounts, like 6 cups fruit to 4 cups yogurt (6:4).

Razón: una comparación de dos cantidades, como 6 tazas de fruta por 4 de yogur (6:4).

🟰Equivalent Ratio

The same ratio made bigger or smaller by × the same number. 6:4 = 12:8.

Razón equivalente: la misma razón al multiplicar ambas partes por el mismo número. 6:4 = 12:8.

1️⃣Unit Rate

How much for just ONE: total ÷ quantity, like dollars per 1 cup.

Tasa unitaria: cuánto por UNO solo: total ÷ cantidad, como dólares por 1 taza.

🛒Better Buy

The pack with the LOWER price per cup (lower unit price wins).

Mejor compra: el paquete con MENOR precio por taza (gana el menor precio unitario).

Formula Bank

Ratio: a : b (fruit : yogurt)

Equivalent ratio: multiply both parts by the same factor

Unit rate: total ÷ quantity

Better buy: price ÷ number of cups → smaller is better

Rate as data (preview): mean of the per-cup prices = (sum of prices) ÷ (how many)

"My unit price is higher / lower than ___ because ___."

⚖️ Write your recipe ratio (6.RP.1)

Level 1 tip: pick amounts that share a factor, like 4 and 6, so you can see simplifying work. The fruit box is pre-filled to get you started.

Need a hint?

A ratio compares two quantities. 6 cups of fruit and 4 cups of yogurt can be written 6:4, 6 to 4, or 6/4. To simplify, divide both by their GCF. GCF(6, 4) = 2, so 6:4 simplifies to 3:2.

Part 1d · Self — your real plan

🛒 Smart shopping — better buy (6.RP.3)

Compare two packs of the same ingredient. Which costs less per cup?

Need a hint?

Unit rate has a denominator of 1. Cost per smoothie = total ÷ smoothies ($9.00 ÷ 3 = $3.00). Better buy = price ÷ cups for each pack: $4.50 ÷ 3 = $1.50/cup vs $7.20 ÷ 6 = $1.20/cup. Lower unit price wins.

Quick ratio check: Simplify the ratio 12:18. Write it as A:B.

Level 1 worked step: GCF(12, 18) = 6 → 12 ÷ 6 = 2 and 18 ÷ 6 = 3 → 2:3.

💡 Guided Steps & Help

Working with Ratios, Rates, & Tape Diagrams:

  1. Identify the parts in your ratio: **Part A : Part B** (e.g. 2 cups blue paint to 3 cups yellow paint).
  2. Add the parts to find the **Total Parts** in the whole mixture ($2 + 3 = 5$ parts).
  3. Divide the total mixture volume by the total parts to find the **Value of One Box** in your tape diagram.
  4. Multiply the value of one box by the parts of each ingredient to find the target amounts.
💡 Example: Ratio 2:3 (Total parts = 5). For 15 gallons total, each part is $15 ÷ 5 = 3$ gallons. Blue = $2 \times 3 = 6$ gallons, Yellow = $3 \times 3 = 9$ gallons.
