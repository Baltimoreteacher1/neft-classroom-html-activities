Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

⚖️Ratio

A compare of two amounts, like 84 points to 12 games (84:12).

Razón: una comparación de dos cantidades, como 84 puntos por 12 juegos (84:12).

🟰Equivalent Ratio

The same ratio made bigger or smaller by × the same number. 84:12 = 420:60.

Razón equivalente: la misma razón al multiplicar ambas partes por el mismo número.

1️⃣Unit Rate

How much for just ONE: total ÷ quantity, like points per 1 game.

Tasa unitaria: cuánto por UNO solo: total ÷ cantidad, como puntos por 1 juego.

🛒Better Buy / Better Rate

Comparing rates fairly: a higher points-per-game is the stronger scorer.

Comparar tasas de forma justa: más puntos por juego = mejor anotador.

Formula Bank

Ratio: a : b (points : games)

Equivalent ratio: multiply both parts by the same factor

Unit rate: total ÷ quantity (points ÷ games)

Compare fairly: scale both to the same games, then compare points

Rate as data (preview): mean ppg = (sum of player rates) ÷ (how many)

1️⃣ Scoring unit rate (6.RP.2)

Level 1 tip: try numbers that divide evenly, like 84 points in 12 games. The points box is pre-filled to get you started.

Need a hint?

Unit rate = total ÷ number of games. 84 points in 12 games is 84 ÷ 12 = 7 points per game. The ratio 84:12 can also be written 7:1 — that is the unit rate.

🟰 Project the season — ratio table (6.RP.3)

Use your athlete's unit rate to project points at 10, 20, and 30 games. All rows are equivalent ratios — the rate stays constant.

Need a hint?

At 7 points per game: 10 games → 70 pts, 20 games → 140 pts, 30 games → 210 pts. Each row multiplies the unit rate by the number of games.

Quick rate check: A player scores 20 points in 5 games. What is the unit rate (points per game)?

Level 1 worked step: 20 ÷ 5 = 4 points per game.
