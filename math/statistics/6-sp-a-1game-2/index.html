What Counts?

  • A statistical question expects answers that vary across a group — the plot spreads.
  • A non-statistical question has one fixed answer — every poller says the same thing, so the plot is a single tall bar.
  • Poll a few times: real variability shows up as scatter, not luck.
  • To fix a flat question, point it at a group instead of one person or one object, then re-poll.
