Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

⚖️Mean (average)

Add all the values, then divide by how many there are.

Media (promedio): suma todos los valores y divide entre cuántos hay.

🎯Median

Put the numbers in order; the middle value is the median.

Mediana: ordena los números; el valor del medio es la mediana.

🔁Mode

The value that appears most often.

Moda: el valor que aparece más veces.

📏Range

How spread out the data is: largest − smallest.

Rango: qué tan disperso está: mayor − menor.

📐MAD

Mean Absolute Deviation: the average distance each value is from the mean.

DAM (desviación absoluta media): la distancia promedio de cada valor a la media.

📈Distribution shape

The overall pattern: symmetric, skewed left/right, or has outliers.

Forma de la distribución: el patrón general: simétrica, sesgada o con valores atípicos.

Formula Bank

Mean = sum of values ÷ count

Median = order the data → middle value

MAD = average of |value − mean|

Range = maximum − minimum

Media = suma ÷ cantidad · Mediana = ordenar → medio · DAM = promedio de |valor − media| · Rango = máximo − mínimo

Need a hint?

Mean: Add all values, divide by count. Sensitive to outliers.
Median: Sort values; take the middle (or average two middles). Resistant to outliers.
If mean > median, the data may be skewed right. If mean < median, it may be skewed left. If they are close, the data is roughly symmetric.

Measures of Variability · 6.SP.5c

Range and MAD — How Spread Out Is the Data?

Compute the range and mean absolute deviation (MAD) to describe how consistent or spread out your data is. A small MAD means the data clusters near the mean.

Quick check: The data set is 4, 8, 6, 10, 2. What is the mean?
Need a hint?

Range = Maximum − Minimum.
MAD steps: (1) Find the mean. (2) Subtract the mean from each value, then take the absolute value. (3) Average all those absolute deviations.
Quick-check mean = (4 + 8 + 6 + 10 + 2) ÷ 5. Add them all up first!

Data Display & Best Measure · 6.SP.4 · 6.SP.2

Choose the Best Display and Measure of Center

Based on your data and its shape, choose the best type of display and the best measure of center. Justify your choices.

First, draw a dot plot of your dataset so you can see the shape before you decide.

Level 1 tip: dot plots are best for small data sets like these (fewer than ~20 values). If the mean and median are close, the mean is a fine measure of center.

Level 2 — Compare two distributions: Choose a second topic, draw its dot plot, and compare the two distributions' shape and spread in writing.
Quick check: A data set has a mean of 14 and a median of 8. A student says the mean is the best measure of center. Should you use the mean or the median, and why?
Need a hint?

Display guide: Use a dot plot for small data sets (fewer than ~20 values). Use a histogram for large data sets grouped into intervals. Use a box plot to show the five-number summary and compare two groups.
Measure of center: If the mean and median are far apart, an outlier or skew is pulling the mean — in that case, the median is more representative. If they are close, the mean is fine.
Quick-check: When mean (14) and median (8) are far apart, the mean is pulled by high outliers. The median (8) is more resistant and better represents the typical value.
