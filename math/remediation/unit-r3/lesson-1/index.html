Decimal Place Value, Compare & Round

Read decimals using tenths, hundredths, and thousandths; compare them; and round them. The foundation for all decimal math.

Why this matters for Grade 6: In Grade 6 you add, subtract, multiply, and divide multi-digit decimals (6.NS.3). All of that depends on understanding place value to the right of the decimal point — knowing that the 5 in 2.53 means 5 tenths, not 5 ones.

Learn It

Decimal Place Value

The decimal point separates the whole number from the part that is less than one. Read the number 3.647:

Ones.TenthsHundredthsThousandths
3.647
36/104/1007/1000

Key idea: adding a zero at the end of a decimal does not change its value. 0.7 = 0.70 — both are 7 tenths.

Compare 0.5 and 0.45. Line up the decimal points and compare place by place:
0.50 vs 0.45 → tenths: 5 tenths vs 4 tenths → 5 > 4, so 0.5 > 0.45.
Round to the nearest tenth: look at the hundredths digit.
Round 3.46 → hundredths digit is 6 (≥5), so round up → 3.5.
Round 2.32 → hundredths digit is 2 (<5), so round down → 2.3.

Try It · Guided

Work These Together

1. What is the value of the digit 4 in 2.47?

Hint

The first digit after the decimal point is in the tenths place. In 2.47 the digit 4 is immediately after the decimal point, so it is 4 tenths.

2. Which is greater: 0.5 or 0.45?

Hint

Write 0.5 as 0.50. Now compare tenths: 5 tenths vs 4 tenths. 5 > 4, so 0.50 > 0.45.

Practice · On Your Own

You Try

3. Round 3.46 to the nearest tenth.

Hint

Look at the hundredths digit: 6. That is ≥ 5, so round the tenths digit up. 3.46 → 3.5.

4. Which number is the smallest?

Hint

Compare tenths first. 0.09 has 0 tenths — that is less than all the others which have at least 3 tenths.

5. Round 6.183 to the nearest hundredth.

Hint

Look at the thousandths digit: 3. That is < 5, so round down. 6.183 → 6.18.
