Multiply Decimals

Multiply like whole numbers, then count and place the decimal point. Great for finding the total cost of multiple items.

Learn It

Count Decimal Places

The key rule for multiplying decimals:

  1. Ignore the decimal points and multiply the numbers as whole numbers.
  2. Count the total number of decimal places in both factors combined.
  3. Place the decimal point in the product so it has that many decimal places.
Example 1: 0.5 × 0.5
Whole-number multiply: 5 × 5 = 25.
Decimal places: 0.5 has 1, and 0.5 has 1 → total = 2 places.
Place the decimal: 25 → 0.25
Example 2: 1.2 × 3
Whole-number multiply: 12 × 3 = 36.
Decimal places: 1.2 has 1, and 3 has 0 → total = 1 place.
Place the decimal: 36 → 3.6
Example 3: 2.5 × 4
Whole-number multiply: 25 × 4 = 100.
Decimal places: 2.5 has 1, and 4 has 0 → total = 1 place.
Place the decimal: 100 → 10.0 = 10
