Place Value, Compare & Round

Read big numbers by their place value, compare them, and round them. This is the foundation everything else in math sits on.

Why this matters for Grade 6: In Grade 6 you divide multi-digit numbers and work with decimals (6.NS.2, 6.NS.3). None of that works without place value — knowing that the 7 in 4,700 means 7 hundreds, not just "7."

Learn It

Place Value

Every digit has a place, and the place tells you its value. Read the number 3,486:

ThousandsHundredsTensOnes
3486
3,000400806
Compare two numbers by lining up place values and checking the biggest place first.
4,821 vs 4,699 → same thousands (4), but 8 hundreds > 6 hundreds, so 4,821 > 4,699.
Round to a place: look at the digit just to the right. 5 or more → round up; 4 or less → round down.
Round 3,486 to the nearest hundred: the tens digit is 8 (≥5), so 3,486 → 3,500.

Try It · Guided

Work These Together

1. What is the value of the 7 in 5,792?

Hint

The 7 is in the hundreds place, so its value is 7 hundreds = 700.

2. Which symbol makes it true?   8,304 ___ 8,340

Hint

Thousands and hundreds match. Compare the tens: 0 tens vs 4 tens. 8,304 has fewer, so 8,304 < 8,340.

Practice · On Your Own

You Try

3. Round 2,648 to the nearest hundred. (Type the number, no comma needed.)

Hint

The tens digit is 4 (less than 5), so round down: 2,648 → 2,600.

4. Round 7,159 to the nearest thousand.

Hint

The hundreds digit is 1 (less than 5), so round down to 7,000.

5. Which number is the largest?

Hint

All start with 9 thousands. Compare hundreds: 9,809 has 8 hundreds — the most.
