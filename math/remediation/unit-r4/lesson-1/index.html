Factors & Divisibility Rules

A factor of a whole number divides into it evenly (no remainder). To find all factors, try every whole number starting at 1 and pair it up.

Example 1 — All factors of 12:

1 × 12 = 12  →  factors 1 and 12
2 × 6 = 12  →  factors 2 and 6
3 × 4 = 12  →  factors 3 and 4
5 × ? — 12 ÷ 5 = 2.4, not a whole number, so 5 is NOT a factor.

Factors of 12 (in order): 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
Example 2 — Is 7 a factor of 21?

Divide: 21 ÷ 7 = 3 exactly (no remainder).
Because 7 × 3 = 21, yes — 7 is a factor of 21.

Check: is 5 a factor of 21?   21 ÷ 5 = 4.2 — not a whole number, so no.

Try It · Guided

Work These Together

1. How many factors does 12 have?

Hint

Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12. Count them: that is 6 factors.

2. Is 42 divisible by 2?

Hint

The divisibility rule for 2: the last digit must be even. The last digit of 42 is 2, which is even, so yes.

3. What is the largest factor of 12 that is less than 12?

Hint

Factors of 12 in order: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12. The largest one below 12 is 6.

4. Which number is NOT divisible by 5?

Hint

Divisible by 5 means the last digit is 0 or 5. Check each: 35 (last digit 5 ✔), 50 (last digit 0 ✔), 42 (last digit 2 ✘), 100 (last digit 0 ✔).

5. Is 7 a factor of 28? Type 1 for yes, 0 for no.

Hint

28 ÷ 7 = 4 exactly (no remainder), so yes, 7 is a factor of 28. Type 1.
