Introduction to Ratios

Compare two quantities with a ratio, understand part-to-part vs part-to-whole, and find equivalent ratios.

Why this matters for Grade 6: In Grade 6 you write and describe ratios to compare quantities (6.RP.1) and use ratio reasoning to solve real-world problems (6.RP.3). Understanding what a ratio means — and how to write equivalent ratios — is the foundation for all of that work.
1
Learn It

What Is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two quantities. You can write it three ways:

3 red counters and 2 blue counters

RRR    BB
Red to blue: 3 to 2  |  3:2  |  3/2
This is a part-to-part ratio — it compares one part (red) to another part (blue).

Part-to-whole ratios compare one part to the total.

Same counters: 3 red out of 5 total → red to total = 3:5
Blue to total = 2:5

Equivalent ratios name the same comparison — just scaled up or down (like equivalent fractions).

Multiply both by 2Multiply both by 3Multiply both by 4
1:21:21:2
2:43:64:8
All of these — 1:2, 2:4, 3:6, 4:8 — are equivalent ratios. They all describe the same relationship: 1 part for every 2 parts.

Try It · Guided

Work These Together

1. A bag has 4 green marbles and 6 yellow marbles. What is the ratio of green to yellow?

Hint

Green to yellow — list green first. There are 4 green and 6 yellow, so the ratio is 4:6.

2. Using the same bag (4 green, 6 yellow, 10 total), what is the part-to-whole ratio of yellow marbles to total marbles?

Hint

Part-to-whole: yellow (the part) to total. Yellow = 6, total = 10, so 6:10.

3
Practice · On Your Own

You Try

3. A recipe uses 2 cups of flour for every 3 cups of oats. Which ratio correctly describes flour to oats?

Hint

Flour comes first in "flour to oats." 2 cups flour : 3 cups oats = 2:3.

4. Which ratio is equivalent to 2:5?

Hint

Multiply both parts of 2:5 by the same number. 2×2 = 4, 5×2 = 10, so 4:10 is equivalent to 2:5.

5. A class has 12 boys and 15 girls. What is the ratio of boys to the total number of students?

Hint

Total = 12 + 15 = 27. Boys to total = 12:27.

★
Exit Ticket

Show What You Know

6. A fruit bowl has 5 apples and 3 oranges. What is the ratio of oranges to apples?

Hint

Oranges to apples: oranges first = 3, apples = 5. So 3:5.

7. Fill in the blank: 3:4 = ___:8   (What number goes in the blank?)

Hint

4 was multiplied by 2 to get 8, so multiply 3 by 2 as well: 3 × 2 = 6.
