📐 Pre-Algebra & Geometry Readiness

Five lessons that build the ratio, rate, and geometry foundations that Grade 6 depends on — ratios and equivalent ratios, unit rates and better-buy comparisons, perimeter and area of rectangles, area of triangles and composite shapes, and the coordinate plane with an introduction to integers. Each lesson is tagged to the Grade 6 standard it unlocks.

For the Teacher

Unit R5 Teacher Notes

Prerequisite: Students should have basic multiplication and division fact fluency before starting Lesson 2 (unit rates). Lessons 3 and 4 require multiplication of whole numbers. Lesson 5 requires number-line reading skills from Unit R1.

Pacing: Each lesson is designed for a 20–30 minute independent session. All five lessons together make a solid one-week unit. The exit ticket in each lesson is a quick formative check — collect student responses before moving on.

Standards alignment: Lessons 1–2 address 6.RP.1 and 6.RP.2 (understand ratio concepts and unit rate language). Lessons 3–4 address 6.G.1 (find area of polygons by composing and decomposing). Lesson 5 bridges to 6.NS.5 and 6.NS.6 (understand positive and negative numbers, plot rational numbers on a coordinate plane).

Pairs with: the Manipulatives Toolkit for hands-on modeling and the Bilingual Vocab Deck for ESOL support.
