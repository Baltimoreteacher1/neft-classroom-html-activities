Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

🎮Independent variable (x)

The input you control. Here it is the number of weeks.

Variable independiente (x): la entrada que tú controlas. Aquí es el número de semanas.

📊Dependent variable (y)

The output that depends on x. Here it is total subscribers.

Variable dependiente (y): el resultado que depende de x. Aquí es el total de suscriptores.

⚡Constant of proportionality (k)

The fixed number you multiply x by. New subscribers each week.

Constante de proporcionalidad (k): el número fijo por el que multiplicas x. Suscriptores nuevos por semana.

📐Rate of change

How much y goes up each time x goes up by 1. It equals k.

Tasa de cambio: cuánto sube y cada vez que x sube en 1. Es igual a k.

🎯Origin (0, 0)

Where a proportional line starts. At week 0, subscribers = 0.

Origen (0, 0): donde empieza una recta proporcional. En la semana 0, suscriptores = 0.

Formula Bank

The equation: y = kx

Find y when you know x: y = k × x

Find x when you know y: x = y ÷ k

Steepness of the line: = k (bigger k → steeper)

Level 2 — with a one-time fee: y = kx + b (no longer proportional)

Design YOUR Channel

Pick a channel you would actually run and research a realistic weekly growth rate. Not sure yet? Click Use example numbers to model the math, then change them to fit your channel.

Level 1 tip: pick a friendly k like 10, 20, or 25 to keep the table easy.

✍️ Write your equation (y = kx)

Need a hint?

The independent variable x = weeks. The dependent variable y = total subscribers. If you gain the same amount every week, that constant rate is k. The equation is y = kx — multiply k times the number of weeks to get the total subscribers.

📊 Build your data table (weeks 0–5)

Remember: y = kx means you multiply your growth rate by each x value. At x = 0, y is always 0 (no weeks, no growth yet) — that is the origin.
Need a hint?

Fill in the equation for each row. If k = 12: week 0 → 12×0 = 0; week 1 → 12×1 = 12; week 2 → 12×2 = 24, and so on. The y values increase by k each time — that is what makes it proportional. Each row is an ordered pair (x, y) you can plot.

📈 Read your graph

Use your equation to find y when you know x, and to work backwards to find x when you know y.

Need a hint?

To find y: substitute x into y = kx and multiply. To find x: divide y by k (x = y ÷ k). On the graph, the line passes through the origin (0, 0) and goes up steeply if k is large.
