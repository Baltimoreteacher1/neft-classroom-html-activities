Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

📅Independent variable (x)

The input you control. Here it is the number of months.

Variable independiente (x): la entrada que controlas. Aquí es el número de meses.

💵Dependent variable (y)

The output that depends on x. Here it is total cost.

Variable dependiente (y): el resultado que depende de x. Aquí es el costo total.

⚡Constant of proportionality (k)

The fixed number you multiply x by. The monthly price.

Constante de proporcionalidad (k): el número fijo por el que multiplicas x. El precio mensual.

📐Rate of change

How much y goes up each time x goes up by 1. It equals k.

Tasa de cambio: cuánto sube y cada vez que x sube en 1. Es igual a k.

🎯Origin (0, 0)

Where a proportional line starts. At month 0, cost = $0.

Origen (0, 0): donde empieza una recta proporcional. En el mes 0, costo = $0.

Formula Bank

The equation: y = kx

Find y when you know x: y = k × x

Find x when you know y: x = y ÷ k

Steepness of the line: = k (bigger k → steeper)

Level 2 — with an activation fee: y = kx + b (no longer proportional)

Sentence Frames

"My independent variable is ___ and my dependent variable is ___."

"Our plan's k is $___ per month, so our equation is y = ___x."

"After ___ months our plan costs $___ because ___."

"Mi variable independiente es ___ y mi variable dependiente es ___. Nuestra k es $___, así que la ecuación es y = ___x."

✍️ Write your equations (y = kx)

Need a hint?

The independent variable x = months. The dependent variable y = total cost in dollars. If the monthly charge is constant, that amount is k. y = kx means total cost = monthly rate × number of months. At month 0, you have paid $0 (the origin).

📊 Build a cost table (months 1–6)

Remember: For y = kx, substitute each month number for x and multiply. The total cost grows by k every month — that constant increase is what makes it proportional.
Need a hint?

If Plan A k = 25: month 1 → 25 × 1 = $25; month 2 → 25 × 2 = $50; and so on. Each row increases by $25 because k = 25. Each row is an ordered pair (x, y) you can plot.

📈 Graph both plans on one grid

Find the total cost for either plan at a month you choose, and work backwards to find how many months reach a target cost.

Need a hint?

To find y: substitute x into y = kx and multiply. To find x: divide y by k (x = y ÷ k). Both lines start at the origin (0, 0); the plan with the bigger k (higher monthly price) is the steeper line.
