Step 1 — Vocabulary
1
Key Vocabulary
📚
Independent Variable (x)
The quantity you choose or control. It does not depend on anything else.
Ex: You choose how many items to buy → x = number of items
Dependent Variable (y)
The quantity that changes because of x. Its value depends on x.
Ex: The total cost changes based on what you chose → y = total cost
Constant of Proportionality (k)
In y = kx, k is the rate of change — how much y increases for every 1 unit of x.
Ex: If each item costs $5, then k = 5, so y = 5x
Proportional Relationship
A relationship where y = kx — the graph is a straight line through the origin.
Ex: $3 per slice → y = 3x. Doubling slices doubles cost.
