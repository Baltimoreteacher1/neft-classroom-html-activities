Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

🍞Dividend

The amount you start with and split up (your batch of dough).

Dividendo: la cantidad con la que empiezas y repartes.

🔪Divisor

The size of each share you cut (the serving size).

Divisor: el tamaño de cada parte que cortas.

🧁Quotient

The answer — how many servings you get.

Cociente: la respuesta — cuántas porciones obtienes.

🔄Reciprocal

A fraction flipped upside down. 1/8 becomes 8/1.

Recíproco: una fracción volteada. 1/8 se vuelve 8/1.

📈Improper Fraction

Top number bigger than bottom, like 5/2.

Fracción impropia: el numerador es mayor que el denominador, como 5/2.

🔢Mixed Number

A whole number with a fraction, like 2 1/2.

Número mixto: un entero con una fracción, como 2 1/2.

Formula Bank

÷ a fraction = × the reciprocal: (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c)

Change mixed → improper first: 2 1/2 = (2×2+1)/2 = 5/2

Smaller pieces → more pieces. Dividing by a smaller fraction gives a bigger answer.

Remainder: full servings = whole part of the quotient; leftover = remainder × serving size.

Sentence Frames

"Dividing by ___ is the same as multiplying by ___."

"My recipe makes ___ servings, which is more / fewer than ___ because ___."

"Dividí ___ entre ___, así que obtengo ___ porciones."

🍞 Portion control (fraction ÷ fraction)

Setup: You have a/b of a pan of dough. Each mini-loaf uses c/d of a pan. How many mini-loaves? Use the rule: (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a×d)/(b×c), then simplify.

Level 1 tip: try friendly pieces like 3/4 of a pan and 1/8 per loaf.

Need a hint?
To divide fractions, multiply by the reciprocal: (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c) = (a×d)/(b×c). Then find the GCF of the top and bottom to simplify.

Level 1 worked step: (3/4) ÷ (1/8) → flip the second fraction → (3/4) × (8/1) = 24/4 = 6 mini-loaves.

🧁 How many servings? (whole ÷ unit fraction)

Setup: You made a whole number of cups of frosting. Each cupcake gets 1/(d) cup. How many cupcakes? Formula: whole ÷ (1/d) = whole × d.
Need a hint?

Dividing by a unit fraction 1/d is the same as multiplying by d. How many 1/3-cup scoops fit in 4 cups? Each cup holds 3 scoops → 4 × 3 = 12.

🔢 Roll cuts (mixed number ÷ fraction)

Setup: A dough roll is whole + n/d feet long. Each piece is c/d of a foot. How many pieces? Step 1: mixed → improper fraction. Step 2: divide fraction by fraction.

Need a hint?

Convert the mixed number: multiply whole × denominator, add the numerator. 2 1/2 → (2×2+1)/2 = 5/2. Then divide: (5/2) ÷ (3/4) = (5/2) × (4/3) = 20/6 = 10/3 = 3 R 1/3. You get 3 full pieces with 1/3 of a piece left over.

Quick division check: A jar holds 6 cups of batter. Each muffin uses 2/3 cup. How many muffins? (Hint: 6 ÷ 2/3 = ?)

Level 1 worked step: 6 ÷ (2/3) → flip → 6 × (3/2) = 18/2 = 9.
