Warm-Up 1
What is 8.4 ÷ 2?
Vocabulary: A decimal is a number with a dot that separates whole and fractional parts. When dividing decimals, use place value to keep the decimal point in the right spot.
Hint: 8 ÷ 2 = 4, and 0.4 ÷ 2 = 0.2. Add them together.
✓Correct! 8.4 ÷ 2 = 4.2. The decimal point stays aligned in the quotient.
✗Divide step by step: 8 ÷ 2 = 4, then 4 tenths ÷ 2 = 2 tenths. The answer is B: 4.2.
Warm-Up 2
When you divide 6.3 by 0.7, the first step is to move the decimal point. What do both numbers become?
Key idea: When the divisor is a decimal, multiply both the dividend and divisor by 10 (or 100) to make the divisor a whole number.
✓Correct! Multiply both by 10: 6.3 becomes 63 and 0.7 becomes 7. Then divide: 63 ÷ 7 = 9.
✗To make the divisor a whole number, multiply both numbers by 10. 6.3 × 10 = 63, and 0.7 × 10 = 7. The answer is A.
