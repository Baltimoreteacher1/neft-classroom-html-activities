Before You Read

Key Words

expression — a math phrase with numbers, variables, and operations, like 5e or 2x + 1.
variable — a letter that stands for a number, like x or e.
exponent — a small number that says how many times to multiply. 2³ = 2 × 2 × 2.
evaluate — find the value by putting in a number for the variable.
order of operations — do ×/÷ before +/−. Exponents come first.
coefficient — the number multiplied by a variable. In 5e, the 5 is the coefficient.
Part 1 — Power-Up Scores (Exponents)

Maya programmed a power-up that doubles each level. At level 4, the score is 2⁴. "An exponent means repeated multiplication," she said.

2⁴ = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 16 points.

Check Your Reading — Q1

What does the exponent in 2⁴ tell you to do?

Solve It — #1

Find the value of 3².

Part 2 — Writing an Expression

Each enemy is worth 5 points. If a player defeats e enemies, the score is 5e (5 times e). "A variable lets one expression work for any number of enemies," Maya explained.

Expression: 5e. If e = 4, then 5e = 5 × 4 = 20 points.

Check Your Reading — Q2

In the expression 5e, what does the letter e stand for?

Solve It — #2

Evaluate 5e when e = 6 (6 enemies defeated).

Part 3 — Order of Operations

A bonus screen showed 2 + 3 × 4. A player typed 20 and got it wrong! "You must do multiplication before addition," Maya reminded them — the order of operations.

2 + 3 × 4 = 2 + 12 = 14 (not 20).

Solve It — #3

Use the order of operations: 10 − 2 × 3 = ?

Part 4 — A Bigger Power-Up

The final boss gives a mega power-up worth 2⁵ points. Maya counted the multiplications carefully.

2⁵ = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 32 points.

Solve It — #4

Find the value of 5².
