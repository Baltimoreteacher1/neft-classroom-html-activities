Saving for a Skateboard

Leo wants a skateboard. One-step equations help him figure out the unknown — how much to save, how many weeks, how much is left.

ESOL Reading + Math One-Step Equations 6.EE.7 Levels: Beginner & Intermediate
Before You Read

Key Words

equation — a math sentence with an equals sign, like x + 8 = 15.
variable — a letter for the unknown number, like x.
solve — find the value of the variable that makes the equation true.
inverse operation — the opposite operation. The inverse of + is −; the inverse of × is ÷.
balance — do the same thing to both sides to keep the equation equal.
check — put your answer back in to make sure it is true.
Part 1 — How Much More? (Addition)

Leo has saved some money. He needs $15 total. He has $8. "How much more do I need?" He wrote an equation: x + 8 = 15.

To undo "+ 8," subtract 8 from both sides: x = 15 − 8 = $7. Check: 7 + 8 = 15. ✓

Check Your Reading — Q1

To solve x + 8 = 15, what operation does Leo use?

Solve It — #1

Solve x + 6 = 14.

Part 2 — How Many Weeks? (Multiplication)

Leo saves $3 each week. He needs $21 more. "How many weeks?" His equation: 3x = 21, where x is the number of weeks.

To undo "× 3," divide both sides by 3: x = 21 ÷ 3 = 7 weeks. Check: 3 × 7 = 21. ✓

Check Your Reading — Q2

Why does Leo divide by 3 to solve 3x = 21?

Solve It — #2

Solve 4x = 24.

Part 3 — How Much Was There? (Subtraction)

Leo spent $4 on wax and had $9 left. "How much did I start with?" Equation: x − 4 = 9.

To undo "− 4," add 4 to both sides: x = 9 + 4 = $13. Check: 13 − 4 = 9. ✓

Solve It — #3

Solve x − 5 = 12.

Part 4 — Sharing Equally (Division)

Leo and friends split a bulk pack of grip tape. The equation x ÷ 2 = 8 shows each person got 8 pieces from x total, shared by 2.

To undo "÷ 2," multiply both sides by 2: x = 8 × 2 = 16 pieces. Check: 16 ÷ 2 = 8. ✓

Solve It — #4

Solve 5x = 35.
