Before you start · Vocabulary

Visual Math Notes

Learn these words first. Tap EN / ES help at the top for Spanish.

🧊Volume

The amount of space inside a 3-D shape, in cubic units.

Volumen: el espacio dentro de una figura 3-D, en unidades cúbicas.

📐Surface Area

The total area of all the outside faces, in square units.

Área de superficie: el área total de todas las caras exteriores, en unidades cuadradas.

🗺️Net

A flat pattern that folds up into a 3-D solid.

Plantilla (net): un patrón plano que se dobla para formar un sólido 3-D.

📦Rectangular Prism

A box shape with 6 rectangular faces.

Prisma rectangular: una caja con 6 caras rectangulares.

📏Edge Length

How long one side of the box is (l, w, or h).

Longitud de arista: lo que mide un lado de la caja (l, w o h).

⬜Unit Cube

A cube 1 unit on every side; volume = 1 cubic unit.

Cubo unitario: un cubo de 1 unidad por lado; volumen = 1 unidad cúbica.

Formula Bank

Volume: V = l × w × h

Surface Area (closed box): SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)

Open-top SA (no lid): SA = lw + 2lh + 2wh

Material cost: cost = SA × cost per square unit

Efficiency: material per cube = SA ÷ V (smaller is better)

Sentence Frames

"My box is ___ × ___ × ___, so its volume is ___ cubic units."

"The surface area is ___ square units, so the cardboard costs $___."

"My box uses ___ square units per cube, which is more / less than ___."

"Mi caja mide ___ × ___ × ___, así que su volumen es ___ unidades cúbicas."

🔗 Connections

Cross-topic — unit rate: "cost per square unit" is a unit rate (a price for exactly 1 square unit), just like cost per pound or miles per hour from earlier units.

Net → 3-D bridge (6.G.4 ↔ area): surface area is just the areas of rectangles from earlier grades, added up. Unfold the box into its flat net, find each rectangle's area, then fold it back.

🧊 Box volume (V = l × w × h)

Level 1 tip: round each measurement to a whole number of inches first.

Check my work: Compute the volume of your box yourself, then type it in.
Need a hint?

Multiply all three measurements. For an 8 × 5 × 3 box: 8 × 5 = 40 unit cubes on the bottom layer, then 40 × 3 = 120 cubic units total. Fractional edges work too — 2.5 × 1.5 × 3 = 11.25 cubic units.

📐 Surface area from the net — SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)

Uses the same length, width, and height you measured above.

Check my work: Compute the surface area yourself, then type it in.
Need a hint?

Find the three face pairs: top/bottom = 2(l × w), front/back = 2(l × h), sides = 2(w × h). Add them. For 8 × 5 × 3: 2(40) + 2(24) + 2(15) = 80 + 48 + 30 = 158 square units.

💵 Cardboard cost (cost = SA × rate)

Check my work: Multiply your surface area by the rate, then type the cost.
Level 2 — Minimize cost (optimization): Keep your volume about the same, but find dimensions that give the smallest surface area (and so the cheapest box). Hint: the closer a box is to a cube, the less material it needs.
