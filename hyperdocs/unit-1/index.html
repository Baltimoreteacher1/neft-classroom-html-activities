Smoothie Stand: Ratios & Unit Rates

Grade 6 Math · Standard 6.RP.A.1–3 · Engage → Explore → Explain → Apply → Reflect

1 Engage

You run a smoothie stand. A recipe uses 2 cups of mango for every 3 cups of ice.

Think: If you double the recipe, how much mango and ice do you need?

A ratio compares two amounts. Today you will use ratios and unit rates.

3 Explain

Ratio: a compare of two amounts. Example: 2 to 3, or 2:3.
Rate: a ratio that compares two different units. Example: 60 miles in 2 hours.
Unit rate: the rate for 1. Example: 30 miles per 1 hour.

Worked example. A pack has 12 cups for $4.

Unit rate (price per 1 cup) = $4 ÷ 12 cups = $0.33 per cup.

Unit rate (cups per $1) = 12 ÷ 4 = 3 cups per dollar.

To find a unit rate, divide the top number by the bottom number.
