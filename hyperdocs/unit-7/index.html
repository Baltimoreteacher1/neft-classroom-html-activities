Unit 7 — Integers & the Number Line

Dive the number line like a submarine. 0 is sea level. Going up is positive (+). Going down is negative (−).

Neft Teacher · Grade 6 Mathematics

Learning Target

  • I can describe positive and negative numbers in real-world contexts. (6.NS.C.5)
  • I can place integers on a number line and find their opposites. (6.NS.C.6)
  • I can compare and order integers and explain using absolute value. (6.NS.C.7)
📐 Standards: 6.NS.C.5 · 6.NS.C.6 · 6.NS.C.7 ⏱ Estimated time: 50 minutes 📦 Materials: pencil, this HyperDoc, optional number-line handout 👥 Format: individual or pair work

Teacher Notes (not for student display)

Pacing

  • Engage — 5 min: pose the submarine hook aloud; cold-call 2 students before they begin.
  • Explore — 10 min: 3D game + 5 min debrief on number-line placement.
  • Explain — 8 min: vocabulary card review with pair-share on absolute value.
  • Apply — 15 min (self-check) + 5 min NTKit auto-graded set.
  • Reflect — 7 min: individual writing; collect deliverable at class end.

Grouping

  • Pair students for Explore; individual for Apply and Reflect.
  • Small-group option: compare self-check answers before pressing "Check My Answers."

Differentiation — Support

  • Provide a printed number line from −20 to +20 for Q1 and Q6.
  • Allow students to draw the number line on scratch paper during Apply.
  • Reduce Apply to Q1–Q4 for students needing scaffolding.

Differentiation — Challenge

  • Ask: "If |a| = |b|, does a = b? Explain with examples."
  • Extension: place rational numbers (½, −¾) on the number line.

ESOL / Language Supports

  • Display a bilingual word wall: entero/integer, valor absoluto/absolute value, opuesto/opposite.
  • Use the submarine diagram as a non-linguistic representation throughout.
  • Accept numeric answers without written justification for emerging English learners.

Step 3 · Explain · Learn

What you need to know

Integer: a whole number that can be positive, negative, or zero. Like −3, 0, or +5.
Negative number: a number less than 0. It has a minus sign (−). Like −7.
Opposite: the same distance from 0, but the other side. The opposite of +6 is −6.
Absolute value: how far a number is from 0. We write it like |−8| = 8. It is never negative.

Worked example: Order −2, 3, −5 from least to greatest.

On a number line, numbers on the left are smaller. So: −5, −2, 3.

Absolute value: |−5| = 5 because −5 is 5 steps from 0.

Rule: the more negative a number is, the smaller it is. −5 is less than −2.
