📖 6.EE.C.9 — Writing & Analyzing Equations

Use variables to represent two quantities in a real-world problem. Write an equation to show how they relate!

⚖️

The Equation

An equation shows the relationship between the independent and dependent variable.

General form: y = kx
where k is the rate (unit rate / constant of proportionality).

⚡

The Rate (k)

The rate tells you how much the dependent variable changes for every 1 unit of the independent variable.

Find it: k = y ÷ x
It stays the same for every row in the table!

🎮

Independent Variable (x)

The input — what you control.
Goes in the first column of a table.
Plotted on the horizontal axis.

🔑 Easy trick: It's the thing that CAUSES the other thing to change!

🎯

Dependent Variable (y)

The output — the result.
Goes in the second column of a table.
Plotted on the vertical axis.

🔑 Easy trick: It DEPENDS on the independent variable!

🔑 Steps to Write an Equation from a Table:
1️⃣ Identify which column is independent (x) and which is dependent (y)
2️⃣ Find the rate: divide any y-value by its matching x-value → k = y ÷ x
3️⃣ Write the equation: y = kx

📊 Worked Examples

🚗 Example 1 — Road Trip
A car travels 60 miles for every hour driven.
Hours (x) Miles (y)
1 60
2 120
3 180
Rate: k = 60 ÷ 1 = 60  →  Check: 120 ÷ 2 = 60 ✅
y = 60x    or    miles = 60 × hours
💵 Example 2 — Babysitting
A babysitter earns $8 per hour.
Hours (x) Earnings (y)
1 8
2 16
4 32
Rate: k = 8 ÷ 1 = 8  →  Check: 16 ÷ 2 = 8 ✅
y = 8x    or    earnings = 8 × hours
⚠️ Common Mistake: The rate (k) must be the SAME for every row. If it's different, the relationship is NOT proportional and y = kx doesn't apply!
