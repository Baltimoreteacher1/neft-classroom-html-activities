Prime Station: The Factor Code

Orbital Station Kepler-9 is crippled and adrift. Every critical system is sealed behind factor-encrypted bulkheads. As the cadet on duty, only your command of prime factorization and the greatest common factor can bring the station back online — before life support fails.

Act 1 · Lesson 1-1

The Locked Bay

🔐 Encryption Cylinder — Prime Key

The cylinder needs the complete prime factorization of 72, written with exponents. Which option equals 72 and uses only prime factors? 72 = ?

🔐 Bulkhead Code — Build the Tree

Open the bay by choosing the fully reduced factor tree for 120 (every leaf must be prime).

The Self-Sealing Vault

Act 2 · Lesson 1-2

The Twin Engines

⚙️ Harmonic Sync — GCF by Factorization

Engine A draws 48 units, Engine B draws 72. Sync them by finding the GCF from their prime factorizations: 48 = 2⁴×3, 72 = 2³×3². Take the lowest power of each shared prime.

⚙️ Fair Distribution — Rationing Crates

You have 54 oxygen cells and 90 ration packs. To stock identical kits with nothing left over, how many kits can you make, and what is in each?

⭐ Captain’s Challenge

Optional — skip anytime, not required to advance.

The Relatively Prime Relay

Two relay codes are relatively prime if their GCF is 1 (they share no prime factors). Which pair is relatively prime?

🔒 Complete Act 2 to unlock the Final Code.
Final Code · Boss

The Master Door

🔒 Master Override — Combined Cipher

The reactor core demands one line that is fully correct: the prime factorization of 84 in exponent form, and the GCF of 84 and 126.

⭐ Captain’s Final Challenge

Optional — skip anytime, not required to advance.

The Reactor Tessellation

The reactor floor is 84 cm by 126 cm and must be tiled with identical square plates, as large as possible, with no gaps. What is the side length of each plate, and how many plates fit?
