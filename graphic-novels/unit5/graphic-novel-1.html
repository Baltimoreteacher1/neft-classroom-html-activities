Theme Park Engineer: Area Architect 🎢

You are Maya, a young theme park engineer. You will build a futuristic park! Each ride opens when you find the correct area. Area is the space inside a shape. Let’s build!

Eres Maya, una joven ingeniera. Vas a construir un parque. Cada juego se abre cuando encuentras el área (el espacio dentro de una figura).

Act 1 · Area of Parallelograms

The Slanted Plaza

🏗️ Build #1 — The Welcome Plaza

The entry plaza is a parallelogram. A parallelogram is a slanted box. To find its area, use A = b × h (base times height). The base is 12 m and the height is 5 m. What is the area? Área de un paralelogramo = base × altura. Base = 12, altura = 5.

Figure: Parallelogram ▱
base (b) = 12 m
height (h) = 5 m — straight up, not the slanted side
💡 Hint: Use the height (the straight-up distance, 5), NOT the slanted side. Multiply: 12 × 5.

🏗️ Build #2 — The Walkway

Now build a slanted walkway. It is also a parallelogram with base 8 m and height 4 m. Use A = b × h again. What is the area? Base = 8, altura = 4. Área = b × h.

💡 Hint: 8 × 4. Think 8, then double it twice: 8, 16, 32.
Sentence frame: “The area of a parallelogram is base × height. The plaza is ____ × ____ = ____ square meters.”
🔒 Finish Act 1 first to unlock Act 2.
Act 2 · Triangles & Trapezoids

Signs & Stages

🔥 Build #3 — The Triangle Sign

The big welcome sign is a triangle. A triangle is half of a box. Use A = ½ × b × h. The base is 10 m and the height is 6 m. What is the area? Área de un triángulo = ½ × base × altura. Base = 10, altura = 6.

Figure: Triangle △
base (b) = 10 m
height (h) = 6 m — straight up from the base
💡 Hint: First multiply 10 × 6 = 60. Then take half: 60 ÷ 2 = 30.

🎪 Build #4 — The Stage Canopy

The stage roof is a trapezoid (a shape with two flat parallel sides). Use A = ½ × (b₁ + b₂) × h. The two parallel sides are 6 m and 10 m, and the height is 4 m. What is the area? Trapecio: ½ × (lado1 + lado2) × altura. Lados = 6 y 10, altura = 4.

Figure: Trapezoid ⏢
top parallel side (b₁) = 6 m
bottom parallel side (b₂) = 10 m
height (h) = 4 m — distance between the two parallel sides
💡 Hint: First add the two flat sides: 6 + 10 = 16. Then 16 × 4 = 64. Then take half: 64 ÷ 2 = 32.
Sentence frame: “For a triangle I use ½ × base × height. For a trapezoid I add the two parallel sides first: ½ × (____ + ____) × ____ = ____.”
🔒 Finish Act 2 first to unlock the Final Build.
Final Build · Composite Figure

The Grand Pavilion

🎪 Final Build — The Pavilion

The grand pavilion is a composite figure: a rectangle base with a triangle roof on top. Find the area of EACH part, then ADD them.
Rectangle: 10 m wide × 4 m tall.
Triangle roof: base 10 m, height 6 m. Figura compuesta: suma el área del rectángulo y la del triángulo.

Figure: Composite — triangle roof on a rectangle base △□
Triangle roof: base = 10 m, height = 6 m
Rectangle base: 10 m wide, 4 m tall
Find each area, then ADD them.
💡 Hint: Rectangle = 10 × 4 = 40. Triangle = ½ × 10 × 6 = 30. Now ADD: 40 + 30.
Sentence frame: “The rectangle area is ____ and the triangle area is ____. The whole pavilion is ____ + ____ = ____ square meters.”
