Treasure Map Navigator

A storm-worn chart names a lost cache somewhere across the archipelago. As the navigator Maya, you must chart ordered pairs across all four quadrants, reflect points across the axes, and reason with integers and absolute value — reading elevation above the waves and depth below them — to triangulate the treasure before the tide turns.

Act 1 · Graph on the Coordinate Plane

Charting the Grid

📍 Cartographer’s Lock — Reflect the Marker

A landmark sits at the lighthouse, (−6, 4). The chart says its mirror beacon lies reflected across the x-axis. Where is the beacon?

📍 Survey Lock — Distance on the Chart

Two buoys lie at (−3, 2) and (5, 2). They share the same y-value, so the leg between them is horizontal. Using absolute value, how long is that leg?

⭐ Navigator’s Challenge

Optional — you can skip this anytime; it never blocks your progress.

The Double Reflection

A point starts at (4, −7). Reflect it across the y-axis, then reflect that result across the x-axis. Where does it land?

Act 2 · Integers & Absolute Value

Elevations & Depths

🏔️ Depth Gauge — Order the Stations

Five dive stations read: reef shelf −9 m, gull rock +14 m, surface buoy 0 m, wreck −23 m, and the cliff ledge +5 m. List them from deepest to highest.

🌊 Pressure Lock — Distance From the Surface

A drone hovers at +18 m above the waves; a diver works at −25 m. Sea level is 0. Who is farther from the surface, and by how much, using absolute value?

⭐ Navigator’s Challenge

Optional — skip anytime; it never blocks your progress.

The Temperature Trap

Two cave inscriptions warn of cold: chamber A at −4°C and chamber B at −11°C. Which statement is true?

Final · Navigate to the Treasure

The Cache

👑 Master Bearing — The Combined Fix

The cache marker is plotted at (−7, −3) and lies buried at −12 m relative to the surface. Choose the single line that is entirely correct: the quadrant, the reflection of the marker across the y-axis, and the burial depth as a distance.

⭐ Navigator’s Final Challenge

Optional — skip anytime; the treasure is already yours.

The Vertical Span

From a watch-point at +9 m elevation, the cache sits at −12 m. What is the total vertical distance the rope must span between them?
