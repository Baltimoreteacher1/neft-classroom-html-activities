Master Chef Kitchen: The Recipe Rescue

You are Kai, a young chef. The restaurant is very busy! The pantry is locked. You must divide fractions to portion the food and unlock the kitchen. Save dinner!

Eres Kai, un chef joven. El restaurante está muy ocupado. Debes dividir fracciones para servir la comida y abrir la despensa.

Act 1 · Lesson 2-1

The Locked Pantry

🍲 Order Ticket #1 — What does it mean?

Kai has 3 cups of soup. Each bowl gets 1/4 cup. The ticket asks: 3 ÷ 1/4. What does this question really mean? ¿Qué significa 3 ÷ 1/4?

💡 Hint: “Divide by 1/4” means “How many 1/4-cup pieces fit inside?” Think: how many quarters are in the whole amount?

🔐 Pantry Lock — Solve It

Now find the answer. How many 1/4-cup bowls can Kai fill from 3 cups of soup? Solve 3 ÷ 1/4. ¿Cuántos tazones de 1/4 de taza salen de 3 tazas?

💡 Hint: Each whole cup makes 4 quarter-cups. So 3 cups make 3 × 4. To divide by a fraction, flip it and multiply: 3 × 4/1 = 3 × 4.
Sentence frame: “3 ÷ 1/4 means: how many ____-cup bowls fit in 3 cups. Each cup makes ____ quarter-cups, so the answer is 3 × ____ = ____ bowls.”
🔒 Finish Act 1 first to unlock Act 2.
Act 2 · Lesson 2-2

The Big Order

🍳 Serving Lock #1 — Count the Servings

A big pot holds 6 cups of stew. Each plate gets a 1/3-cup serving. How many plates can Kai serve? Solve 6 ÷ 1/3. ¿Cuántos platos de 1/3 de taza salen de 6 tazas?

💡 Hint: Each cup makes 3 thirds. Flip 1/3 to get 3, then multiply: 6 × 3.

🥐 Serving Lock #2 — Fraction by Fraction

Kai has 3/4 cup of sauce. Each small dish needs 1/8 cup. How many dishes can Kai fill? Solve 3/4 ÷ 1/8. ¿Cuántos platos de 1/8 de taza salen de 3/4 de taza?

💡 Hint: To divide fractions, flip the second one and multiply: 3/4 × 8/1 = (3 × 8) / 4 = 24/4.
Sentence frame: “6 ÷ 1/3 = 6 × ____ = ____ plates. To divide 3/4 ÷ 1/8, I flip 1/8 to get ____, so 3/4 × ____ = ____ dishes.”
🔒 Finish Act 2 first to unlock the Final Dish.
Final Dish · Boss

The Banquet

🍽️ The Banquet Code — Big Order

The banquet pot holds 4 cups of curry. Each guest bowl takes 2/3 cup. How many bowls can Kai serve? Solve 4 ÷ 2/3. ¿Cuántos tazones de 2/3 de taza salen de 4 tazas?

💡 Hint: Flip 2/3 to get 3/2, then multiply: 4 × 3/2 = 12/2 = 6.
Sentence frame: “4 ÷ 2/3: I flip 2/3 to get ____, so 4 × ____ = ____ ÷ 2 = ____ bowls.”
