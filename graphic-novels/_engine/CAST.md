# Two-Voice Cast — all 10 worlds (Phase 1 proposal)

Every world has **two named voices**:

1. **Protagonist** — the existing themed role; the **reader's stand-in**. The
   answer choices are this character's possible replies.
2. **Companion/Guide** — a recurring named sidekick who moves the story and, on
   ~one beat per act, **voices the common misconception out loud** so the
   student (protagonist) has to catch it. The companion's wrong line maps to an
   existing distractor — the misconception becomes a character moment, not a
   trick answer.

Names are original and non-branded. Avatars are optional (drop-in later); until
then each speaker shows a colored name tag. The Unit 1 reference cast
(Cadet + AXIS) is locked from your brief; Units 2–10 mirror its pattern.

## How the companion maps to existing distractors (no math invented)

The companion's wrong line is always one of the **existing** answer choices from
the current source file. Examples already present on disk:

- **U1 Act 1:** distractor "9 (= 3×3)" → AXIS says _"9 is prime, right?"_ The
  correct reply "Not 9 — that's 3×3, the prime is 7" is the student catching it.
- **U1 Act 2 GCF:** distractor "6 (common, not the biggest)" → AXIS says
  _"6 divides both, that's our answer!"_ Correct reply: "6 works but 12 is the
  GREATEST common factor."

This keeps **every correct answer, distractor, standard, and Spanish line** from
the source file — we only re-voice them.
