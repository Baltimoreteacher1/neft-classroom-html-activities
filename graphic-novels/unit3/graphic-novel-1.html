Global Travel Planner

You are Maya, a young trip planner. You will travel around the world! Each city has a ratio puzzle. Solve it to unlock the next stop. Use math to plan the perfect trip!

Eres Maya, una joven planificadora de viajes. Resuelve cada acertijo de razones para desbloquear la siguiente ciudad.

Act 1 · Understand Ratios

Paris: Compare the Groups

🇫🇷 Paris Gate #1 — Write the Ratio

A ratio compares two amounts. The tour bus has 2 guides and 6 travelers. What is the ratio of guides to travelers? Una razón compara dos cantidades. ¿Cuál es la razón de guías a viajeros?

💡 Hint: Write the first amount, then the second: guides : travelers = 2 : 6.

🇫🇷 Paris Gate #2 — Same Ratio

Maya sees 1 souvenir stamp for every 3 passports. A bigger group has 4 stamps. How many passports do they have if the ratio stays the same? Hay 1 sello por cada 3 pasaportes. Si hay 4 sellos, ¿cuántos pasaportes hay?

💡 Hint: 1 stamp → 3 passports. So 4 stamps → 4 × 3 passports.
Sentence frame: “The ratio of guides to travelers is ____ to ____. For every 1 stamp there are ____ passports, so 4 stamps means ____ passports.”
🔒 Finish Act 1 first to unlock Act 2.
Act 2 · Ratio Tables & Graphs

At the money desk, $1 = 5 coins. Maya changes $6. How many coins does she get? $1 equivale a 5 monedas. ¿Cuántas monedas por $6?

Final Trip · Both Skills

The Last Gateway

🌏 Final Gateway — Ratio + Table

The last gate needs BOTH skills. The plane uses 2 bags for every 1 traveler. Use the ratio table to find the bags for 5 travelers. Pick the line that is fully correct. 2 maletas por cada 1 viajero. ¿Cuántas maletas para 5 viajeros?

Travelers and bags

💡 Hint: The ratio is bags : travelers = 2 : 1. Multiply travelers by 2. 5 × 2 = ?
Sentence frame: “The ratio of bags to travelers is 2 to ____. For 5 travelers there are ____ bags because 5 × 2 = ____.”
