Aquarium Architect 🐠🌊

The city has commissioned a landmark aquarium, and you, Maya, are the lead architect. Every exhibit stays sealed until you certify it: the exact volume of water it holds (even with fractional edge lengths) and the precise surface area of glass — computed from a net — needed to enclose it. Get the engineering right and the tanks come alive.

Act 1 · Volume of Prisms

Volume by Design

💧 Exhibit A — The Mangrove Pool

The mangrove pool measures 3½ ft long, 2 ft wide, and 4 ft deep. Certify its volume using V = l × w × h.

💧 Exhibit B — The Jewel Cube

A compact display measures 2½ ft × 1½ ft × 2 ft. Two edges are fractional. Find the exact volume. Volume = l × w × h. Multiply the fractions exactly — do not round.

The Reverse Blueprint

A reef tank must hold exactly 90 ft³ of water. Its base footprint is fixed at 5 ft × 6 ft. What height does the tank need? (Reverse the volume formula.)

Act 2 · Surface Area from Nets

Glass & Nets

🧱 Glazing Order #1 — Unfold & Sum

Unfold the kelp-forest tank (6 ft × 4 ft × 3 ft) into a net and sum the six face areas to order the glass. Surface area = sum of all 6 faces = 2(lw + lh + wh).

The walk-through gallery tank is 8 ft × 3 ft × 2 ft. Compute its surface area to finish the glass order. Surface area = 2(lw + lh + wh), in square feet (ft²).

A touch pool is built by fusing two prisms: a main tank 4 × 4 × 3 ft and a viewing nook 2 × 2 × 3 ft, joined on a shared 2 ft × 3 ft face (so that face is hidden on BOTH prisms). What is the surface area of the composite tank?

Final Build · Boss

The Grand Ocean Hall

🌟 Certification — Volume AND Glass

The centerpiece tank is 7 ft × 4 ft × 3 ft. Final sign-off requires BOTH the water volume and the glass surface area on one line. Choose the fully correct certification. Volume = l × w × h (ft³). Surface area = 2(lw + lh + wh) (ft²).

The Overflow Reservoir

Behind the hall, an overflow reservoir must hold exactly 96 ft³. Its footprint is fixed at 6 ft × 4 ft. What height makes it hold exactly 96 ft³?
