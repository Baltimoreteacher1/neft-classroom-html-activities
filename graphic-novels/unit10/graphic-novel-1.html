Aquarium Architect 🐠🌊

You are Maya, a young architect. You will design a big aquarium! Each tank opens when you find the right volume (how much water fits) or the right surface area (how much glass you need).

Eres Maya, una joven arquitecta. Vas a diseñar un gran acuario. Usa el volumen y el área de superficie para abrir cada tanque.

Act 1 · Volume

Fill the Tank

💧 Tank Lock #1 — Fill It Up

The clownfish tank is a box. To find volume, multiply length × width × height. The tank is 5 ft long, 4 ft wide, 3 ft tall. How much water fits? Volumen = largo × ancho × alto. ¿Cuánta agua cabe en un tanque de 5 × 4 × 3?

💡 Hint: First multiply 5 × 4 = 20. Then 20 × 3 = ?

💧 Tank Lock #2 — The Shallow Reef

The seahorse tank is long but shallow. It is 6 ft long, 4 ft wide, and only ½ ft tall. Find the volume. El tanque mide 6 × 4 × ½. Encuentra el volumen.

💡 Hint: 6 × 4 = 24. Then 24 × ½ is the same as half of 24.
Sentence frame: “The volume of the tank is ____ × ____ × ____ = ____ cubic feet (ft³).”
🔒 Finish Act 1 first to unlock Act 2.

🧱 Glass Lock #2 — Add the Glass

This tank is 4 ft long, 3 ft wide, 2 ft tall. Each pair of faces matches. Add all six faces to get the surface area (how much glass). Suma las 6 caras del tanque de 4 × 3 × 2 para hallar el área de superficie.

💡 Hint: front & back = 4×3 = 12 each. left & right = 3×2 = 6 each. top & bottom = 4×2 = 8 each. Add: 12+12+6+6+8+8.
Sentence frame: “A box has ____ faces. The surface area is the sum of all the faces: ____ ft².”
🔒 Finish Act 2 first to unlock the Final Tank.
Final Tank · Boss Build

The Grand Centerpiece

🌟 Grand Tank — Volume AND Glass

The big centerpiece tank is 6 ft long, 5 ft wide, 4 ft tall. The build needs BOTH the volume (water) and the surface area (glass). Pick the line that is fully correct. Elige la línea correcta: el volumen y el área de superficie de un tanque de 6 × 5 × 4.

💡 Hint: Volume = 6×5×4. Surface area: front/back 6×5=30 each, left/right 5×4=20 each, top/bottom 6×4=24 each. Add the six faces.
Sentence frame: “The volume is ____ ft³ and the surface area is ____ ft².”
