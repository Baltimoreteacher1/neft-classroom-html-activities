## 8. The three live-system collisions (READ BEFORE APPROVING TARGET STRUCTURE)

1. **Vite build.** `lessons/` are Vite entrypoints rendered by `engine/`, with a `closeBundle` hook copying `readiness/` into `dist/`. Moving lessons to `/unit-N/lessons/N-1/` **breaks the build entry list and the engine's path assumptions.** This is the deepest risk.
2. **Catalog/routes generators.** `data/catalog.json`, `data/routes.json`, `_redirects`, and `_headers` are **generated** by `scripts/generate-catalog.mjs` and `tools/generate-route-files.mjs`. Any move must update the generators (or the next run reverts hand edits). The Phase 2 `_redirects` plan must instead edit `data/routes.json`.
3. **Absolute paths everywhere.** Every page hardcodes `/assets/shared.css` (REPO-MAP calls this "load-bearing"). Lessons reference the engine by absolute path. Folder moves cascade into hundreds of files.

**Recommendation:** Adopt `math/unit-N/` as the canonical unit home (it already has `index.html`, `projects/`, `supplemental/`). Scope the reorg to: (a) fold theme-folder activities/games into `math/unit-N/{games,supplemental}` split by standard, (b) retire the duplicate top-level `unit-1/4/5/` practice dirs, (c) dedupe the clusters in §5, (d) leave `lessons/` and `engine/` **in place** to protect the build, with `math/unit-N/` linking to them. A literal `/unit-N/lessons/` move would require rewriting the Vite/engine layer — much larger and riskier than the cleanup you described.
