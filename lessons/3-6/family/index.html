Unit 3, Lesson 6: Use Ratio Reasoning6.RP.3

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Ratio reasoning means using a scale factor or a proportion to find a missing amount. A proportion is two equal ratios. You can check it by cross-multiplying.

Key idea: Find the scale factor, then multiply both parts; cross-multiply to check a proportion.

Key words

  • Proportion — A math sentence saying two ratios are equal.
    Español: Proporción — Una oración matemática que dice que dos razones son iguales.
  • Cross-multiply — Multiplying across two ratios to check if they are equal.
    Español: Multiplicación cruzada — Multiplicar en cruz dos razones para ver si son iguales.
  • Scale — To multiply or divide both parts of a ratio by the same number.
    Español: Escalar — Multiplicar o dividir ambas partes de una razón por el mismo número.
  • Equivalent — Having the same value.
    Español: Equivalente — Tener el mismo valor.
  • Unit rate — A rate for just 1 of something. You find it by dividing.
    Español: Tasa unitaria — Una tasa para solo 1 de algo. La encuentras al dividir.

Example

  1. Chef Reyes's recipe serves 8 people and uses 5 cups of tomatoes. The banquet has 120 guests.
  2. I find the scale factor: 120 divided by 8 = 15.
  3. I multiply the tomatoes by 15: 5 x 15 = 75 cups of tomatoes.
  4. To check, I can cross-multiply 5/8 = 75/120: 5 x 120 = 600 and 8 x 75 = 600.
  5. The cross products match, so 75 cups is correct.

Practice at home

  1. A recipe calls for 6 cups of flour for every 15 cookies. How many cups of flour are needed to make 45 cookies?

Common mistake

A common mistake in Use Ratio Reasoning is skipping the key idea: "Find the scale factor, then multiply both parts; cross-multiply to check a proportion." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
