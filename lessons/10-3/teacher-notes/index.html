Warm-up

Now a smaller box: 4 long, 3 wide, 2 tall. How many pairs of faces does it have?

Teaching notes

Key idea: A box has 3 pairs of matching faces, so add up all 6 faces to get the surface area.

I do:

  1. My box is 8 long, 5 wide, and 3 tall. I unfold it into a net and see 3 pairs of faces.
  2. Top and bottom: each is 8 × 5 = 40, and there are 2 of them, so 2 × 40 = 80.
  3. Front and back: each is 8 × 3 = 24, so 2 × 24 = 48.
  4. Left and right: each is 5 × 3 = 15, so 2 × 15 = 30.
  5. I add all the pairs: 80 + 48 + 30 = 158. The surface area is 158 in².

You do: In the table below, find the area of each face pair, then add them for the total surface area. Write your answers in square inches (in²).

Common misconception

A common mistake in Surface Area Using Nets is skipping the key idea: "A box has 3 pairs of matching faces, so add up all 6 faces to get the surface area." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Surface area, Net, Face, Two-dimensional, Edge (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A rectangular prism has l = 9 in, w = 5 in, h = 3 in. What is the surface area?

  • 174 in² ✓ correct
  • 135 in²
  • 174 in³
  • 87 in²

SA = 2(9×5) + 2(9×3) + 2(5×3) = 90 + 54 + 30 = 174 in². Surface area uses square units (in²).

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
