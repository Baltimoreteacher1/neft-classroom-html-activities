Unit 10, Lesson 3: Surface Area Using Nets6.G.4

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Surface area is the total area of all the flat faces of a solid. A net unfolds the box flat so you can see and add all 6 faces. The answer uses square units.

Key idea: A box has 3 pairs of matching faces, so add up all 6 faces to get the surface area.

Key words

  • Surface area — The total area of all the flat sides of a solid.
    Español: Área de superficie — El área total de todos los lados planos de un sólido.
  • Net — A flat shape that folds up into a solid.
    Español: Plantilla (desarrollo plano) — Una figura plana que se dobla y forma un sólido.
  • Face — One flat side of a solid shape.
    Español: Cara — Un lado plano de una figura sólida.
  • Two-dimensional — A flat shape with length and width, but no thickness.
    Español: Bidimensional — Una figura plana con largo y ancho, pero sin grosor.
  • Edge — The line where two flat sides of a solid meet.
    Español: Arista — La línea donde se unen dos lados planos de un sólido.

Example

  1. My box is 8 long, 5 wide, and 3 tall. I unfold it into a net and see 3 pairs of faces.
  2. Top and bottom: each is 8 × 5 = 40, and there are 2 of them, so 2 × 40 = 80.
  3. Front and back: each is 8 × 3 = 24, so 2 × 24 = 48.
  4. Left and right: each is 5 × 3 = 15, so 2 × 15 = 30.
  5. I add all the pairs: 80 + 48 + 30 = 158. The surface area is 158 in².
