net

Level 1 A flat shape you can fold up to make a solid 3D shape.

Example: Six squares in a row and column can fold into a cube.

⬜

face

Level 1 One flat side of a solid shape.

Example: A box has 6 faces.

🎁

surface area

Level 1 The total amount of flat space on all the outside sides of a shape added together.

Example: Add up the area of all 6 faces to get the surface area.

🟦

area

Level 1 How much flat space is inside one shape, like a wall or a side.

Example: A square that is 3 by 3 has an area of 9.

Learn It

Area of a rectangle, then add

A net shows every face of a box laid flat. The area of each rectangular face is length × width. To get surface area, find each face's area and add them all together. The answer is in square units.

One face: a 5 × 2 rectangle has area 5 × 2 = 10 square units.
Add the faces: if three faces have areas 10, 10, and 6, the total so far is 10 + 10 + 6 = 26 square units.

Level 0 Find one rectangle's area, then add a couple.

A. Area of a 3 × 2 rectangle = ___

Hint

Area = length × width = 3 × 2 = 6.

B. Add two faces: 6 + 6 = ___

Hint

6 + 6 = 12.

C. Add a third face: 12 + 4 = ___

Hint

12 + 4 = 16.

Level 1 Find face areas, then add them.

A. Area of a 5 × 4 face = ___

Hint

5 × 4 = 20.

B. A net has faces of area 12, 12, and 8. Add them: ___

Hint

12 + 12 = 24, then 24 + 8 = 32.

C. How many faces (rectangles) does a box (rectangular prism) have?

Hint

A box has 6 faces: top, bottom, front, back, and two sides.

Level 2 Stretch: add a full set of face areas.

A. A net has faces of area 10, 10, 15, 15, 6, and 6. What is the total area?

Hint

10+10 = 20, +15+15 = 50, +6+6 = 62.

B. Two faces are 4 × 3 each. What is their combined area? (square units)

Hint

Each face is 4 × 3 = 12, and 12 + 12 = 24.
