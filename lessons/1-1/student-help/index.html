I can…

I can write a number as a product of its prime factors using a factor tree.

Key words

  • Prime number — A number bigger than 1 that you can only divide by 1 and itself.
  • Composite number — A number bigger than 1 that you can divide by more than just 1 and itself.
  • Prime factorization — Writing a number as prime numbers multiplied together.
  • Factor tree — A picture that splits a number into its prime numbers, step by step.
  • Exponent — A small number that tells how many times to multiply a number by itself.

Steps

  1. I want the prime factorization of 60. I start by splitting it: 60 = 6 × 10.
  2. 6 is not prime, so I break it: 6 = 2 × 3. Both 2 and 3 are prime, so I stop those branches.
  3. 10 is not prime, so I break it: 10 = 2 × 5. Both 2 and 5 are prime, so I stop.
  4. Now every factor is prime: 60 = 2 × 2 × 3 × 5.
  5. Using exponents, I write it as 2² × 3 × 5. I check: 2 × 2 × 3 × 5 = 60. Correct.
