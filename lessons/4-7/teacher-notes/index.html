Warm-up

Arcade Depot sells 350 tokens for $38.50. What do we divide?

Teaching notes

Key idea: Divide the price by the number of items to get the cost per unit. The lower cost per unit is the better buy.

I do:

  1. Fun Supply Co. sells 500 tokens for $60. I want the cost for 1 token.
  2. I divide the price by the tokens: $60 ÷ 500 = $0.12.
  3. So this deal is $0.12 per token. That is the unit rate.
  4. Now I can compare it fairly to a deal with a different number of tokens.

You do: In the table, find the unit rate for each supplier by dividing price by quantity. Mark the option with the lower unit rate as the better buy.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Solve Problems with Unit Rates is skipping the key idea: "Divide the price by the number of items to get the cost per unit. The lower cost per unit is the better buy." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Unit Rate, Better Buy, Comparison, Per Unit, Rate, Proportion (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A car travels 195 miles on 6 gallons of gas. What is the unit rate in miles per gallon?

  • 32.5 miles per gallon ✓ correct
  • 33 miles per gallon
  • 30 miles per gallon
  • 1,170 miles per gallon

195 ÷ 6 = 32.5 miles per gallon.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
