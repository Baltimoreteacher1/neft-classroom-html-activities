Key Vocabulary Level 1 support

Picture first, then the word, then a plain-language meaning. Say each word out loud.

$2.50 per pound means $2.50 for every 1 pound

Unit Rate

A rate simplified so the second quantity is 1

$0.10 each vs $0.15 each — the $0.10 option is the better buy

Better Buy

The option with the lower unit rate (lower cost per unit)

$3 per game < $4 per game

Comparison

Looking at two or more values to see which is greater or lesser

$0.50 per token means each token costs $0.50

Per Unit

For each single item or single unit of measurement

120 miles in 3 hours is a rate (miles and hours are different units)

Rate

A ratio that compares two quantities with different units

5/3 = x/12 → x = 20

Proportion

An equation that shows two ratios are equivalent, used to solve for unknowns

Key Ideas & Notes

  • You're the new supply manager for the arcade!
  • You need to order tokens, prizes, and snacks from different suppliers.
  • Each supplier offers a different package deal.
  • Your job is to find the unit rate for each deal and figure out which supplier gives you the best value for every item you need to order.
  • Calculate the unit rate for each supplier's deal. Then determine which is the better buy for each item.

Think About It

  • How can you compare deals when the quantities are different?
  • What operation helps you find the cost for just one item?
  • Does the biggest package always give the best unit rate?

My Notes

Guided Examples

Example 1

Which is the better buy: 6 bouncy balls for $4.50 or 10 bouncy balls for $8.00?

Solution: $4.50 ÷ 6 = $0.75 each. $8.00 ÷ 10 = $0.80 each. $0.75 < $0.80, so 6 for $4.50 is the better buy.

Answer: A. 6 for $4.50 — $0.75 each

Example 2

A faucet drips 15 ounces of water in 5 minutes. What is the unit rate?

Solution: 15 ÷ 5 = 3 ounces per minute.

Answer: A. 3 ounces per minute

Example 3

A printer prints 42 pages in 6 minutes. How many pages does it print in 15 minutes at the same rate?

Solution: Unit rate: 42 ÷ 6 = 7 pages per minute. In 15 minutes: 7 × 15 = 105 pages.

Answer: A. 105 pages

Answer Key

  1. Try It 1: A. 80 tokens — Unit rate: 24 ÷ 3 = 8 tokens per minute. In 10 minutes: 8 × 10 = 80 tokens.
  2. Try It 2: A. 540 widgets — Unit rate: 360 ÷ 8 = 45 widgets per hour. In 12 hours: 45 × 12 = 540 widgets.
  3. Exit Ticket: A. 32.5 miles per gallon — 195 ÷ 6 = 32.5 miles per gallon.
