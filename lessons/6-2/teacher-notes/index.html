Warm-up

Let's try a simpler one. Find 150 + 12s when s = 1.

Teaching notes

Key idea: Replace the variable with its number, then follow the order of operations to find the total.

I do:

  1. Our cost expression is 150 + 12s, where s is the number of speakers.
  2. I want the cost for s = 4 speakers.
  3. I replace s with 4: 150 + 12 × 4.
  4. First I multiply: 12 × 4 = 48.
  5. Then I add: 150 + 48 = 198. So 4 speakers cost $198.

You do: In the table, evaluate 150 + 12s for each number of speakers. Start with s = 2: 150 + 12(2).

Common misconception

A common mistake in Evaluate Expressions is skipping the key idea: "Replace the variable with its number, then follow the order of operations to find the total." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Variable, Expression, Evaluate, Substitute, Coefficient, Like terms (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

Evaluate 5(n − 3) + 2 when n = 7.

  • 22 ✓ correct
  • 32
  • 17
  • 36

5(7 − 3) + 2 = 5(4) + 2 = 20 + 2 = 22.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
