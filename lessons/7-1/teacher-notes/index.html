Unit 7, Lesson 1: Write Equations6.EE.6

Everything you need to teach this tomorrow. ~45 min.

Objectives

Content: I can write an equation to represent a real-world situation.

Language: I can explain my equation using the words equation, variable, equal sign, and expression.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

New clue: 'A number plus 5 equals 9.' What is the unknown?

Teaching notes

Key idea: Find the unknown, choose a variable, then turn the words into symbols around an equal sign.

I do:

  1. Clue: 'A number of stolen gems plus 8 more equals 20 total.'
  2. The unknown is the number of gems. I let n stand for it.
  3. 'Plus 8 more' means + 8. 'Equals 20 total' means = 20.
  4. So my equation is n + 8 = 20.

You do: On the balance scale, build n + 8 = 20 and find what goes on each side. Then write equations for the practice clues on your own.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Write Equations is skipping the key idea: "Find the unknown, choose a variable, then turn the words into symbols around an equal sign." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Equation, Variable, Equal sign, Expression, Constant, Unknown (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

Which equation represents: 'A number divided by 6 equals 9'?

  • n / 6 = 9 ✓ correct
  • 6n = 9
  • n − 6 = 9
  • n + 6 = 9

'Divided by 6' means division: n / 6 = 9. Check: 54 / 6 = 9 ✓

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

A detective is tracking stolen paintings. She knows that the number of paintings recovered plus 14 paintings still missing equals the 37 total stolen. The equation p + 14 = 37 represents this situation.
