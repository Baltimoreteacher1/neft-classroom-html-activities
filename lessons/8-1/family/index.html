Unit 8, Lesson 1: Statistical Questions and Data6.SP.1

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A statistical question is one you answer by collecting data, and the answers are different for different people or cases. A non-statistical question has just one fixed answer.

Key idea: If you expect the answers to vary, it is a statistical question.

Key words

  • Statistical Question — A question where the answers will be different for different people.
    Español: Pregunta estadística — Una pregunta cuyas respuestas serán distintas para distintas personas.
  • Data — Facts and numbers you collect, like answers to a survey.
    Español: Datos — Datos y números que recoges, como respuestas a una encuesta.
  • Variability — How spread out the numbers are.
    Español: Variabilidad — Qué tan separados están los números.
  • Survey — Collecting facts by asking people questions.
    Español: Encuesta — Recoger datos haciendo preguntas a las personas.
  • Data distribution — How the data looks: where it sits and how spread out it is.
    Español: Distribución de datos — Cómo se ven los datos: dónde están y qué tan separados están.

Example

  1. Question 1: "How many points did our top player score last night?" There is one player and one game, so there is one fixed answer, like 18 points.
  2. One answer, no variety, so this is NOT a statistical question.
  3. Question 2: "How many points does each player score per game?" The answers vary: 8, 12, 12, 15, 18, 20, 22.
  4. Because the answers are different for different players, I must collect data. This IS a statistical question.

Practice at home

  1. Which question is a statistical question? A) How many students are in the school? B) How many hours does each student in 6th grade exercise per week? C) What time does school start? D) How many days are in a week?

Answer check

  1. Which question is a statistical question? A) How many students are in the school? B) How many hours does each student in 6th grade exercise per week? C) What time does school start? D) How many days are in a week? → B) How many hours does each student exercise per week?

Common mistake

A common mistake in Statistical Questions and Data is skipping the key idea: "If you expect the answers to vary, it is a statistical question." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
