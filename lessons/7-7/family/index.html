Unit 7, Lesson 7: Equations and Inequalities Problem Solving6.EE.7

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

To model means to write a real situation as math. I use an equation when there is one exact total, and an inequality when there is a limit or a range of allowed values.

Key idea: 'Equals' or 'exactly' points to an equation (=); 'at least, at most, more than, fewer than' points to an inequality.

Key words

  • Model — To show a real-life situation with an equation or inequality.
    Español: Modelar — Mostrar una situación real con una ecuación o desigualdad.
  • Equation — A math sentence with an equal sign showing both sides are the same.
    Español: Ecuación — Una oración matemática con un signo igual que muestra que ambos lados son iguales.
  • Inequality — A math sentence that compares two sides with <, >, ≤, or ≥.
    Español: Desigualdad — Una oración matemática que compara dos lados con <, >, ≤ o ≥.
  • Reasonableness — Checking if your answer makes sense.
    Español: Razonabilidad — Revisar si tu respuesta tiene sentido.
  • Variable — A letter that stands for the unknown amount in a word problem.
    Español: Variable — Una letra que representa la cantidad desconocida en un problema verbal.
  • Constraint — A limit in a problem that tells what values are allowed.
    Español: Restricción — Un límite en un problema que indica qué valores se permiten.

Example

  1. Clue: 'A total divided equally among 4 people gave each $85.'
  2. This has an exact total, so I use an equation. Let t be the total.
  3. Divided among 4 to get 85 means t ÷ 4 = 85, so t = 85 × 4 = 340.
  4. Check reasonableness: $340 split 4 ways is $85 each. That makes sense!

Practice at home

  1. A box of donuts has some donuts. After giving away 7, there are fewer than 5 left. Which inequality represents the starting number of donuts d?

Common mistake

A common mistake in Equations and Inequalities Problem Solving is skipping the key idea: "'Equals' or 'exactly' points to an equation (=); 'at least, at most, more than, fewer than' points to an inequality." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
