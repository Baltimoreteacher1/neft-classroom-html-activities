Sound Studio Mission

The Doubling Mixboard

You are the lead sound engineer at Amplitude Studios. Every time you flip a switch on the new mixboard, the volume doubles — that is the power of exponents. The headline act goes on in one hour, and you must master powers before the speakers can be safely dialed in.

Key Vocabulary Level 1 support

Picture first, then the word, then a plain-language meaning. Say each word out loud.

In 2³, the small 3 means multiply 2 by itself 3 times: 2 × 2 × 2 = 8

Exponent

A number that tells how many times to use the base as a factor

In 5², the base is 5 — it is the number being multiplied: 5 × 5 = 25

Base

The number that is used as a repeated factor

10³ = 10 × 10 × 10 = 1,000 — read as '10 to the third power' or '10 cubed'

Power

An expression written with a base and an exponent

Evaluate 3⁴: write 3 × 3 × 3 × 3, then multiply step by step: 9 × 9 = 81

Evaluate

To find the value of a numerical or algebraic expression

In 3x, the 3 is the coefficient — if x = 4, then 3x = 3 × 4 = 12

Coefficient

The number multiplied by a variable

4x and 2x are like terms (both have x); 4x and 4x² are NOT like terms (different powers)

Like terms

Terms with the same variable raised to the same power

Key Ideas & Notes

  • You're a sound engineer at a music studio.
  • Each time you flip a switch, the volume doubles.
  • The starting volume is 1 unit.
  • After flipping the switch 3 times, the volume is 2³ = 2 × 2 × 2 = 8 units — that's 8 times louder!
  • Evaluate each exponential expression. Write the repeated multiplication and find the value.

Think About It

  • What happens to the volume each time you flip a switch?
  • Why is 2³ equal to 8, not 6?
  • How is repeated multiplication different from repeated addition?

My Notes

Guided Examples

Example 1

What is the value of 4³?

Solution: 4³ = 4 × 4 × 4 = 64.

Answer: A. 64

Example 2

What is the value of 6²?

Solution: 6² = 6 × 6 = 36.

Answer: A. 36

Example 3

Which expression shows 5³ as repeated multiplication?

Solution: 5³ means use 5 as a factor 3 times: 5 × 5 × 5. The exponent tells how many times to multiply, not what to multiply by.

Answer: A. 5 × 5 × 5
