Unit 1, Lesson 7: Divide Decimals6.NS.3

Objectives

Content: I can divide with decimals by making the divisor a whole number first.

Language: I can explain my work using the words dividend, divisor, quotient, and equivalent division.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.

Warm-up

Let's do 8.4 ÷ 2.1. How many places do we move to make 2.1 a whole number? (1 place, so 2.1 becomes 21.)

Teaching notes

Key idea: Move the decimal point the same number of places in BOTH the divisor and the dividend; the answer stays the same.

I do:

  1. I want 18.9 ÷ 6.3. The divisor is 6.3, and I want it to be a whole number.
  2. I move the decimal point 1 place right in the divisor: 6.3 becomes 63.
  3. I must move the dividend the same 1 place right: 18.9 becomes 189. Now the problem is 189 ÷ 63.
  4. I divide: 189 ÷ 63 = 3. So 18.9 ÷ 6.3 = 3.
  5. I check by multiplying: 6.3 × 3 = 18.9. Correct — 3 pods can be filled.

You do: Next, you will rewrite decimal divisions as whole-number divisions and solve them on your own. Remember: move the point the same number of places in both numbers.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Divide Decimals is skipping the key idea: "Move the decimal point the same number of places in BOTH the divisor and the dividend; the answer stays the same." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Dividend, Divisor, Quotient, Decimal division, Equivalent division (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.
