Unit 1, Lesson 7: Divide Decimals6.NS.3

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Dividing splits a total into equal groups. When you divide by a decimal, you first make the divisor (the number you divide by) a whole number, so the division is easier.

Key idea: Move the decimal point the same number of places in BOTH the divisor and the dividend; the answer stays the same.

Key words

  • Dividend — The number you are splitting up in a division problem.
    Español: Dividendo — El número que estás repartiendo en una división.
  • Divisor — The number you split by in a division problem.
    Español: Divisor — El número entre el que divides en una división.
  • Quotient — The answer when you divide.
    Español: Cociente — La respuesta cuando divides.
  • Decimal division — Dividing with decimals. First make the number you divide by a whole number.
    Español: División decimal — Dividir con decimales. Primero haz entero el número entre el que divides.
  • Equivalent division — Multiplying both numbers by 10 or 100 gives the same answer.
    Español: División equivalente — Multiplicar ambos números por 10 o 100 da la misma respuesta.

Example

  1. I want 18.9 ÷ 6.3. The divisor is 6.3, and I want it to be a whole number.
  2. I move the decimal point 1 place right in the divisor: 6.3 becomes 63.
  3. I must move the dividend the same 1 place right: 18.9 becomes 189. Now the problem is 189 ÷ 63.
  4. I divide: 189 ÷ 63 = 3. So 18.9 ÷ 6.3 = 3.
  5. I check by multiplying: 6.3 × 3 = 18.9. Correct — 3 pods can be filled.

Practice at home

Match each decimal division to its quotient.

  1. 7.2 ÷ 0.8
  2. 4.56 ÷ 0.6
  3. 16.8 ÷ 2.4

Answer check

  1. 7.2 ÷ 0.8 → 9
  2. 4.56 ÷ 0.6 → 7.6
  3. 16.8 ÷ 2.4 → 7

Common mistake

A common mistake in Divide Decimals is skipping the key idea: "Move the decimal point the same number of places in BOTH the divisor and the dividend; the answer stays the same." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
