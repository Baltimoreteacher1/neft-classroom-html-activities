Unit 2, Lesson 1: Interpret Division of Fractions6.NS.1

Everything you need to teach this tomorrow. ~50 min.

Objectives

Content: I can use models to interpret what it means to divide by a fraction.

Language: I can explain a fraction division using the words dividend, divisor, quotient, reciprocal, and unit fraction.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.

Warm-up

Let's try 2 ÷ 1/3. How many 1/3-size pieces fit into 2?

Teaching notes

Key idea: Dividing by a fraction smaller than 1 gives an answer BIGGER than what you started with, because many tiny pieces fit inside.

I do:

  1. I want to cut a 3-foot strip of tape into 1/4-foot pieces. I ask: how many 1/4-foot pieces fit into 3 feet?
  2. First I look at just 1 foot. How many 1/4-foot pieces fit in 1 foot? Four, because 4 fourths make one whole.
  3. Now I have 3 feet. So I do 3 groups of 4: 3 × 4 = 12.
  4. So 3 ÷ 1/4 = 12. There are 12 pieces. The answer is bigger than 3 because each piece is very small.

You do: Now use the bar model to show how many 1/4-pieces fit into 3 wholes, then move on to the practice problems.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Interpret Division of Fractions is skipping the key idea: "Dividing by a fraction smaller than 1 gives an answer BIGGER than what you started with, because many tiny pieces fit inside." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Dividend, Divisor, Quotient, Reciprocal, Unit fraction (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

What does 5 ÷ 1/4 mean, and what is the quotient?

  • How many 1/4-size pieces fit into 5; quotient is 20 ✓ correct
  • 5 groups of 1/4; quotient is 5/4
  • 1/4 of 5; quotient is 5/4
  • 5 minus 1/4; quotient is 4 3/4

5 ÷ 1/4 means 'how many 1/4-size pieces fit into 5.' Since each whole has 4 fourths, 5 × 4 = 20.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
