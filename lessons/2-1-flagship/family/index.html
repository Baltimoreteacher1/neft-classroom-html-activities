Unit 2, Lesson 1: Interpret Division of Fractions6.NS.1

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Dividing by a fraction asks one question: how many of that small piece fit inside the whole amount? For 3 ÷ 1/4, we ask how many 1/4-foot pieces fit into 3 feet.

Key idea: Dividing by a fraction smaller than 1 gives an answer BIGGER than what you started with, because many tiny pieces fit inside.

Key words

  • Dividend — The number you are splitting up in a division problem.
    Español: Dividendo — El número que estás repartiendo en una división.
  • Divisor — The number you split by in a division problem.
    Español: Divisor — El número entre el que divides en una división.
  • Quotient — The answer when you divide.
    Español: Cociente — La respuesta cuando divides.
  • Reciprocal — A fraction turned upside down.
    Español: Recíproco — Una fracción volteada de arriba abajo.
  • Unit fraction — A fraction with 1 on top, like 1/4.
    Español: Fracción unitaria — Una fracción con 1 arriba, como 1/4.

Example

  1. I want to cut a 3-foot strip of tape into 1/4-foot pieces. I ask: how many 1/4-foot pieces fit into 3 feet?
  2. First I look at just 1 foot. How many 1/4-foot pieces fit in 1 foot? Four, because 4 fourths make one whole.
  3. Now I have 3 feet. So I do 3 groups of 4: 3 × 4 = 12.
  4. So 3 ÷ 1/4 = 12. There are 12 pieces. The answer is bigger than 3 because each piece is very small.

Practice at home

Match each division expression to its quotient.

  1. 5 ÷ 1/2
  2. 6 ÷ 1/3
  3. 2 ÷ 1/6

Answer check

  1. 5 ÷ 1/2 → 10
  2. 6 ÷ 1/3 → 18
  3. 2 ÷ 1/6 → 12

Common mistake

A common mistake in Interpret Division of Fractions is skipping the key idea: "Dividing by a fraction smaller than 1 gives an answer BIGGER than what you started with, because many tiny pieces fit inside." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
