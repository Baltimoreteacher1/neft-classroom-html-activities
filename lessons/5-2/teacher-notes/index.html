Unit 5, Lesson 2: Area of Trapezoids6.G.1

Objectives

Language: I can explain my steps using the words trapezoid, base, height, and area.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

Now a smaller trapezoid: bases 3 cm and 5 cm, height 2 cm.

Teaching notes

Key idea: Area of a trapezoid = ½ × (base 1 + base 2) × height. We add the two bases first, then take half.

I do:

  1. My trapezoid window has a top base of 4 feet, a bottom base of 8 feet, and a height of 5 feet.
  2. I write the formula: A = ½ × (b1 + b2) × h.
  3. I add the bases first: 4 + 8 = 12.
  4. I multiply by the height: 12 × 5 = 60.
  5. I take half: ½ × 60 = 30. So the area is 30 square feet.

You do: Next you will plot the window on a grid and find its area using the formula. Remember the order: add the bases, multiply by the height, then take half.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Area of Trapezoids is skipping the key idea: "Area of a trapezoid = ½ × (base 1 + base 2) × height. We add the two bases first, then take half." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Trapezoid, Base 1 (b1), Base 2 (b2), Height, Area (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A trapezoid has bases of 9 inches and 5 inches, and a height of 6 inches. What is its area?

  • 42 sq in ✓ correct
  • 84 sq in
  • 27 sq in
  • 42 in

A = 1/2 x (b1 + b2) x h = 1/2 x (9 + 5) x 6 = 1/2 x 14 x 6 = 42 square inches.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
