Unit 5, Lesson 2: Area of Trapezoids6.G.1

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A trapezoid is a four-sided shape with one pair of parallel sides. The two parallel sides are called the bases, and they have different lengths.

Key idea: Area of a trapezoid = ½ × (base 1 + base 2) × height. We add the two bases first, then take half.

Key words

  • Trapezoid — A four-sided shape with just one pair of parallel sides.
    Español: Trapecio — Una figura de cuatro lados con solo un par de lados paralelos.
  • Base 1 (b1) — One of the two parallel sides of a trapezoid.
    Español: Base 1 (b1) — Uno de los dos lados paralelos de un trapecio.
  • Base 2 (b2) — The other parallel side of a trapezoid.
    Español: Base 2 (b2) — El otro lado paralelo de un trapecio.
  • Height — The straight-up distance between the two parallel sides.
    Español: Altura — La distancia recta entre los dos lados paralelos.
  • Area — How much space is inside a flat shape.
    Español: Área — Cuánto espacio hay dentro de una figura plana.

Example

  1. My trapezoid window has a top base of 4 feet, a bottom base of 8 feet, and a height of 5 feet.
  2. I write the formula: A = ½ × (b1 + b2) × h.
  3. I add the bases first: 4 + 8 = 12.
  4. I multiply by the height: 12 × 5 = 60.
  5. I take half: ½ × 60 = 30. So the area is 30 square feet.

Practice at home

Match each shape to its area.

  1. Trapezoid b₁=10, b₂=6, h=4
  2. Trapezoid b₁=8, b₂=4, h=5
  3. Trapezoid b₁=12, b₂=8, h=3

Answer check

  1. Trapezoid b₁=10, b₂=6, h=4 → 32 sq units
  2. Trapezoid b₁=8, b₂=4, h=5 → 30 sq units
  3. Trapezoid b₁=12, b₂=8, h=3 → 30 sq units

Common mistake

A common mistake in Area of Trapezoids is skipping the key idea: "Area of a trapezoid = ½ × (base 1 + base 2) × height. We add the two bases first, then take half." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
