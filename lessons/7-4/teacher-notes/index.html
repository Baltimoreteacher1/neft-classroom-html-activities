Unit 7, Lesson 4: Write Inequalities6.EE.8

Everything you need to teach this tomorrow. ~45 min.

Objectives

Content: I can write inequalities to represent real-world situations.

Language: I can explain my inequality using the words inequality, greater than, less than, and at least / at most.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

Clue: 'A bag holds fewer than 10 items.' What does 'fewer than' mean?

Teaching notes

Key idea: 'At least' means ≥ and 'at most' means ≤; 'more than' means > and 'fewer/less than' means <.

I do:

  1. Clue: 'The suspect is at least 18 years old.'
  2. I let a stand for the age. 'At least 18' means 18 or more.
  3. 'At least' includes 18, so I use the symbol ≥.
  4. My inequality is a ≥ 18. So a could be 18, 19, 20, and up.

You do: Sort the phrase cards into the matching symbol: >, <, ≥, or ≤. Then write inequalities for the practice situations.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Write Inequalities is skipping the key idea: "'At least' means ≥ and 'at most' means ≤; 'more than' means > and 'fewer/less than' means <." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Inequality, Greater than, Less than, At least / At most, Variable, No more than (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

Which inequality represents: 'A number n is at least 14'?

  • n ≥ 14 ✓ correct
  • n > 14
  • n ≤ 14
  • n < 14

'At least 14' means 14 or greater, so n ≥ 14.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

A detective knows the getaway car was traveling at most 65 miles per hour on the highway. The inequality s ≤ 65 represents the car's speed s.
