Unit 7, Lesson 4: Write Inequalities6.EE.8

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

An inequality compares two amounts using <, >, ≤, or ≥. I use one when many values are allowed, not just one. I pick a variable and match the words to the right symbol.

Key idea: 'At least' means ≥ and 'at most' means ≤; 'more than' means > and 'fewer/less than' means <.

Key words

  • Inequality — A math sentence that compares two sides with <, >, ≤, or ≥.
    Español: Desigualdad — Una oración matemática que compara dos lados con <, >, ≤ o ≥.
  • Greater than — The > sign, showing one number is bigger.
    Español: Mayor que — El signo >, que muestra que un número es mayor.
  • Less than — The < sign, showing one number is smaller.
    Español: Menor que — El signo <, que muestra que un número es menor.
  • At least / At most — 'At least' means ≥. 'At most' means ≤.
    Español: Al menos / A lo más — 'Al menos' significa ≥. 'Como máximo' significa ≤.
  • Variable — A letter that stands for an unknown number.
    Español: Variable — Una letra que representa un número desconocido.
  • No more than — A phrase meaning less than or equal to (≤).
    Español: No más de — Una frase que significa menor o igual que (≤).

Example

  1. Clue: 'The suspect is at least 18 years old.'
  2. I let a stand for the age. 'At least 18' means 18 or more.
  3. 'At least' includes 18, so I use the symbol ≥.
  4. My inequality is a ≥ 18. So a could be 18, 19, 20, and up.

Practice at home

  1. Which inequality represents: 'A number n is at least 14'?

Answer check

  1. Which inequality represents: 'A number n is at least 14'? → n ≥ 14

Common mistake

A common mistake in Write Inequalities is skipping the key idea: "'At least' means ≥ and 'at most' means ≤; 'more than' means > and 'fewer/less than' means <." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
