Unit 6, Lesson 7: Simplify Algebraic Expressions6.EE.3

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Like terms are terms with the same variable, like 2x and 5x. To simplify an expression, you add or subtract the like terms to make it shorter.

Key idea: Only combine terms that have the same variable part; numbers without a variable combine with each other.

Key words

  • Like Terms — Terms with the same letter, like 2x and 5x.
    Español: Términos semejantes — Términos con la misma letra, como 2x y 5x.
  • Combine — To add or subtract terms with the same letter into one.
    Español: Combinar — Sumar o restar términos con la misma letra en uno solo.
  • Simplify — To write an expression in its shortest form.
    Español: Simplificar — Escribir una expresión en su forma más corta.
  • Coefficient — The number in front of a letter, like the 3 in 3x.
    Español: Coeficiente — El número frente a una letra, como el 3 en 3x.
  • Term — A single number, variable, or product of them, separated by + or − signs.
    Español: Término — Un solo número, variable o producto de ellos, separado por signos + o −.
  • Distributive property — Multiplying a sum by a number means multiplying each part by that number.
    Español: Propiedad distributiva — Multiplicar una suma por un número significa multiplicar cada parte por ese número.

Example

  1. The rooms need microphones and stands: (4 + 2 + 6) microphones and (3 + 5 + 1) stands.
  2. Microphones are like items, so I add: 4 + 2 + 6 = 12 microphones.
  3. Stands are like items, so I add: 3 + 5 + 1 = 9 stands.
  4. In algebra this is the same as 4m + 2m + 6m + 3s + 5s + 1s = 12m + 9s.

Practice at home

Match each expression to its simplified form.

  1. 8x + 3x + 1
  2. 5a − 2a + 7
  3. 4n + n + 6

Answer check

  1. 8x + 3x + 1 → 11x + 1
  2. 5a − 2a + 7 → 3a + 7
  3. 4n + n + 6 → 5n + 6

Common mistake

A common mistake in Simplify Algebraic Expressions is skipping the key idea: "Only combine terms that have the same variable part; numbers without a variable combine with each other." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
