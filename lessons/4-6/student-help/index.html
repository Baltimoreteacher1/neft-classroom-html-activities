I can…

I can convert measurement units using ratios and conversion factors.

Key words

  • Conversion Factor — A ratio that helps you change one unit into another.
  • Unit — A standard amount used to measure, like an inch or a liter.
  • Equivalent — Having the same value, just in different units.
  • Ratio — A way to compare two amounts.
  • Customary units — The U.S. way of measuring, like inches, feet, and pounds.
  • Metric units — A way of measuring by 10s, like meters, liters, and grams.

Steps

  1. The cabinet is 5 feet tall. I want to know how many inches that is.
  2. My conversion factor is 1 foot = 12 inches.
  3. Inches are smaller than feet, so I multiply: 5 × 12 = 60.
  4. So 5 feet = 60 inches.
