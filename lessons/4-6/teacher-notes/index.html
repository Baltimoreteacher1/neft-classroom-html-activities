Unit 4, Lesson 6: Convert Measurement Units6.RP.3d

Everything you need to teach this tomorrow. ~45 min.

Objectives

Content: I can convert measurement units using ratios and conversion factors.

Language: I can explain my conversion using the words conversion factor, unit, equivalent, and ratio.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

A wire is 4 yards long. The factor is 1 yard = 3 feet.

Teaching notes

Key idea: Multiply when changing to a smaller unit, and divide when changing to a bigger unit.

I do:

  1. The cabinet is 5 feet tall. I want to know how many inches that is.
  2. My conversion factor is 1 foot = 12 inches.
  3. Inches are smaller than feet, so I multiply: 5 × 12 = 60.
  4. So 5 feet = 60 inches.

You do: In the table, use the conversion factor to fill in each converted measurement. Decide each time: are you going to a smaller unit (multiply) or a bigger unit (divide)?

Common misconception

A common mistake in Convert Measurement Units is skipping the key idea: "Multiply when changing to a smaller unit, and divide when changing to a bigger unit." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Conversion Factor, Unit, Equivalent, Ratio, Customary units, Metric units (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

Which is the correct conversion of 5 yards to inches?

  • 180 inches ✓ correct
  • 60 inches
  • 15 inches
  • 540 inches

5 yards × 3 = 15 feet. 15 feet × 12 = 180 inches. Or: 5 × 36 = 180 inches.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

The arcade is ordering a new game cabinet. The doorway is 80 inches tall. The cabinet is listed as 7 feet. Your friend says it won't fit because 7 feet is 82 inches.
