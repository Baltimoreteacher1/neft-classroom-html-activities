Convert Measurement Units

Lesson 4-6

Name:Date:Class:

Vocabulary

Conversion Factor

A ratio used to convert from one unit to another

1 foot = 12 inches, so the factor is 12

Unit

A standard quantity used for measuring

Inches, feet, pounds, ounces

Equivalent

Having the same value expressed in different units

2 feet = 24 inches

Ratio

A comparison of two quantities by division

12 inches per 1 foot

Customary units

The measurement system used in the United States (inches, feet, yards, pounds, ounces)

12 inches = 1 foot, 3 feet = 1 yard, 16 ounces = 1 pound

Metric units

The measurement system based on powers of 10 (meters, liters, grams)

1 meter = 100 centimeters, 1 kilogram = 1,000 grams

Key Ideas & Notes

  • You're building a brand-new arcade cabinet!
  • The blueprints use feet, but the parts catalog lists measurements in inches.
  • The wiring instructions use yards, and the weight limits are in pounds — but the shipping labels use ounces.
  • You need to convert between units to make sure everything fits and ships correctly.
  • Use conversion factors to complete the ratio table. Convert each measurement to the target unit.

Think About It

  • Why might the blueprint and the parts catalog use different units?
  • How many inches are in 1 foot? How many feet in 1 yard?
  • How do you decide whether to multiply or divide when converting?

Guided Examples

Example 1

How many inches are in 7 feet?

Solution: 7 × 12 = 84 inches.

Answer: A. 84 inches

Example 2

How many feet are in 3 yards?

Solution: 1 yard = 3 feet. 3 × 3 = 9 feet.

Answer: A. 9 feet

Example 3

How many ounces are in 4 pounds?

Solution: 1 pound = 16 ounces. 4 × 16 = 64 ounces.

Answer: A. 64 ounces

Answer Key

  1. Try It 1: A. 2 feet — 24 ÷ 12 = 2 feet.
  2. Try It 2: A. 2 feet 6 inches — 30 ÷ 12 = 2 remainder 6. So 30 inches = 2 feet 6 inches.
  3. Exit Ticket: A. 180 inches — 5 yards × 3 = 15 feet. 15 feet × 12 = 180 inches. Or: 5 × 36 = 180 inches.
