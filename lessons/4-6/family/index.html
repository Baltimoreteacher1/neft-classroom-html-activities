Unit 4, Lesson 6: Convert Measurement Units6.RP.3d

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A unit is a standard amount used to measure, like a foot or an inch. A conversion factor is a ratio that relates two units, like 1 foot = 12 inches.

Key idea: Multiply when changing to a smaller unit, and divide when changing to a bigger unit.

Key words

  • Conversion Factor — A ratio that helps you change one unit into another.
    Español: Factor de conversión — Una razón que te ayuda a cambiar una unidad por otra.
  • Unit — A standard amount used to measure, like an inch or a liter.
    Español: Unidad — Una cantidad estándar para medir, como una pulgada o un litro.
  • Equivalent — Having the same value, just in different units.
    Español: Equivalente — Tener el mismo valor, solo en unidades distintas.
  • Ratio — A way to compare two amounts.
    Español: Razón — Una manera de comparar dos cantidades.
  • Customary units — The U.S. way of measuring, like inches, feet, and pounds.
    Español: Unidades usuales (sistema inglés) — La forma de medir de EE. UU., como pulgadas, pies y libras.
  • Metric units — A way of measuring by 10s, like meters, liters, and grams.
    Español: Unidades métricas — Una forma de medir de 10 en 10, como metros, litros y gramos.

Example

  1. The cabinet is 5 feet tall. I want to know how many inches that is.
  2. My conversion factor is 1 foot = 12 inches.
  3. Inches are smaller than feet, so I multiply: 5 × 12 = 60.
  4. So 5 feet = 60 inches.

Practice at home

  1. 3 yards
  2. 4 feet
  3. 5 pounds

Answer check

  1. 3 yards → 9 feet
  2. 4 feet → 48 inches
  3. 5 pounds → 80 ounces

Common mistake

A common mistake in Convert Measurement Units is skipping the key idea: "Multiply when changing to a smaller unit, and divide when changing to a bigger unit." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
