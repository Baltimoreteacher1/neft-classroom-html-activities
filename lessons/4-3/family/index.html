Unit 4, Lesson 3: Percents Greater Than 100% and Less Than 1%6.RP.3c

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

100% means one whole. A percent greater than 100% is more than the whole. A percent less than 1% is a very tiny part.

Key idea: To change any percent to a decimal, divide by 100 (move the decimal point two places to the left).

Key words

  • Percent — A way to compare a number to 100, shown with the % sign.
    Español: Porcentaje — Una manera de comparar un número con 100, con el signo %.
  • Greater than 100% — A percent bigger than 100%, more than the whole thing.
    Español: Mayor que 100% — Un porcentaje mayor que 100%, más que todo el entero.
  • Less than 1% — A percent smaller than 1%, a very tiny part.
    Español: Menor que 1% — Un porcentaje menor que 1%, una parte muy pequeña.
  • Equivalent — Having the same value, just written a different way.
    Español: Equivalente — Tener el mismo valor, escrito de otra forma.
  • Mixed number — A whole number plus a fraction, like 1 1/2.
    Español: Número mixto — Un número entero más una fracción, como 1 1/2.

Example

  1. A player hit 150% of the goal. I want this as a decimal.
  2. I divide by 100: 150 ÷ 100 = 1.5.
  3. Since 1.5 is more than 1, this player beat the whole goal.
  4. Now a tiny boost of 0.5%: 0.5 ÷ 100 = 0.005, much less than 1.

Practice at home

  1. 1.5
  2. 0.003
  3. 2.75

Answer check

  1. 1.5 → 150%
  2. 0.003 → 0.3%
  3. 2.75 → 275%

Common mistake

A common mistake in Percents Greater Than 100% and Less Than 1% is skipping the key idea: "To change any percent to a decimal, divide by 100 (move the decimal point two places to the left)." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
