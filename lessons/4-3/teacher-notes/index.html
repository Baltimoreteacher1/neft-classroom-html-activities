Unit 4, Lesson 3: Percents Greater Than 100% and Less Than 1%6.RP.3c

Objectives

Content: I can write and interpret percents greater than 100% and less than 1%.

Language: I can explain my reasoning using the words percent, greater than 100%, less than 1%, and equivalent.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

Let's write 200% as a decimal. What is 200 ÷ 100? (2.0)

Teaching notes

Key idea: To change any percent to a decimal, divide by 100 (move the decimal point two places to the left).

I do:

  1. A player hit 150% of the goal. I want this as a decimal.
  2. I divide by 100: 150 ÷ 100 = 1.5.
  3. Since 1.5 is more than 1, this player beat the whole goal.
  4. Now a tiny boost of 0.5%: 0.5 ÷ 100 = 0.005, much less than 1.

You do: Sort each value into greater than 100%, between 1% and 100%, or less than 1%. Think about whether the decimal form is bigger than 1 or close to 0.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Percents Greater Than 100% and Less Than 1% is skipping the key idea: "To change any percent to a decimal, divide by 100 (move the decimal point two places to the left)." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Percent, Greater than 100%, Less than 1%, Equivalent, Mixed number (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

Which statement is true about 0.25%?

  • It equals 0.0025 as a decimal ✓ correct
  • It equals 0.25 as a decimal
  • It equals 1/4 as a fraction
  • It is greater than 1%

0.25% = 0.25 ÷ 100 = 0.0025. It is much less than 1%, not equal to 1/4.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension
