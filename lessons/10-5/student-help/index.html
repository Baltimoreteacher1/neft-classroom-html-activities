I can…

I can find the surface area of a pyramid by adding the base area and the lateral faces.

Key words

  • Pyramid — A solid with a flat bottom and triangle sides that meet at one point on top.
  • Slant height — The height of a side triangle, measured along its slanted face.
  • Lateral face — A triangle side of a pyramid, not the bottom.
  • Base — The flat bottom of a pyramid.
  • Apex — The point at the top of a pyramid where the sides meet.
  • Lateral area — The total area of just the side triangles, not the bottom.

Steps

  1. My square pyramid has a 6 × 6 base and a slant height of 8.
  2. First the base area: 6 × 6 = 36.
  3. Now one triangular face: ½ × 6 × 8 = 24. A square pyramid has 4 of them, so 4 × 24 = 96.
  4. I add the base and the side faces: 36 + 96 = 132.
  5. So the total surface area is 132 in². I used the slant height (8) for the triangles, not the pyramid's height.

Try it

A square pyramid has a base edge of 6 in and a slant height of 5 in. What is the total surface area?

Check your answer

96 in²

Base: 6 × 6 = 36 in². Each lateral face: ½ × 6 × 5 = 15 in². Four faces: 4 × 15 = 60 in². SA = 36 + 60 = 96 in². Surface area uses square units (in²).
