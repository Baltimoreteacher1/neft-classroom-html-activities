Warm-up

Now a smaller square pyramid: base 4 × 4, slant height 5. What is the base area?

Teaching notes

Key idea: Surface area of a pyramid = base area + the area of all the triangular lateral faces.

I do:

  1. My square pyramid has a 6 × 6 base and a slant height of 8.
  2. First the base area: 6 × 6 = 36.
  3. Now one triangular face: ½ × 6 × 8 = 24. A square pyramid has 4 of them, so 4 × 24 = 96.
  4. I add the base and the side faces: 36 + 96 = 132.
  5. So the total surface area is 132 in². I used the slant height (8) for the triangles, not the pyramid's height.

You do: In the table below, find the area of one lateral face, then the total surface area of each pyramid display. Remember: total SA = base area + all the triangular faces.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Surface Area of Pyramids is skipping the key idea: "Surface area of a pyramid = base area + the area of all the triangular lateral faces." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

Exit ticket

A square pyramid has a base edge of 6 in and a slant height of 5 in. What is the total surface area?

  • 96 in² ✓ correct
  • 60 in²
  • 96 in³
  • 36 in²

Base: 6 × 6 = 36 in². Each lateral face: ½ × 6 × 5 = 15 in². Four faces: 4 × 15 = 60 in². SA = 36 + 60 = 96 in². Surface area uses square units (in²).

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
