Unit 3, Lesson 7: Ratio and Rate Problem Solving6.RP.3

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A rate compares two amounts with different units, like dollars and pounds. A unit rate is the amount for just 1. You find it by dividing, and you can use it to find any amount.

Key idea: Find the unit rate by dividing, then multiply it to find the amount you need.

Key words

  • Rate — A ratio comparing two amounts with different units, like miles per hour.
    Español: Tasa — Una razón que compara dos cantidades con unidades distintas, como millas por hora.
  • Unit rate — A rate for just 1 of something, like cost for 1 item.
    Español: Tasa unitaria — Una tasa para solo 1 de algo, como el precio de 1 artículo.
  • Per — For each one. Example: 5 dollars per book.
    Español: Por — Por cada uno. Ejemplo: 5 dólares por libro.
  • Problem solving — Using ratios and rates to find a missing amount.
    Español: Resolución de problemas — Usar razones y tasas para encontrar una cantidad que falta.
  • Proportion — A math sentence saying two ratios are equal. It helps find a missing number.
    Español: Proporción — Una oración matemática que dice que dos razones son iguales. Ayuda a hallar un número que falta.

Example

  1. Team 1 knows that 3 pounds of chicken cost $18.
  2. To find the unit rate, I divide the cost by the pounds: 18 divided by 3 = $6 per pound.
  3. Now I can find any amount. For 5 pounds, I multiply: 6 x 5 = $30.
  4. For 8 pounds, I multiply: 6 x 8 = $48.
  5. The unit rate $6 per pound is the shortcut for every amount.

Practice at home

  1. A printer prints 30 pages in 5 minutes. At this rate, how many pages will it print in 12 minutes?

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
