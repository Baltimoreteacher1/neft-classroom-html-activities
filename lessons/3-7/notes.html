Rate

A ratio that compares two quantities with different units

$12 ÷ 4 = $3 per 1 pound

Unit rate

A rate where the second quantity is exactly 1 unit

60 miles per hour, $5 per ticket

Per

For each or for every one; used to describe unit rates

My Notes

Guided Examples

Example 1

A bakery makes 24 cookies in 3 batches. What is the unit rate of cookies per batch?

Solution: Unit rate: 24 cookies ÷ 3 batches = 8 cookies per batch.

Answer: A. 8 cookies per batch

Example 2

Apples cost $5 for 4 pounds. What is the cost per pound?

Solution: Unit rate: $5 ÷ 4 pounds = $1.25 per pound.

Answer: A. $1.25

Example 3

A cyclist rides 36 miles in 3 hours. At the same rate, how far will she ride in 5 hours?

Solution: Unit rate: 36 ÷ 3 = 12 miles per hour. In 5 hours: 12 × 5 = 60 miles.

Answer: A. 60 miles

Answer Key

  1. Try It 1: A. The car — Car: 150÷3 = 50 mph. Bus: 200÷5 = 40 mph. The car travels faster at 50 mph vs. 40 mph.
  2. Try It 2: Factory A: 240 ÷ 8 = 30 widgets/hour. Factory B: 200 ÷ 5 = 40 widgets/hour. Factory B is faster. Together: (30 + 40) × 10 = 70 × 10 = 700 widgets in 10 hours.
  3. Exit Ticket: A. 72 — Unit rate: 30 ÷ 5 = 6 pages per minute. In 12 minutes: 6 × 12 = 72 pages.
