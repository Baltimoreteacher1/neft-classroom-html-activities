Unit 3, Lesson 7: Ratio and Rate Problem Solving6.RP.3

Everything you need to teach this tomorrow. ~45 min.

Objectives

Content: I can solve real-world rate problems by finding and using unit rates.

Language: I can explain my solution using the words rate, unit rate, per, and proportion.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

A car drives 100 miles in 2 hours. What is the unit rate in miles per hour?

Teaching notes

Key idea: Find the unit rate by dividing, then multiply it to find the amount you need.

I do:

  1. Team 1 knows that 3 pounds of chicken cost $18.
  2. To find the unit rate, I divide the cost by the pounds: 18 divided by 3 = $6 per pound.
  3. Now I can find any amount. For 5 pounds, I multiply: 6 x 5 = $30.
  4. For 8 pounds, I multiply: 6 x 8 = $48.
  5. The unit rate $6 per pound is the shortcut for every amount.

You do: Next you will complete the chicken cost table and find the unit rate. Divide to find the cost of 1 pound, then use it for other amounts.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Ratio and Rate Problem Solving is skipping the key idea: "Find the unit rate by dividing, then multiply it to find the amount you need." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Rate, Unit rate, Per, Problem solving, Proportion (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A printer prints 30 pages in 5 minutes. At this rate, how many pages will it print in 12 minutes?

  • 72 ✓ correct
  • 60
  • 36
  • 42

Unit rate: 30 ÷ 5 = 6 pages per minute. In 12 minutes: 6 × 12 = 72 pages.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

A runner trains for a 5K race. During practice, she runs 3 miles in 27 minutes. She wants to know her pace (minutes per mile) and predict how long the full 5K (3.1 miles) will take at the same rate.
