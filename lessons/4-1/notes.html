Rates and Unit Rates

Lesson 4-1

Name:Date:Class:

Vocabulary

Rate

A ratio that compares two quantities with different units

$12 for 4 games is a rate

Unit Rate

A rate simplified so the second quantity is 1

$3 per 1 game

Per

For each; used to describe a unit rate

60 miles per hour means 60 miles for every 1 hour

Ratio

A comparison of two quantities by division

5 to 3 or 5:3

Better Buy

The option with the lower unit rate (lower cost per unit)

Pack A: $0.50 per pencil vs Pack B: $0.40 per pencil — Pack B is the better buy

Divide

The operation used to find a unit rate from a total and a count

$12 ÷ 4 games = $3 per game

Key Ideas & Notes

  • You're comparing ticket prices at different game booths in the arcade.
  • Booth A charges $3 for 5 games, and Booth B charges $5 for 8 games.
  • Which booth gives you a better deal?
  • To find out, you need to figure out the cost per game — that's the unit rate!
  • Calculate the unit rate (cost per game) for each arcade booth. Divide the total cost by the number of games.

Think About It

  • What two quantities are being compared at each booth?
  • Which booth seems like a better deal at first glance?
  • How can you compare prices when the number of games is different?

My Notes

Guided Examples

Example 1

A booth charges $4.50 for 9 games. What is the unit rate?

Solution: $4.50 ÷ 9 = $0.50 per game.

Answer: A. $0.50 per game

Example 2

A car travels 180 miles using 6 gallons of gas. What is the unit rate in miles per gallon?

Solution: 180 miles ÷ 6 gallons = 30 miles per gallon.

Answer: A. 30 miles per gallon

Example 3

A pack of 8 markers costs $6.00. What is the cost per marker?

Solution: $6.00 ÷ 8 = $0.75 per marker.

Answer: A. $0.75

Answer Key

  2. Try It 2: A. Runner B — Runner A: 4÷12 ≈ 0.33 laps/min. Runner B: 5÷14 ≈ 0.36 laps/min. Runner B runs slightly more laps per minute.
  3. Exit Ticket: A. 6 for $1.50 — $0.25 each — $1.50 ÷ 6 = $0.25 per pencil. $2.80 ÷ 10 = $0.28 per pencil. $0.25 < $0.28, so 6 for $1.50 is the better buy.
