Unit 2, Lesson 2: Divide Whole Numbers by Fractions6.NS.1

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

To divide a whole number by a fraction, you can use Keep, Change, Flip: keep the first number, change divide to multiply, and flip the fraction upside down (its reciprocal). Then multiply.

Key idea: Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal (the fraction flipped over).

Key words

  • Whole Number — A counting number with no fraction or decimal, like 0, 1, 2, 3.
    Español: Número entero — Un número de contar sin fracción ni decimal, como 0, 1, 2, 3.
  • Fraction — A number that shows part of a whole, like 3/4.
    Español: Fracción — Un número que muestra una parte de un todo, como 3/4.
  • Reciprocal — A fraction turned upside down.
    Español: Recíproco — Una fracción volteada de arriba abajo.
  • Keep, Change, Flip — A way to divide fractions: keep the first, change ÷ to ×, flip the second.
    Español: Mantener, cambiar, invertir — Una manera de dividir fracciones: deja la primera, cambia ÷ por ×, voltea la segunda.
  • Quotient — The answer when you divide.
    Español: Cociente — La respuesta cuando divides.

Example

  1. Agent Park has 4 pounds of powder and each bag holds 1/2 pound. I want 4 ÷ 1/2.
  2. Keep the 4. Change ÷ to ×. Flip 1/2 to make 2/1, which is just 2.
  3. Now I multiply: 4 × 2 = 8.
  4. So 4 ÷ 1/2 = 8. He can fill 8 bags. That makes sense: each pound fills 2 bags, and 4 pounds × 2 = 8.

Practice at home

Match each division expression to its quotient.

  1. 4 ÷ 1/3
  2. 7 ÷ 1/2
  3. 5 ÷ 1/6

Answer check

  1. 4 ÷ 1/3 → 12
  2. 7 ÷ 1/2 → 14
  3. 5 ÷ 1/6 → 30

Common mistake

A common mistake in Divide Whole Numbers by Fractions is skipping the key idea: "Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal (the fraction flipped over)." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
