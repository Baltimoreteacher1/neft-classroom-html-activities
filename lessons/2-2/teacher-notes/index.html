Language: I can explain my steps using the words fraction, reciprocal, quotient, and Keep, Change, Flip.

Warm-up

Let's try 3 ÷ 1/4 together. What do we keep? The 3.

Teaching notes

Key idea: Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal (the fraction flipped over).

I do:

  1. Agent Park has 4 pounds of powder and each bag holds 1/2 pound. I want 4 ÷ 1/2.
  2. Keep the 4. Change ÷ to ×. Flip 1/2 to make 2/1, which is just 2.
  3. Now I multiply: 4 × 2 = 8.
  4. So 4 ÷ 1/2 = 8. He can fill 8 bags. That makes sense: each pound fills 2 bags, and 4 pounds × 2 = 8.

You do: Now use the number line to show how many 1/2-jumps fit into 4, then try the practice problems with Keep, Change, Flip.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Divide Whole Numbers by Fractions is skipping the key idea: "Dividing by a fraction is the same as multiplying by its reciprocal (the fraction flipped over)." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Whole Number, Fraction, Reciprocal, Keep, Change, Flip, Quotient (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

What is 9 ÷ 1/3?

  • 27 ✓ correct
  • 3
  • 9/3
  • 1/27

9 ÷ 1/3 = 9 × 3/1 = 27. There are 27 thirds in 9 wholes.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension
