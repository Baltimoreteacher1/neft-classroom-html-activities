Language: I can explain my work using the words coordinate plane, ordered pair, origin, and quadrant.

Warm-up

Now let's plot (2, 1). Where do we start? At the origin (0, 0).

Teaching notes

Key idea: Always start at the origin (0, 0), move right/left first, then up/down.

I do:

  1. I will plot the point (5, 4). The 5 is the x-coordinate, and the 4 is the y-coordinate.
  2. I start at the origin, which is (0, 0).
  3. First I move 5 units to the right along the x-axis.
  4. Then I move 4 units up. I place my dot. That is the point (5, 4).

You do: In the explore, plot Captain Vega's waypoints (2, 1), (5, 4), and (8, 1) to trace the treasure route.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Graph on the Coordinate Plane is skipping the key idea: "Always start at the origin (0, 0), move right/left first, then up/down." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Coordinate plane, Ordered pair, Origin, Quadrant, Reflection, Integer (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

Point P is located 6 units right and 3 units up from the origin. What ordered pair names point P?

  • (6, 3) ✓ correct
  • (3, 6)
  • (6, 0)
  • (0, 3)

Right 6 = x-coordinate of 6, Up 3 = y-coordinate of 3. The ordered pair is (6, 3).

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

A drone delivery company programs flight paths using coordinate pairs. A drone starts at the warehouse (0, 0), flies to pickup at (3, 6), then delivers to (8, 2), then returns to base (0, 0).
