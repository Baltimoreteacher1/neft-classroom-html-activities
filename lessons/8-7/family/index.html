Unit 8, Lesson 7: Shape of Data Distributions6.SP.2

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

The shape of a data distribution describes how the values are spread out. Data can be symmetric (the same on both sides), skewed (bunched on one side with a long tail on the other), or have clusters and gaps.

Key idea: Symmetric data mirrors around the center; skewed data has a long tail, and the skew is named for the direction the tail points.

Key words

  • Symmetric — Data that looks about the same on the left and right.
    Español: Simétrico — Datos que se ven casi iguales a la izquierda y a la derecha.
  • Skewed — Data bunched on one side with a tail on the other.
    Español: Sesgado — Datos amontonados de un lado con una cola del otro.
  • Cluster — A group of numbers that are close together.
    Español: Agrupamiento — Un grupo de números que están cerca unos de otros.
  • Gap — A big empty space where there is no data.
    Español: Hueco (espacio) — Un espacio grande y vacío donde no hay datos.
  • Data distribution — How the data looks: where it sits and how spread out it is.
    Español: Distribución de datos — Cómo se ven los datos: dónde están y qué tan separados están.
  • Variability — How spread out the numbers are.
    Español: Variabilidad — Qué tan separados están los números.

Example

  1. Example 1, bar heights from left to right: 2, 5, 8, 5, 2. The bars rise to a peak in the middle and fall evenly on both sides.
  2. Both sides look like mirror images, so this shape is symmetric.
  3. Example 2, bar heights: 9, 6, 3, 1. The tallest bars are on the left and the bars get shorter going right.
  4. The long tail points to the right, so this shape is skewed right. I name the skew after the tail, not the peak.

Practice at home

Match each distribution shape to how its mean and median compare.

  1. Symmetric
  2. Skewed Right
  3. Skewed Left

Answer check

  1. Symmetric → Mean ≈ Median — use either (mean is fine)
  2. Skewed Right → Mean > Median — use the median (high tail pulls the mean up)
  3. Skewed Left → Mean < Median — use the median (low tail pulls the mean down)

Common mistake

A common mistake in Shape of Data Distributions is skipping the key idea: "Symmetric data mirrors around the center; skewed data has a long tail, and the skew is named for the direction the tail points." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.
