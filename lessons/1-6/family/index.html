Unit 1, Lesson 6: Multiply Decimals6.NS.3

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

To multiply decimals, you multiply the numbers as if there were no decimal points. Then you count the decimal places and put the point back in the answer.

Key idea: Multiply like whole numbers, then the product has the SAME total of decimal places as both factors combined.

Key words

  • Product — The answer when you multiply.
    Español: Producto — La respuesta cuando multiplicas.
  • Decimal point — The dot that splits the whole number from the part after it.
    Español: Punto decimal — El punto que separa el número entero de la parte que sigue.
  • Estimate — A close answer you get by rounding first.
    Español: Estimar — Una respuesta cercana que obtienes al redondear primero.
  • Place value — What a digit is worth based on where it sits in a number.
    Español: Valor posicional — Lo que vale un dígito según el lugar que ocupa en el número.
  • Decimal places — How many digits come after the decimal point.
    Español: Cifras decimales — Cuántos dígitos hay después del punto decimal.

Example

  1. I want 4.5 × 12.60, the cost of 4.5 meters at $12.60 each.
  2. First I multiply without points, like whole numbers: 45 × 1260 = 56,700.
  3. Now I count decimal places: 4.5 has 1, and 12.60 has 2, so 1 + 2 = 3 decimal places.
  4. I put the point 3 places from the right in 56,700: that gives 56.700, or $56.70.
  5. I check with an estimate: 5 × $13 = $65, and $56.70 is close, so my answer is reasonable.

Practice at home

Match each expression to its product.

  1. 2.5 × 4
  2. 1.2 × 0.3
  3. 3.6 × 0.5

Answer check

  1. 2.5 × 4 → 10
  2. 1.2 × 0.3 → 0.36
  3. 3.6 × 0.5 → 1.8

Common mistake

A common mistake in Multiply Decimals is skipping the key idea: "Multiply like whole numbers, then the product has the SAME total of decimal places as both factors combined." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
