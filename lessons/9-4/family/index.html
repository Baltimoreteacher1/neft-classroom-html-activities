Unit 9, Lesson 4: Rational Numbers on the Number Line6.NS.6

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A rational number is any number you can write as a fraction. This includes fractions, decimals, and integers. Many rational numbers fall between two whole numbers.

Key idea: To plot a rational number, find the two whole numbers it falls between, then split the space into equal parts.

Key words

  • Rational number — Any number you can write as a fraction. This includes fractions, decimals, and integers.
    Español: Número racional — Cualquier número que puedes escribir como fracción. Incluye fracciones, decimales y enteros.
  • Fraction — A number that shows part of a whole, like 3/4.
    Español: Fracción — Un número que muestra una parte de un todo, como 3/4.
  • Decimal — A number with a dot, like 0.5, that shows a part less than one.
    Español: Decimal — Un número con un punto, como 0.5, que muestra una parte menor que uno.
  • Number line — A straight line with numbers spaced out evenly.
    Español: Recta numérica — Una línea recta con números separados de forma pareja.
  • Equivalent — Having the same value, just written a different way.
    Español: Equivalente — Tener el mismo valor, escrito de otra forma.
  • Integer — Whole numbers and their opposites, like -2, -1, 0, 1, 2.
    Español: Número entero — Números enteros y sus opuestos, como -2, -1, 0, 1, 2.

Example

  1. I will plot -2.5 on the number line.
  2. First, the whole numbers it falls between: -2.5 is between -2 and -3.
  3. The .5 means halfway, so I split the space between -2 and -3 in half.
  4. I place my dot exactly halfway between -2 and -3. That is -2.5.

Practice at home

  1. Which rational number is located between -2 and -3 on the number line?

Answer check

Common mistake

A common mistake in Rational Numbers on the Number Line is skipping the key idea: "To plot a rational number, find the two whole numbers it falls between, then split the space into equal parts." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
