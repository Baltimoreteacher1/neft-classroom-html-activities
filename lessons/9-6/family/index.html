Unit 9, Lesson 6: Distance on the Coordinate Plane6.NS.8

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Distance is how far apart two points are, and it is never negative. When two points share a row or a column, you can find the distance using their coordinates.

Key idea: If points are on the same side of zero, subtract; if they are on opposite sides of zero, add the absolute values.

Key words

  • Distance — How far apart two points are. It is never negative.
    Español: Distancia — Qué tan separados están dos puntos. Nunca es negativo.
  • Absolute value — How far a number is from zero.
    Español: Valor absoluto — Qué tan lejos está un número de cero.
  • Horizontal distance — How far apart two points are going left or right.
    Español: Distancia horizontal — Qué tan separados están dos puntos yendo a la izquierda o a la derecha.
  • Vertical distance — How far apart two points are going up or down.
    Español: Distancia vertical — Qué tan separados están dos puntos yendo hacia arriba o hacia abajo.
  • Coordinate plane — A grid with a line going across and a line going up that cross.
    Español: Plano cartesiano — Una cuadrícula con una línea horizontal y una vertical que se cruzan.
  • Integer — Whole numbers and their opposites, like -2, -1, 0, 1, 2.
    Español: Número entero — Números enteros y sus opuestos, como -2, -1, 0, 1, 2.

Example

  1. I will find the distance from (-3, 2) to (4, 2). Both have y = 2, so they sit on the same horizontal line.
  2. The points are on opposite sides of zero, so I add the absolute values.
  3. |-3| = 3 and |4| = 4.
  4. I add: 3 + 4 = 7. The distance between them is 7 units.

Practice at home

Common mistake

A common mistake in Distance on the Coordinate Plane is skipping the key idea: "If points are on the same side of zero, subtract; if they are on opposite sides of zero, add the absolute values." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
