Unit 6, Lesson 6: Equivalent Expressions6.EE.4

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Two expressions are equivalent when they always give the same value, no matter what number you put in for the variable. They can look different but mean the same thing.

Key idea: Equivalent expressions give the same answer for every value of the variable.

Key words

  • Equivalent — Expressions that always have the same value.
    Español: Equivalente — Expresiones que siempre tienen el mismo valor.
  • Simplify — To write an expression in a shorter, simpler way.
    Español: Simplificar — Escribir una expresión de forma más corta y simple.
  • Like Terms — Terms with the same letter, like 2x and 5x.
    Español: Términos semejantes — Términos con la misma letra, como 2x y 5x.
  • Combine — To add or subtract terms with the same letter.
    Español: Combinar — Sumar o restar términos con la misma letra.
  • Coefficient — The number in front of a letter, like the 3 in 3x.
    Español: Coeficiente — El número frente a una letra, como el 3 en 3x.

Example

  1. Producer A wrote 3x + 2x + 10 and Producer B wrote 5x + 10. Are they equivalent?
  2. I test x = 4. For A: 3(4) + 2(4) + 10 = 12 + 8 + 10 = 30.
  3. For B: 5(4) + 10 = 20 + 10 = 30.
  4. Both give 30, and 3x + 2x is just 5x, so they are equivalent.

Practice at home

Match each expression to its simplified equivalent.

  1. 6x + 2x
  2. 3(n + 5)
  3. 4a + 7 + a

Answer check

  1. 6x + 2x → 8x
  2. 3(n + 5) → 3n + 15
  3. 4a + 7 + a → 5a + 7

Common mistake

A common mistake in Equivalent Expressions is skipping the key idea: "Equivalent expressions give the same answer for every value of the variable." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
