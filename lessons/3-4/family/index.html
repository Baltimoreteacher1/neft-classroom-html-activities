Unit 3, Lesson 4: Equivalent Ratios6.RP.3

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Equivalent ratios are ratios that mean the same thing. You make one by multiplying or dividing BOTH numbers by the same amount.

Key idea: Multiply or divide both numbers by the same amount to make an equivalent ratio.

Key words

  • Ratio — A way to compare two amounts.
    Español: Razón — Una manera de comparar dos cantidades.
  • Equivalent ratios — Two ratios that mean the same thing.
    Español: Razones equivalentes — Dos razones que significan lo mismo.
  • Rate — A ratio comparing two amounts with different units, like miles per hour.
    Español: Tasa — Una razón que compara dos cantidades con unidades distintas, como millas por hora.
  • Proportion — A math sentence saying two ratios are equal.
    Español: Proporción — Una oración matemática que dice que dos razones son iguales.
  • Simplify — To make a ratio smaller while keeping the same comparison.
    Español: Simplificar — Hacer una razón más pequeña sin cambiar la comparación.

Example

  1. Chef Montoya's soup serves 8 people, but she needs to serve 32.
  2. I find the scale factor: 32 divided by 8 = 4.
  3. The recipe uses 3 cups of tomatoes to 2 cups of broth, a 3:2 ratio.
  4. I multiply both by 4: 3 x 4 = 12 cups of tomatoes, and 2 x 4 = 8 cups of broth.
  5. So 3:2 and 12:8 are equivalent ratios, and the flavor stays the same.

Practice at home

  1. A store sells 5 notebooks for $8. At this rate, how much would 15 notebooks cost?

Common mistake

A common mistake in Equivalent Ratios is skipping the key idea: "Multiply or divide both numbers by the same amount to make an equivalent ratio." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
