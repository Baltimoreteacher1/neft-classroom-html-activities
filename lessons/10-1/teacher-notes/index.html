Objectives

Language: I can explain my work using the words volume, rectangular prism, cubic units, and edge.

Warm-up

Now a smaller box: 5 long, 2 wide, 2 tall. What formula do we use?

Teaching notes

Key idea: To find the volume of a box, multiply all three edges: V = length × width × height.

I do:

  1. My time capsule box is 6 long, 4 wide, and 3 tall. I write the formula: V = length × width × height.
  2. I put in the numbers: V = 6 × 4 × 3.
  3. First I multiply two of them: 6 × 4 = 24.
  4. Then I multiply by the last one: 24 × 3 = 72.
  5. So the volume is 72 cubic units. I use cubic units because volume fills space in 3 directions.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Volume with Whole Number Edges is skipping the key idea: "To find the volume of a box, multiply all three edges: V = length × width × height." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Volume, Rectangular prism, Cubic units, Length, width, height, Net, Edge (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A rectangular prism has l = 11 in, w = 5 in, h = 4 in. What is the volume?

  • 220 in³ ✓ correct
  • 55 in³
  • 220 in²
  • 200 in³

V = 11 × 5 × 4 = 220 cubic inches. Remember: volume uses cubic units (in³).

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
