Warm-up

Solve x − 5 ≤ 8. What is the inverse of subtracting 5?

Teaching notes

Key idea: Solve with inverse operations, then graph the solution set: open circle for < or >, closed for ≤ or ≥.

I do:

  1. Solve x + 3 > 53. The inverse of adding 3 is subtracting 3.
  2. I subtract 3 from both sides: x + 3 − 3 > 53 − 3, so x > 50.
  3. The symbol > does not include 50, so I use an open circle at 50.
  4. 'Greater than' shades right. Check: x = 60 gives 60 + 3 = 63 > 53. True!

You do: Solve and graph x + 4 > 10, x − 5 ≤ 8, and x + 7 ≥ 15 on the number line. Then finish the practice problems and test a value to check.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Solve and Graph Inequalities is skipping the key idea: "Solve with inverse operations, then graph the solution set: open circle for < or >, closed for ≤ or ≥." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Solve, Graph, Solution, Substitute, Solution set, Inverse operation (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

Solve and describe the graph: x + 5 < 11

  • x < 6; open circle at 6, shade left ✓ correct
  • x < 16; open circle at 16, shade left
  • x ≤ 6; closed circle at 6, shade left
  • x > 6; open circle at 6, shade right

Subtract 5 from both sides: x < 11 − 5 = 6. Open circle at 6 (not included), shade left. ✓

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

A detective needs at least $75 for a surveillance camera. She has already saved $48. The inequality 48 + s ≥ 75 represents how much more money s she needs.
