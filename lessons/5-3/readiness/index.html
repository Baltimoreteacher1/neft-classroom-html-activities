Learn It

A triangle is half a rectangle

Take any rectangle and cut it along a diagonal — you get two equal triangles. So a triangle's area is base × height, then divided by 2 (take half). First find base × height like a rectangle, then halve it.

Rectangle 6 × 4 = 24. A triangle with the same base and height has area = half of 24 = 12 square units.
The height is straight up from the base (perpendicular), not the slanted side of the triangle.

Hint

Level 1 Do base × height, then halve.

A. Multiply base × height: 8 × 3 = ___?

Hint

8 × 3 = 24.

B. A triangle is half of that. Half of 24 = ___?

Hint

24 ÷ 2 = 12 square units.

C. A triangle has a slanted side of 7 and a straight-up height of 5. Which number is the height?

Hint

Height is perpendicular (straight up), so it is 5.

Level 2 Stretch into full triangle area.

A. A triangle has base 10 and height 6. Find base × height ÷ 2: ___?

Hint

10 × 6 = 60, then half of 60 = 30 square units.

B. A triangle has base 9 and height 4. What is its area?

Hint

9 × 4 = 36, then half of 36 = 18 square units.
