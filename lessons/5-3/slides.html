LESSON 5-3 · OBJECTIVES

🎯 Lesson Objectives

Content Objective:

I can find the area of a triangle using the formula A = ½ × base × height.

Language Objective:

I can explain my steps using the words base, height, area, and perpendicular.

📋 Scenario Launch

The triangular garden bed has a base of 12 feet and a height of 8 feet. Which measurement is the height, and how is it different from the slanted side?

Word Bank:
baseheightperpendicularareaformula

👀 Things I Notice:

🔹 The height is the ___ distance from the base to the top corner.
🔹 It is different from the slanted side because ___.

💭 Things I Wonder:

🔹 It ___ matter which side is the base because ___.
🔹 No matter which base I choose, the area is the same since ___.

📖 Guided Practice

On the grid your triangle (base 12, height 8) fits inside a 12 by 8 rectangle. Why do we divide by 2 to find the triangle's area?

✍️ TWR Sentence Expansion

🔹 A triangle is half of a ___ with the same base and height.
🔹 So the area is 1/2 times 12 times 8, which equals ___.
🔹 The rectangle's area is ___, so the triangle is ___.
