Unit 5, Lesson 3: Area of Triangles6.G.1

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A triangle has three sides. The base is one side, and the height is the straight-up (perpendicular) distance from the base to the top corner.

Key idea: Area of a triangle = ½ × base × height. A triangle is exactly half of a rectangle with the same base and height.

Key words

  • Base — A side of the triangle you use to find the area.
    Español: Base — Un lado del triángulo que usas para hallar el área.
  • Height — The straight-up distance from the base to the top corner.
    Español: Altura — La distancia recta desde la base hasta la esquina de arriba.
  • Area — How much space is inside a flat shape.
    Español: Área — Cuánto espacio hay dentro de una figura plana.
  • Perpendicular — Two lines that meet to make a square corner (90 degrees).
    Español: Perpendicular — Dos líneas que se unen formando una esquina recta (90 grados).
  • Composite figure — A shape made by putting two or more simple shapes together.
    Español: Figura compuesta — Una figura formada al juntar dos o más figuras simples.
  • Formula — A math rule written with symbols.
    Español: Fórmula — Una regla matemática escrita con símbolos.

Example

  1. My triangular garden has a base of 12 feet and a height of 8 feet.
  2. I write the formula: A = ½ × base × height.
  3. I put in the numbers: A = ½ × 12 × 8.
  4. I multiply base × height first: 12 × 8 = 96.
  5. I take half: ½ × 96 = 48. So the area is 48 square feet.

Practice at home

Match each shape to its area.

  1. Triangle b=8, h=6
  2. Triangle b=10, h=8
  3. Triangle b=12, h=5

Answer check

  1. Triangle b=8, h=6 → 24 sq units
  2. Triangle b=10, h=8 → 40 sq units
  3. Triangle b=12, h=5 → 30 sq units

Common mistake

A common mistake in Area of Triangles is skipping the key idea: "Area of a triangle = ½ × base × height. A triangle is exactly half of a rectangle with the same base and height." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
