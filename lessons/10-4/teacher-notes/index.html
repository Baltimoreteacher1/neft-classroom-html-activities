Warm-up

Now a rectangular prism: 5 long, 4 wide, 3 tall. How many faces does it have?

Teaching notes

Key idea: No matter the prism, find the area of every face and add them all together.

I do:

  1. My triangular prism has triangle bases (base 6, height 4), is 10 long, and the two slant sides are 5 each.
  2. Each triangle base is ½ × 6 × 4 = 12, and there are 2 of them: 2 × 12 = 24.
  3. Now the 3 rectangles: 6 × 10 = 60, 5 × 10 = 50, and 5 × 10 = 50, which add to 160.
  4. I add the bases and the rectangles: 24 + 160 = 184.
  5. So the surface area is 184 in². I counted 5 faces because it is a triangular prism.

You do: In the table below, find the total surface area of each capsule, whether it is rectangular or triangular. Add up every face and write your answer in square units.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Surface Area of Prisms is skipping the key idea: "No matter the prism, find the area of every face and add them all together." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Surface area, Rectangular prism, Triangular prism, Face, Net, Lateral face (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A rectangular prism has l = 8 ft, w = 6 ft, h = 3 ft. What is the surface area?

  • 180 ft² ✓ correct
  • 144 ft²
  • 180 ft³
  • 90 ft²

SA = 2(8×6) + 2(8×3) + 2(6×3) = 96 + 48 + 36 = 180 ft². Surface area uses square units (ft²).

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
