Unit 10, Lesson 4: Surface Area of Prisms6.G.4

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Surface area is the total area of all the faces of a solid. A rectangular prism has 6 faces; a triangular prism has 5 faces (2 triangles + 3 rectangles). Add every face to get the total.

Key idea: No matter the prism, find the area of every face and add them all together.

Key words

  • Surface area — The total area of all the flat sides of a solid.
    Español: Área de superficie — El área total de todos los lados planos de un sólido.
  • Rectangular prism — A solid box shape with six flat rectangle sides.
    Español: Prisma rectangular — Una figura sólida en forma de caja con seis lados rectangulares.
  • Triangular prism — A solid with two triangle ends and three flat rectangle sides.
    Español: Prisma triangular — Un sólido con dos extremos triangulares y tres lados rectangulares.
  • Face — One flat side of a solid shape.
    Español: Cara — Un lado plano de una figura sólida.
  • Net — A flat shape that folds up into a solid.
    Español: Plantilla (desarrollo plano) — Una figura plana que se dobla y forma un sólido.
  • Lateral face — The side faces of a solid, not the top or bottom.
    Español: Cara lateral — Los lados de un sólido, no la parte de arriba ni la de abajo.

Example

  1. My triangular prism has triangle bases (base 6, height 4), is 10 long, and the two slant sides are 5 each.
  2. Each triangle base is ½ × 6 × 4 = 12, and there are 2 of them: 2 × 12 = 24.
  3. Now the 3 rectangles: 6 × 10 = 60, 5 × 10 = 50, and 5 × 10 = 50, which add to 160.
  4. I add the bases and the rectangles: 24 + 160 = 184.
  5. So the surface area is 184 in². I counted 5 faces because it is a triangular prism.
