Unit 7, Lesson 3: Solve Multiplication and Division Equations6.EE.7

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

When a variable is multiplied or divided by a number, I use the inverse operation to get it alone. Division undoes multiplication, and multiplication undoes division.

Key idea: Divide to undo multiplying, and multiply to undo dividing — always on both sides.

Key words

  • Inverse operation — Two math actions that undo each other, like × and ÷.
    Español: Operación inversa — Dos operaciones que se deshacen entre sí, como × y ÷.
  • Multiply — To add equal groups again and again.
    Español: Multiplicar — Sumar grupos iguales una y otra vez.
  • Divide — To split into equal groups.
    Español: Dividir — Repartir en grupos iguales.
  • Isolate — To get the letter by itself on one side.
    Español: Despejar — Dejar la letra sola en un lado.
  • Coefficient — The number multiplied by a variable.
    Español: Coeficiente — El número que multiplica a una variable.
  • Solution — The value of the variable that makes the equation true.
    Español: Solución — El valor de la variable que hace verdadera la ecuación.

Example

  1. Solve 3x = 21. This means 3 times x equals 21.
  2. The inverse of multiplying by 3 is dividing by 3.
  3. I divide both sides by 3: 3x ÷ 3 = 21 ÷ 3.
  4. That leaves x = 7. Check: 3 × 7 = 21. True!

Practice at home

  1. Solve: w / 8 = 6

Answer check

  1. Solve: w / 8 = 6 → w = 48

Common mistake

A common mistake in Solve Multiplication and Division Equations is skipping the key idea: "Divide to undo multiplying, and multiply to undo dividing — always on both sides." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
