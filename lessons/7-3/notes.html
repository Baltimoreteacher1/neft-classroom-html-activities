Key Vocabulary Level 1 support

Picture first, then the word, then a plain-language meaning. Say each word out loud.

Multiplication and division are inverses: 3 × 7 = 21, so 21 ÷ 3 = 7

Inverse operation

Two math actions that undo each other, like × and ÷.

3 × 7 = 21 means 3 groups of 7 equals 21

Multiply

To add equal groups again and again.

36 ÷ 4 = 9 means 36 split into 4 equal groups gives 9 per group

Divide

To split into equal groups.

3x = 21 → divide both sides by 3 → x = 7 (x is alone)

Isolate

To get the letter by itself on one side.

In 6x = 18, the coefficient is 6; divide both sides by 6 to get x = 3.

Coefficient

The number multiplied by a variable.

x = 3 is the solution of 6x = 18 because 6 × 3 = 18.

Solution

The value of the variable that makes the equation true.

Answer Key & Teacher Guide

  1. We Try (notes): A. n = 24 — Multiply both sides by 3: n = 8 × 3 = 24. Check: 24 / 3 = 8 ✓
  2. You Try (notes): A. m = 7 — Divide both sides by 9: m = 63 ÷ 9 = 7. Check: 9 × 7 = 63 ✓
  3. Try It 1: A. n = 0.25 — n = 2 ÷ 8 = 0.25.
  4. Try It 2: A. t/5 = 12 — Total divided by 5 equals 12, so t = 60.
  5. Exit Ticket: A. w = 48 — Multiply both sides by 8: w = 6 × 8 = 48. Check: 48 / 8 = 6 ✓

Writing (TWR) — what to look for

  • Kernel sentence: A complete sentence needs a subject and a verb. Example: Solution is the value of the variable that makes the equation true.
  • Expansion: because gives a reason, but shows a contrast or exception, so shows a result. Answers vary; each must keep the kernel idea and add the correct kind of detail.
  • Sentence types: Statement ends with a period, question with "?", exclamation with "!", and a command starts with an action verb (a "bossy" verb).
