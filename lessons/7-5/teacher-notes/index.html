Unit 7, Lesson 5: Graph Inequalities6.EE.8

Everything you need to teach this tomorrow. ~45 min.

Objectives

Content: I can graph the solutions of an inequality on a number line.

Language: I can explain my graph using the words number line, open circle, closed circle, and solution set.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

Graph x < 5. What number is the boundary?

Teaching notes

Key idea: Closed circle for ≤ or ≥ (included), open circle for < or > (not included); shade right for greater, left for less.

I do:

  1. Graph t ≥ 2. The boundary number is 2.
  2. The symbol ≥ includes 2, so I draw a closed (filled) circle at 2.
  3. 'Greater than or equal to' means larger values work, so I shade to the right.
  4. Now the graph shows 2, 3, 4, and every value above 2.

You do: Graph x > 4, x ≤ 7, and x ≥ 2 by choosing the right circle and shading. Then complete the practice graphs on your own.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Graph Inequalities is skipping the key idea: "Closed circle for ≤ or ≥ (included), open circle for < or > (not included); shade right for greater, left for less." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Number line, Open circle, Closed circle, Solution set, Inequality, Boundary point (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

How would you graph the inequality x ≥ 9?

  • Closed circle at 9, shaded right ✓ correct
  • Open circle at 9, shaded right
  • Closed circle at 9, shaded left
  • Open circle at 9, shaded left

x ≥ 9 means 9 is included (closed circle) and values are 9 or greater (shaded right).

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

A detective determines that fewer than 12 people were at the scene. The inequality p < 12 represents the number of people p. She graphs this with an open circle at 12 and shading to the left.
