Vocabulary

Read each word, its meaning, and the example. These are the words you'll use in the lesson — learn them first, then practice.

✖️

factor

Level 1 A number that divides another number evenly with nothing left over.

Example: 4 divides 12 evenly, so it is a building number of 12.

🤝

common factor

Level 1 A number that divides two different numbers evenly.

Example: 3 divides both 6 and 9 evenly.

🏆

greatest common factor (GCF)

Level 1 The biggest number that divides two numbers evenly.

Example: For 8 and 12, the biggest shared one is 4.

Learn It

What is a factor?

A factor of a number divides it evenly (no remainder). To list factors, find every pair that multiplies to the number.

Factors of 12: walk up the pairs — 1×12, 2×6, 3×4. So the factors are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12.
A common factor of two numbers is a factor they share. The greatest common factor (GCF) is the biggest one they share — that's the lesson goal.

Practice · Your Level

Worksheet — Try It

Your Skills Check picks one for you, but you can switch any time:

Level 0 Let's list factors one step at a time.

A. Fill the missing partner: 1 × ___ = 6

Hint

What times 1 gives 6? 1 × 6 = 6.

B. Fill the missing partner: 2 × ___ = 6

Hint

2 × 3 = 6.

C. So the factors of 6 are 1, 2, 3, and ___?

Hint

Every number is a factor of itself: 6.

Level 1 List the factors, then spot the shared ones.

A. How many factors does 8 have? (1, 2, 4, 8)

Hint

Pairs: 1×8 and 2×4 → four factors: 1, 2, 4, 8.

B. Which number is a common factor of 8 and 12?

Hint

Factors of 8: 1,2,4,8. Factors of 12: 1,2,3,4,6,12. They share 1, 2, and 4.

Level 2 Warm-up straight into GCF.

A. Factors of 18 and 24 — what is the greatest factor they share?

Hint

18: 1,2,3,6,9,18. 24: 1,2,3,4,6,8,12,24. Shared: 1,2,3,6 → greatest is 6.

B. What is the GCF of 9 and 15?

Hint

9: 1,3,9. 15: 1,3,5,15. Greatest shared = 3.
