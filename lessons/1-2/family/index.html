Unit 1, Lesson 2: Greatest Common Factor6.NS.4

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A factor is a number that divides another number evenly, with nothing left over. The greatest common factor is the biggest factor that two numbers share.

Key idea: The GCF is the largest number that divides BOTH numbers with no remainder.

Key words

  • Factor — A number that divides another number with nothing left over.
    Español: Factor — Un número que divide a otro sin que sobre nada.
  • Greatest Common Factor — The biggest number that divides two or more numbers evenly.
    Español: Máximo común divisor — El número más grande que divide a dos o más números sin que sobre nada.
  • Common factor — A number that divides two or more numbers evenly.
    Español: Factor común — Un número que divide a dos o más números sin que sobre nada.
  • Divisible — A number you can divide evenly, with nothing left over.
    Español: Divisible — Un número que se puede dividir sin que sobre nada.
  • Prime factorization — Writing a number as prime numbers multiplied together. It helps you find the GCF.
    Español: Factorización prima — Escribir un número como números primos multiplicados. Ayuda a encontrar el MCD.

Example

  1. I want the GCF of 24 and 36. First I list the factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24.
  2. Next I list the factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36.
  3. Now I circle the factors in BOTH lists: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12. These are the common factors.
  4. The biggest one is 12, so the GCF of 24 and 36 is 12. I check: 24 ÷ 12 = 2 and 36 ÷ 12 = 3. Both are even, so 12 works.

Practice at home

Match each pair of numbers to their GCF.

  1. GCF(6, 9)
  2. GCF(12, 18)
  3. GCF(16, 24)

Answer check

  1. GCF(6, 9) → 3
  2. GCF(12, 18) → 6
  3. GCF(16, 24) → 8

Common mistake

A common mistake in Greatest Common Factor is skipping the key idea: "The GCF is the largest number that divides BOTH numbers with no remainder." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
