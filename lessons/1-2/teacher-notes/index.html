Unit 1, Lesson 2: Greatest Common Factor6.NS.4

Everything you need to teach this tomorrow. ~45 min.

Objectives

Content: I can find the greatest common factor (GCF) of two numbers by listing or comparing their factors.

Language: I can explain how I found the GCF using the words factor, common factor, divisible, and greatest common factor.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

Let's find the GCF of 8 and 12. What are the factors of 8? (1, 2, 4, 8.)

Teaching notes

Key idea: The GCF is the largest number that divides BOTH numbers with no remainder.

I do:

  1. I want the GCF of 24 and 36. First I list the factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24.
  2. Next I list the factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36.
  3. Now I circle the factors in BOTH lists: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12. These are the common factors.
  4. The biggest one is 12, so the GCF of 24 and 36 is 12. I check: 24 ÷ 12 = 2 and 36 ÷ 12 = 3. Both are even, so 12 works.

You do: Next, you will sort numbers to find the common factors of two numbers and choose the greatest one. Remember: pick the LARGEST factor the two numbers share.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Greatest Common Factor is skipping the key idea: "The GCF is the largest number that divides BOTH numbers with no remainder." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Factor, Greatest Common Factor, Common factor, Divisible, Prime factorization (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

What is the GCF of 18 and 27?

  • 9 ✓ correct
  • 3
  • 6
  • 18

Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18. Factors of 27: 1, 3, 9, 27. Common: 1, 3, 9. GCF = 9.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
