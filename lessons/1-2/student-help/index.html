Stuck? Start here. Read the steps, try the problem, then check your answer.

I can…

I can find the greatest common factor (GCF) of two numbers by listing or comparing their factors.

Key words

  • Factor — A number that divides another number with nothing left over.
  • Greatest Common Factor — The biggest number that divides two or more numbers evenly.
  • Common factor — A number that divides two or more numbers evenly.
  • Divisible — A number you can divide evenly, with nothing left over.
  • Prime factorization — Writing a number as prime numbers multiplied together. It helps you find the GCF.

Steps

  1. I want the GCF of 24 and 36. First I list the factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24.
  2. Next I list the factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36.
  3. Now I circle the factors in BOTH lists: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12. These are the common factors.
  4. The biggest one is 12, so the GCF of 24 and 36 is 12. I check: 24 ÷ 12 = 2 and 36 ÷ 12 = 3. Both are even, so 12 works.
