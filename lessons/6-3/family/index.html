Unit 6, Lesson 3: Write Algebraic Expressions6.EE.2a

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

An algebraic expression uses numbers, a variable (a letter for an unknown number), and operations. We read a word phrase and write it with math symbols.

Key idea: Words like "each" or "per" mean multiply, and "plus" or "more than" mean add.

Key words

  • Variable — A letter that stands for a number that is unknown or can change.
    Español: Variable — Una letra que representa un número desconocido o que puede cambiar.
  • Algebraic Expression — A math phrase that has at least one letter.
    Español: Expresión algebraica — Una frase matemática que tiene al menos una letra.
  • Coefficient — The number in front of a letter, like the 3 in 3x.
    Español: Coeficiente — El número frente a una letra, como el 3 en 3x.
  • Constant — A number on its own that does not change.
    Español: Constante — Un número solo que no cambia.
  • Like terms — Terms with the same letter, like 2x and 5x.
    Español: Términos semejantes — Términos con la misma letra, como 2x y 5x.

Example

  1. Tickets cost $12 each, plus a flat $50 venue fee. Let t be the number of tickets.
  2. "$12 each ticket" means 12 times t, which I write as 12t.
  3. The flat fee $50 is added once, so I add 50.
  4. I put them together: total revenue = 12t + 50.

Practice at home

Match each word phrase to its algebraic expression.

  1. the product of 5 and n
  2. 8 less than a number w
  3. a number p increased by 12

Answer check

  1. the product of 5 and n → 5n
  2. 8 less than a number w → w − 8
  3. a number p increased by 12 → p + 12

Common mistake

A common mistake in Write Algebraic Expressions is skipping the key idea: "Words like "each" or "per" mean multiply, and "plus" or "more than" mean add." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
