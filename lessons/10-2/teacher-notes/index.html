Warm-up

Now try a box that is 4 long, 2 wide, and 0.5 tall. What formula do we use?

Teaching notes

Key idea: A fractional edge does not change the rule: still multiply length × width × height.

I do:

  1. My capsule is 2 ft long, 1.5 ft wide, and 1 ft tall. I write V = length × width × height.
  2. I put in the numbers: V = 2 × 1.5 × 1.
  3. First I multiply 2 × 1.5 = 3.
  4. Then 3 × 1 = 3.
  5. So the volume is 3 ft³. A fractional edge like 1.5 did not change my steps at all.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Volume of Rectangular Prisms is skipping the key idea: "A fractional edge does not change the rule: still multiply length × width × height." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Volume, Rectangular prism, Cubic units, Dimensions, Net, Base area (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A rectangular prism has l = 7 ft, w = 2 ft, h = 3 ft. What is the volume?

  • 42 ft³ ✓ correct
  • 24 ft³
  • 42 ft²
  • 12 ft³

V = 7 × 2 × 3 = 42 cubic feet. Remember: volume uses cubic units (ft³).

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
