Unit 10, Lesson 2: Volume of Rectangular Prisms6.G.2

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Volume is the space inside a box. The rule V = length × width × height stays the same even when one edge is a fraction or decimal. You can also use volume = base area × height.

Key idea: A fractional edge does not change the rule: still multiply length × width × height.

Key words

  • Volume — How much space is inside a solid shape.
    Español: Volumen — Cuánto espacio hay dentro de una figura sólida.
  • Rectangular prism — A solid box shape with six flat rectangle sides.
    Español: Prisma rectangular — Una figura sólida en forma de caja con seis lados rectangulares.
  • Cubic units — The units used to measure space inside, like cubic inches.
    Español: Unidades cúbicas — Las unidades para medir el espacio interior, como pulgadas cúbicas.
  • Dimensions — How long, how wide, and how tall a shape is.
    Español: Dimensiones — Qué tan largo, ancho y alto es una figura.
  • Net — A flat shape that folds up into a solid.
    Español: Plantilla (desarrollo plano) — Una figura plana que se dobla y forma un sólido.
  • Base area — The area of the bottom of a solid. Volume = base area × height.
    Español: Área de la base — El área del fondo de un sólido. Volumen = área de la base × altura.

Example

  1. My capsule is 2 ft long, 1.5 ft wide, and 1 ft tall. I write V = length × width × height.
  2. I put in the numbers: V = 2 × 1.5 × 1.
  3. First I multiply 2 × 1.5 = 3.
  4. Then 3 × 1 = 3.
  5. So the volume is 3 ft³. A fractional edge like 1.5 did not change my steps at all.
