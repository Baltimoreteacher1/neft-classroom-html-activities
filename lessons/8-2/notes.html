Mean, Median, and Mode

Lesson 8-2

Name:Date:Class:

Key Vocabulary Level 1 support

Picture first, then the word, then a plain-language meaning. Say each word out loud.

Mean of 2, 4, 6: add them (2+4+6=12), divide by 3 → mean = 4

Mean

The sum of all values divided by the number of values (average)

Data: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9 → the middle (3rd) value is 5, so median = 5

Median

The middle value when data is ordered from least to greatest

Data: 2, 3, 3, 5, 7 → 3 appears twice (most often), so mode = 3

Mode

The value that appears most often in a data set

Data: 10, 12, 11, 13, 50 → 50 is far from the others, so 50 is an outlier

Outlier

A data value that is much greater or less than most other values

If most values cluster in the center with fewer at the ends, the distribution is symmetric

Data distribution

The shape and spread of a data set

8, 9, 10, 11 has low variability (close together); 2, 10, 25, 40 has high variability (far apart)

Variability

How spread out the values in a data set are

My Notes

Guided Examples

Example 1

Find the mean of: 10, 14, 8, 12, 16

Solution: Sum = 10 + 14 + 8 + 12 + 16 = 60. Mean = 60 ÷ 5 = 12.

Answer: A. 12

Example 2

Find the median of: 3, 7, 2, 9, 5

Solution: Ordered: 2, 3, 5, 7, 9. The middle value is 5.

Answer: A. 5

Example 3

Find the mode of: 4, 7, 4, 9, 2, 4, 8

Solution: 4 appears three times — more than any other value. Mode = 4.

Answer: A. 4

Answer Key

  1. Try It 1: A. 10 — Sum = 6 + 8 + 10 + 12 + 14 = 50. Mean = 50 ÷ 5 = 10.
  2. Try It 2: A. 7 — With 6 values, the median is the average of the 3rd and 4th values: (6 + 8) ÷ 2 = 7.
  3. Exit Ticket: A. 7 — Ordered: 4, 7, 7, 10, 12. The middle (3rd) value is 7.
