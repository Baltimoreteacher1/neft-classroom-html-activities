💡 What are mean, median, and mode?

Mean, median, and mode are three ways to find the center, or "typical" value, of a data set. The mean is the average, the median is the middle, and the mode is the most common value.

Key Idea:

Mean = add then divide, median = the middle of the ordered numbers, mode = the value that shows up most.

MEAN

Media

Click to flip ➔

The average. Add all the numbers, then divide by how many there are.

El promedio. Suma todos los números y divide entre cuántos hay.

Ex: Mean of 2, 4, 6: add them (2+4+6=12), divide by 3 → mean = 4
📝 The average found by adding all values and dividing by how many there are is the ___.

MEDIAN

Mediana

Click to flip ➔

The middle number when you put them in order.

El número del medio cuando los pones en orden.

Ex: Data: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9 → the middle (3rd) value is 5, so median = 5
📝 The middle value of a data set when the numbers are in order is the ___.

MODE

Moda

Click to flip ➔

The number that shows up most often.

El número que aparece más veces.

Ex: Data: 2, 3, 3, 5, 7 → 3 appears twice (most often), so mode = 3
📝 The value that appears most often in a data set is the ___.

OUTLIER

Valor atípico

Click to flip ➔

A number that is much bigger or smaller than the rest.

Un número mucho mayor o menor que los demás.

Ex: Data: 10, 12, 11, 13, 50 → 50 is far from the others, so 50 is an outlier
📝 A value that is much larger or smaller than the rest of the data is an ___.
