Unit 8, Lesson 2: Mean, Median, and Mode6.SP.3

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Mean, median, and mode are three ways to find the center, or "typical" value, of a data set. The mean is the average, the median is the middle, and the mode is the most common value.

Key idea: Mean = add then divide, median = the middle of the ordered numbers, mode = the value that shows up most.

Key words

  • Mean — The average. Add all the numbers, then divide by how many there are.
    Español: Media — El promedio. Suma todos los números y divide entre cuántos hay.
  • Median — The middle number when you put them in order.
    Español: Mediana — El número del medio cuando los pones en orden.
  • Mode — The number that shows up most often.
    Español: Moda — El número que aparece más veces.
  • Outlier — A number that is much bigger or smaller than the rest.
    Español: Valor atípico — Un número mucho mayor o menor que los demás.
  • Data distribution — How the data looks: where it sits and how spread out it is.
    Español: Distribución de datos — Cómo se ven los datos: dónde están y qué tan separados están.
  • Variability — How spread out the numbers are.
    Español: Variabilidad — Qué tan separados están los números.

Example

  1. My small data set: 2, 4, 4, 6, 9. There are 5 numbers.
  2. Mean: add them, 2 + 4 + 4 + 6 + 9 = 25, then divide by 5. So the mean = 25 ÷ 5 = 5.
  3. Median: the numbers are already in order. The middle (3rd) value is 4, so the median = 4.
  4. Mode: the number 4 appears twice, more than any other, so the mode = 4.

Answer check

  1. Mean → Sum ÷ Count
  2. Median → Middle value
  3. Mode → Most frequent

Common mistake

A common mistake in Mean, Median, and Mode is skipping the key idea: "Mean = add then divide, median = the middle of the ordered numbers, mode = the value that shows up most." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.
