Objectives & Targets

Content Objective:

I can write and describe a ratio that compares two quantities.

Language Objective:

I can explain a comparison using the words ratio, part-to-part, part-to-whole, and colon notation.

👨‍🍳 CULINARY ACADEMY

Scenario Launch

📋 Chef Academy Ratio Rookie

Welcome to Chef Academy! Head Chef Reyes is teaching new students how to read recipes like a pro. Her signature cookie recipe calls for 3 cups of flour for every 2 cups of sugar. Before the students can start baking, they need to understand how these ingredient amounts compare to each other.

Vocabulary Bank:

👀 Notice Prompts:

🔹 What two ingredients are being compared in the recipe?
🔹 How could you describe the relationship between flour and sugar?
🔹 Is the amount of flour more than, less than, or equal to the amount of sugar?

💭 Wonder Prompts:

🔹 What if we compared the sugar to the flour instead — would the ratio change?

Concept Introduction

💡 What is a ratio?

A ratio compares two amounts. It tells how much of one thing there is for another thing.

Key Idea:

A ratio compares two quantities, and the order you write them in matters.

Parte a parte

A ratio comparing one part of a group to another part.

Ex: 3 cups flour : 2 cups sugar (ingredient to ingredient)
