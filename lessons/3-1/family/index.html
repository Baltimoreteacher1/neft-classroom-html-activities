Unit 3, Lesson 1: Understand Ratios6.RP.1

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A ratio compares two amounts. It tells how much of one thing there is for another thing.

Key idea: A ratio compares two quantities, and the order you write them in matters.

Key words

  • Ratio — A way to compare two amounts, like 3 to 2.
    Español: Razón — Una manera de comparar dos cantidades, como 3 a 2.
  • Comparison — Looking at two or more amounts to see how they are related.
    Español: Comparación — Mirar dos o más cantidades para ver cómo se relacionan.
  • Part-to-part — A ratio comparing one part of a group to another part.
    Español: Parte a parte — Una razón que compara una parte de un grupo con otra parte.
  • Part-to-whole — A ratio comparing one part to the whole group.
    Español: Parte a todo — Una razón que compara una parte con todo el grupo.
  • Colon notation — Writing a ratio with two dots between the numbers, like 3:2.
    Español: Notación con dos puntos — Escribir una razón con dos puntos entre los números, como 3:2.

Example

  1. Chef Reyes uses 3 cups of flour for every 2 cups of sugar.
  2. I am comparing two amounts: flour and sugar.
  3. I write flour to sugar, in that order, as 3 to 2.
  4. In colon notation I write it as 3:2.
  5. So the ratio of flour to sugar is 3:2.

Practice at home

  1. A bag of trail mix contains 8 peanuts and 5 raisins. What is the ratio of raisins to total pieces?

Answer check

  1. A bag of trail mix contains 8 peanuts and 5 raisins. What is the ratio of raisins to total pieces? → 5:13

Common mistake

A common mistake in Understand Ratios is skipping the key idea: "A ratio compares two quantities, and the order you write them in matters." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
