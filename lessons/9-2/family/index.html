Unit 9, Lesson 2: Integers and Absolute Value6.NS.7

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

An integer is a whole number that can be positive, negative, or zero. The absolute value of a number is how far it is from zero on the number line. Distance is never negative.

Key idea: Absolute value tells you the distance from zero, so it is always positive or zero.

Key words

  • Integer — A whole number that can be positive, negative, or zero.
    Español: Número entero — Un número entero que puede ser positivo, negativo o cero.
  • Positive — A number bigger than zero, to the right of zero on a number line.
    Español: Positivo — Un número mayor que cero, a la derecha del cero en la recta numérica.
  • Negative — A number smaller than zero, to the left of zero on a number line.
    Español: Negativo — Un número menor que cero, a la izquierda del cero en la recta numérica.
  • Absolute value — How far a number is from zero. It is never negative.
    Español: Valor absoluto — Qué tan lejos está un número de cero. Nunca es negativo.
  • Opposite — Two numbers the same distance from zero, on opposite sides, like 3 and -3.
    Español: Opuesto — Dos números a la misma distancia de cero, en lados opuestos, como 3 y -3.

Example

  1. I want to find the absolute value of -6, written |-6|.
  2. I find -6 on the number line. It sits 6 steps to the left of zero.
  3. Distance does not care about direction, so -6 is 6 units from zero.
  4. So |-6| = 6. Even though -6 is negative, its absolute value is positive 6.

Practice at home

  1. Which integer has an absolute value of 8 and is located to the LEFT of zero on the number line?

Answer check

  1. Which integer has an absolute value of 8 and is located to the LEFT of zero on the number line? → -8

Common mistake

A common mistake in Integers and Absolute Value is skipping the key idea: "Absolute value tells you the distance from zero, so it is always positive or zero." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
