Key Vocabulary Level 1 support

Picture first, then the word, then a plain-language meaning. Say each word out loud.

3.75 means 3 ones + 7 tenths + 5 hundredths — it is between 3 and 4

Decimal

A number that uses a decimal point to show a value between whole numbers

In 4.38: the 4 = 4 ones, the 3 = 3 tenths (0.3), the 8 = 8 hundredths (0.08)

Place value

The value of a digit based on its position in a number

0.7 = 7/10 — like 7 slices of a pizza cut into 10 equal slices

Tenths

The first place to the right of the decimal point, representing parts out of 10

0.05 = 5/100 — like 5 pennies out of a dollar (100 pennies)

Hundredths

The second place to the right of the decimal point, representing parts out of 100

15.7 becomes 15.70 so you can subtract 15.70 − 8.36 with aligned place values

Annex zeros

Adding zeros to the end of a decimal to give numbers the same number of decimal places

My Notes

Guided Examples

Example 1

What is 14.6 + 3.85?

Solution: Line up decimals: 14.60 + 3.85. Hundredths: 0 + 5 = 5. Tenths: 6 + 8 = 14, write 4 carry 1. Ones: 4 + 3 + 1 = 8. Tens: 1. Answer: 18.45.

Answer: A. 18.45

Example 2

What is 20.3 − 7.48?

Solution: Rewrite as 20.30 − 7.48. Annex a zero so both have two decimal places. Work carefully with borrowing: 20.30 − 7.48 = 12.82.

Answer: A. 12.82

Example 3

What is 9.05 + 4.7?

Solution: Line up decimals: 9.05 + 4.70. Hundredths: 5 + 0 = 5. Tenths: 0 + 7 = 7. Ones: 9 + 4 = 13. Answer: 13.75.

Answer: A. 13.75

Answer Key

  1. Try It 1: A. 26.53 — Rewrite 50 as 50.00. Then 50.00 − 23.47 = 26.53. You must borrow across multiple places.
  2. Try It 2: A. $21.14 — 4.59 + 12.75 + 3.80 = 21.14. Line up decimal points and add column by column.
  3. Exit Ticket: A. 17.75 — Rewrite as 32.60 − 14.85. Hundredths: 0 − 5 → borrow → 10 − 5 = 5. Tenths: 5 − 8 → borrow → 15 − 8 = 7. Ones: 1 − 4 → borrow → 11 − 4 = 7. Tens: 2 − 1 = 1. Answer: 17.75.
