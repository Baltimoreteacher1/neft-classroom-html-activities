Unit 8, Lesson 5: Display Data: Box Plots6.SP.4

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A box plot is a simple picture that shows how a set of data is spread out, using just five key numbers: the lowest value, the first quartile (Q1), the median (middle), the third quartile (Q3), and the highest value.

Key idea: The box always holds the middle 50% of the data, and the line inside the box is the median.

Key words

  • Box Plot — A graph that shows how data is spread using five key numbers.
    Español: Diagrama de caja — Una gráfica que muestra cómo se reparten los datos con cinco números clave.
  • Median — The middle number that splits the data in half.
    Español: Mediana — El número del medio que divide los datos en dos mitades.
  • Quartile — Numbers that split the data into four equal parts.
    Español: Cuartil — Números que dividen los datos en cuatro partes iguales.
  • Interquartile Range — The spread of the middle half of the data (Q3 − Q1).
    Español: Rango intercuartílico — La extensión de la mitad central de los datos (Q3 − Q1).
  • Data distribution — How the data looks: where it sits and how spread out it is.
    Español: Distribución de datos — Cómo se ven los datos: dónde están y qué tan separados están.
  • Variability — How spread out the numbers are.
    Español: Variabilidad — Qué tan separados están los números.

Example

  1. My data, in order: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 18, 22, 24, 28. There are 11 numbers.
  2. The middle number is the 6th one, 14, so the median = 14.
  3. The lower half is 4, 6, 8, 10, 12. Its middle is 8, so Q1 = 8.
  4. The upper half is 15, 18, 22, 24, 28. Its middle is 22, so Q3 = 22.
  5. The lowest is 4 and the highest is 28. I draw a box from 8 to 22, a line at 14, and whiskers out to 4 and 28.

Practice at home

Match each box plot term to its meaning.

  1. Minimum
  2. Q1
  3. Median

Answer check

  1. Minimum → Smallest value in the data set
  2. Q1 → Median of the lower half
  3. Median → Middle value of the entire data set

Common mistake

A common mistake in Display Data: Box Plots is skipping the key idea: "The box always holds the middle 50% of the data, and the line inside the box is the median." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
