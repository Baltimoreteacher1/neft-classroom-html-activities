LESSON 8-5 · OBJECTIVES & TOPIC

🎯 Lesson Objectives

Content Objective:

I can make and read a box plot to summarize a data set.

Language Objective:

I can explain my box plot using the words box plot, median, quartile, and interquartile range.

STANDARD: 6.SP.4

📋 Team Scoring Breakdown

The basketball league wants to compare scoring across teams. Here are the points scored by each player on the Wildcats in their last game: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 18, 22, 24, 28. The league needs a quick visual that shows the spread, the middle 50%, and any high or low scorers. Time to build a box plot!

💡 What is a box plot?

A box plot is a simple picture that shows how a set of data is spread out, using just five key numbers: the lowest value, the first quartile (Q1), the median (middle), the third quartile (Q3), and the highest value.

Key Idea:

The box always holds the middle 50% of the data, and the line inside the box is the median.

BOX PLOT

Diagrama de caja

Click to flip ➔

A graph that shows how data is spread using five key numbers.

Una gráfica que muestra cómo se reparten los datos con cinco números clave.

Ex: A box from Q1 to Q3 with a line at the median, and whiskers from min to max
📝 A graph that shows data with a box and whiskers based on the five-number summary is a ___.

MEDIAN

Mediana

Click to flip ➔

The middle number that splits the data in half.

El número del medio que divide los datos en dos mitades.

Ex: Data: 10, 15, 20, 25, 30 → median is 20 (the 3rd value)
📝 The middle value of the data, shown as a line inside the box, is the ___.

QUARTILE

Cuartil

Click to flip ➔

Numbers that split the data into four equal parts.

Números que dividen los datos en cuatro partes iguales.

Ex: Data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14 → Q1 = 4 (median of lower half), Q3 = 12 (median of upper half)
📝 A value that divides the data into four equal parts is a ___.

INTERQUARTILE RANGE

Rango intercuartílico

Click to flip ➔

The spread of the middle half of the data (Q3 − Q1).

La extensión de la mitad central de los datos (Q3 − Q1).

Ex: If Q1 = 12 and Q3 = 20, then IQR = 20 − 12 = 8 — the middle 50% spans 8 units
📝 The distance between the first and third quartiles, showing the middle 50% of data, is the ___.

🛠️ Fix & Justify

Correct Work: Q1 is the median of the lower half (3, 7, 9), which is 7. Q3 is the median of the upper half (15, 18, 21), which is 18. The student used the wrong value for Q1 — they picked 9 (the last value in the lower half) instead of the median of that half.
