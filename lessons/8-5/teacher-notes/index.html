Warm-up

New, smaller data set: 2, 5, 5, 8, 11. First, what is the median (the middle value)?

Teaching notes

Key idea: The box always holds the middle 50% of the data, and the line inside the box is the median.

I do:

  1. My data, in order: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 18, 22, 24, 28. There are 11 numbers.
  2. The middle number is the 6th one, 14, so the median = 14.
  3. The lower half is 4, 6, 8, 10, 12. Its middle is 8, so Q1 = 8.
  4. The upper half is 15, 18, 22, 24, 28. Its middle is 22, so Q3 = 22.
  5. The lowest is 4 and the highest is 28. I draw a box from 8 to 22, a line at 14, and whiskers out to 4 and 28.

You do: On the next screens you'll find the five-number summary for the Wildcats' scores yourself: the minimum, Q1, the median, Q3, and the maximum. Then you'll place each value on the number line to build the box plot — just like I did.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Display Data: Box Plots is skipping the key idea: "The box always holds the middle 50% of the data, and the line inside the box is the median." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Box Plot, Median, Quartile, Interquartile Range, Data distribution, Variability (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A box plot has Q1 = 20 and Q3 = 36. What is the IQR and what does it represent?

  • IQR = 16; it is the spread of the middle 50% of the data ✓ correct
  • IQR = 56; it is the total of Q1 and Q3
  • IQR = 28; it is the median between Q1 and Q3
  • IQR = 36; it is the value of Q3

IQR = Q3 − Q1 = 36 − 20 = 16. The IQR tells you the range of the middle 50% of the data.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

Two basketball teams' scoring data are displayed as box plots. Team A: Min=40, Q1=52, Median=60, Q3=68, Max=75. Team B: Min=35, Q1=55, Median=61, Q3=65, Max=80. A fan says Team B is better because they scored 80 points once.
