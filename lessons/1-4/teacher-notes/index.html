Warm-up

Let's do 384 ÷ 8. What easy chunk of 8 can we take from 384? (8 × 40 = 320.)

Teaching notes

Key idea: Subtract easy chunks of the divisor, then add up how many you took out to get the quotient.

I do:

  1. I want 1,344 ÷ 12. The dividend is 1,344 and the divisor is 12.
  2. First I take a big easy chunk: 12 × 100 = 1,200. I subtract: 1,344 − 1,200 = 144.
  3. Now I divide 144: 12 × 12 = 144. I subtract: 144 − 144 = 0, so nothing is left.
  4. I add my chunks: 100 + 12 = 112. So 1,344 ÷ 12 = 112.
  5. I check by multiplying: 12 × 112 = 1,344. Correct, and the remainder is 0.

You do: Next, you will sort the steps that solve 1,344 ÷ 12 and then divide on your own. Remember: take away easy chunks, then add how many you used.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Divide Multi-Digit Numbers is skipping the key idea: "Subtract easy chunks of the divisor, then add up how many you took out to get the quotient." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Dividend, Divisor, Quotient, Remainder, Partial quotients (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

What is 2,352 ÷ 16?

  • 147 ✓ correct
  • 137
  • 157
  • 146

16 × 147 = 2,352. Check: 16 × 100 = 1,600, 16 × 40 = 640, 16 × 7 = 112. 1,600 + 640 + 112 = 2,352.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.
