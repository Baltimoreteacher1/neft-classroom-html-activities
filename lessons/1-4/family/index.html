Unit 1, Lesson 4: Divide Multi-Digit Numbers6.NS.2

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

Dividing means splitting a total into equal groups. Partial quotients let you divide a big number by taking away easy chunks until nothing is left.

Key idea: Subtract easy chunks of the divisor, then add up how many you took out to get the quotient.

Key words

  • Dividend — The number you are splitting up in a division problem.
    Español: Dividendo — El número que estás repartiendo en una división.
  • Divisor — The number you split by in a division problem.
    Español: Divisor — El número entre el que divides en una división.
  • Quotient — The answer when you divide.
    Español: Cociente — La respuesta cuando divides.
  • Remainder — What is left over when a number does not divide evenly.
    Español: Residuo — Lo que sobra cuando un número no se divide de forma exacta.
  • Partial quotients — A way to divide by breaking the problem into smaller, easier steps.
    Español: Cocientes parciales — Una manera de dividir separando el problema en pasos más fáciles.

Example

  1. I want 1,344 ÷ 12. The dividend is 1,344 and the divisor is 12.
  2. First I take a big easy chunk: 12 × 100 = 1,200. I subtract: 1,344 − 1,200 = 144.
  3. Now I divide 144: 12 × 12 = 144. I subtract: 144 − 144 = 0, so nothing is left.
  4. I add my chunks: 100 + 12 = 112. So 1,344 ÷ 12 = 112.
  5. I check by multiplying: 12 × 112 = 1,344. Correct, and the remainder is 0.

  2. 1,008 ÷ 16
  3. 1,440 ÷ 24

  2. 1,008 ÷ 16 → 63
  3. 1,440 ÷ 24 → 60

Common mistake

A common mistake in Divide Multi-Digit Numbers is skipping the key idea: "Subtract easy chunks of the divisor, then add up how many you took out to get the quotient." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
