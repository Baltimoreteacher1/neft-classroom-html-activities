Words First · Know these before you start

Vocabulary

Read each word, its meaning, and the example. These are the words you'll use in the lesson — learn them first, then practice.

🟪

area

Level 1 The amount of flat space inside a shape.

Example: A rug that covers 6 square feet of floor has an area of 6.

📏

base

Level 1 One flat side of a shape that you measure across the bottom.

Example: The bottom edge of this shape is 8 cm long, so the base is 8 cm.

📐

height

Level 1 How tall a shape is, measured straight up from the base.

Example: The straight-up distance is 5 cm, so the height is 5 cm.

▰

parallelogram

Level 1 A four-sided shape where both pairs of opposite sides are the same direction and never meet.

Example: A slanted box shape with two pairs of matching sides is a parallelogram.

🧮

square units

Level 1 The little squares we use to measure area, like square cm.

Example: If 12 small squares fit inside, the area is 12 square units.

Learn It

Area = base × height

Area is how many unit squares fit inside a shape. For a rectangle you multiply length × width. A parallelogram works the same way: multiply the base by the height (the straight-up distance, not the slanted side).

Rectangle 6 × 4: area = 6 × 4 = 24 square units.
The height is always straight up and down (perpendicular) from the base — it is NOT the slanted side of a parallelogram.

Hint

Hint

5 × 5 = 25 square units.

Level 1 Find the base and height, then multiply.

A. A parallelogram has a slanted side of 6 and a straight-up height of 4. Which number is the height?

Hint

Height is always perpendicular (straight up), so it is 4.

B. A parallelogram has base 8 and height 3. Area = 8 × 3 = ___?

Hint

Base × height = 8 × 3 = 24 square units.
