Unit 2, Lesson 4: Divide Mixed Numbers6.NS.1

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

A mixed number, like 3 1/2, has a whole part and a fraction part. Before you can divide, you change each mixed number into an improper fraction (top heavier than bottom). Then you use Keep, Change, Flip.

Key idea: Always convert a mixed number to an improper fraction first, then divide using Keep, Change, Flip.

Key words

  • Mixed Number — A whole number plus a fraction, like 2 1/3.
    Español: Número mixto — Un número entero más una fracción, como 2 1/3.
  • Improper Fraction — A fraction where the top is bigger than or equal to the bottom, like 7/4.
    Español: Fracción impropia — Una fracción donde el número de arriba es mayor o igual al de abajo, como 7/4.
  • Convert — To change a number to a new form but keep the same value.
    Español: Convertir — Cambiar un número a otra forma sin cambiar su valor.
  • Simplify — To make a fraction smaller using the same parts, like 2/4 = 1/2.
    Español: Simplificar — Hacer una fracción más pequeña con las mismas partes, como 2/4 = 1/2.
  • Reciprocal — A fraction turned upside down. You use it in keep-change-flip.
    Español: Recíproco — Una fracción volteada de arriba abajo. Se usa en deja-cambia-voltea.

Example

  1. Agent Chen has a 3 1/2-mile route cut into 1/4-mile segments. I want 3 1/2 ÷ 1/4.
  2. First I convert 3 1/2. Multiply the whole by the bottom: 3 × 2 = 6. Add the top: 6 + 1 = 7. So 3 1/2 = 7/2.
  3. Now Keep, Change, Flip: keep 7/2, change ÷ to ×, flip 1/4 to 4/1.
  4. Multiply: 7/2 × 4/1 = 28/2.
  5. Simplify: 28/2 = 14. So 3 1/2 ÷ 1/4 = 14 segments.

Practice at home

Match each mixed number division to its quotient.

  1. 2 1/4 ÷ 3/4
  2. 4 1/2 ÷ 3/2
  3. 1 2/3 ÷ 5/6

Answer check

  1. 2 1/4 ÷ 3/4 → 3
  2. 4 1/2 ÷ 3/2 → 3
  3. 1 2/3 ÷ 5/6 → 2

Common mistake

A common mistake in Divide Mixed Numbers is skipping the key idea: "Always convert a mixed number to an improper fraction first, then divide using Keep, Change, Flip." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
