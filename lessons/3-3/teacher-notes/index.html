Unit 3, Lesson 3: Graph Ratio Tables6.RP.3a

Everything you need to teach this tomorrow. ~45 min.

Objectives

Content: I can graph the values from a ratio table as points on the coordinate plane.

Language: I can explain my graph using the words coordinate plane, ordered pair, origin, and proportional.

Before class

  • Print / open: guided notes, homework, slides.
  • Have the family page link ready for absent students.
  • Preview the key vocabulary below with ESOL students.

Warm-up

A recipe uses 1 cup of sugar for every 3 cups of flour.

Teaching notes

Key idea: The points from equal ratios form a straight line that passes through the origin (0, 0).

I do:

  1. The recipe uses 2 ounces of sauce for every 1 sundae.
  2. I put sundaes on the x-axis and ounces of sauce on the y-axis.
  3. For 1 sundae I need 2 ounces, so I plot the ordered pair (1, 2): right 1, up 2.
  4. For 2 sundaes I need 4 ounces, so I plot (2, 4): right 2, up 4.
  5. When I connect (1, 2), (2, 4), (3, 6), they form a straight line through the origin (0, 0).

You do: Next you will plot the sundae points on the coordinate grid. Check that your points line up straight through the origin.

Common misconception

A common mistake in Graph Ratio Tables is skipping the key idea: "The points from equal ratios form a straight line that passes through the origin (0, 0)." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

ESOL / SPED supports

  • Pre-teach: Coordinate plane, Ordered pair, Linear pattern, Proportional, Origin (Spanish + visuals are in the lesson vocabulary).
  • Offer the sentence frames: “I know ___ because ___.” “First, I ___. Then, I ___.” “The answer is ___, so ___.”
  • Chunk the I-do steps; let students restate each step to a partner before moving on.
  • Provide the worked example as a reference while they practice.

Exit ticket

A ratio table shows cups of rice to cups of water: (1, 2), (2, 4), (3, 6). If you plot these points, the line passes through which point?

  • (5, 10) ✓ correct
  • (4, 6)
  • (5, 8)
  • (6, 10)

The ratio is 1:2, so for 5 cups of rice you need 5 × 2 = 10 cups of water. The point (5, 10) continues the pattern.

Reteach

Re-run the I-do example with smaller numbers and have students narrate each step using the key idea.

Extension

A food truck tracks how many tacos it sells and how much salsa it uses. After recording data for a week, the owner plots the points on a graph and notices they form a straight line. This helps the owner predict exactly how much salsa to prepare for any number of tacos.
