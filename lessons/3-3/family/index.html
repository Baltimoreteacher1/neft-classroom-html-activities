Unit 3, Lesson 3: Graph Ratio Tables6.RP.3a

A quick guide for families and for any student who missed class.

What students are learning

An ordered pair (x, y) tells where to plot a point: go right x, then up y. When you graph equal ratios, the points make a straight line through the origin (0, 0).

Key idea: The points from equal ratios form a straight line that passes through the origin (0, 0).

Key words

  • Coordinate plane — A grid with a line going across and a line going up to plot points.
    Español: Plano cartesiano — Una cuadrícula con una línea horizontal y una vertical para marcar puntos.
  • Ordered pair — Two numbers (x, y) that tell where a point is on a grid.
    Español: Par ordenado — Dos números (x, y) que dicen dónde está un punto en una cuadrícula.
  • Linear pattern — Points that make a straight line on a grid.
    Español: Patrón lineal — Puntos que forman una línea recta en una cuadrícula.
  • Proportional — Two amounts that grow together at the same rate.
    Español: Proporcional — Dos cantidades que crecen juntas al mismo ritmo.
  • Origin — The point (0, 0) where the two grid lines cross.
    Español: Origen — El punto (0, 0) donde se cruzan las dos líneas de la cuadrícula.

Example

  1. The recipe uses 2 ounces of sauce for every 1 sundae.
  2. I put sundaes on the x-axis and ounces of sauce on the y-axis.
  3. For 1 sundae I need 2 ounces, so I plot the ordered pair (1, 2): right 1, up 2.
  4. For 2 sundaes I need 4 ounces, so I plot (2, 4): right 2, up 4.
  5. When I connect (1, 2), (2, 4), (3, 6), they form a straight line through the origin (0, 0).

Practice at home

  1. A ratio table shows cups of rice to cups of water: (1, 2), (2, 4), (3, 6). If you plot these points, the line passes through which point?

Answer check

  1. A ratio table shows cups of rice to cups of water: (1, 2), (2, 4), (3, 6). If you plot these points, the line passes through which point? → (5, 10)

Common mistake

A common mistake in Graph Ratio Tables is skipping the key idea: "The points from equal ratios form a straight line that passes through the origin (0, 0)." — always check your work against this rule before you submit.

How families can help at home

  • Ask your student to teach you the example above in their own words.
  • Have them say why each step works, not just the answer.
  • Use the sentence starters below so they explain their thinking in full sentences.
