Scenario Launch

📋 Scenario Launch

👩‍🏫 Say: Read the scenario aloud slowly. Give students 10 seconds of think time.

Chef Academy students are tracking how much chocolate sauce they need for different numbers of sundaes. They know the recipe uses 2 ounces of sauce for every 1 sundae. Chef Reyes challenges them to plot the ratio table values on a coordinate grid to see the pattern. Will the points form a straight line?

👀 Notice:

🔹 What pattern do you see in the ratio table values?
🔹 What do you think the graph will look like?

💭 Wonder:

🔹 Could we use the graph to predict amounts not in our table?

Concept Launch

💡 How do we graph a ratio table?

👩‍🏫 Say: This is the big idea for today. Students should be able to repeat it by the end.

An ordered pair (x, y) tells where to plot a point: go right x, then up y. When you graph equal ratios, the points make a straight line through the origin (0, 0).

Key Idea:

The points from equal ratios form a straight line that passes through the origin (0, 0).

Watch me

I put sundaes on the x-axis and ounces of sauce on the y-axis.
For 1 sundae I need 2 ounces, so I plot the ordered pair (1, 2): right 1, up 2.
For 2 sundaes I need 4 ounces, so I plot (2, 4): right 2, up 4.
When I connect (1, 2), (2, 4), (3, 6), they form a straight line through the origin (0, 0).

Visual Vocabulary

Coordinate plane

Plano cartesiano

A grid with a line going across and a line going up to plot points.

Una cuadrícula con una línea horizontal y una vertical para marcar puntos.

Example: A + shape made by two number lines crossing at the center point (0, 0)

Ordered pair

Par ordenado
