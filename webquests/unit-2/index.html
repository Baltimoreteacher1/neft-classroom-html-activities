Fraction Kitchen WebQuest: Dividing Fractions

Grade 6 Math · You will divide fractions to fill bakery orders.

1. Introduction

Welcome to the Fraction Kitchen! A bakery gets big orders. To share the dough, you must divide fractions. Example: A baker has 3/4 cup of jam. Each tart needs 1/8 cup. How many tarts can the baker fill? In this WebQuest you will learn to divide fractions and answer real bakery questions.

2. Task

By the end, you will:

  • Show how to divide a fraction by a fraction.
  • Use the rule: keep, change, flip (multiply by the reciprocal).
  • Finish the Check Your Understanding and save it as a PDF or DOC to turn in.

3. Process (do these steps in order)

  1. Learn the words. Read the Vocabulary box below.
  2. Play the game. Open the Unit 2 — Fraction Kitchen 3D game and fill 3 orders.
  3. Review the rule. To divide, keep the first fraction, change ÷ to ×, and flip the second fraction. Example: 3/4 ÷ 1/8 = 3/4 × 8/1 = 24/4 = 6.
  4. Practice more. Use the Neft Teacher math activities for Unit 2.
  5. Show what you know. Type your name in the box, then answer the Check Your Understanding questions and click Grade my answers.

Vocabulary

Fraction
A number that shows part of a whole, like 3/4.
Reciprocal (flip)
Turn a fraction upside down. The reciprocal of 1/8 is 8/1.
Divide (÷)
Split into equal groups. "How many groups fit?"
Quotient
The answer you get when you divide.
