📦 The Volume Vault WebQuest

Volume of Prisms — pack the box, find the space inside!

1. Introduction

Welcome to the Volume Vault! 📦 Your job is to fill boxes (called rectangular prisms) with little unit cubes.

Volume tells you how much space is inside a box. We measure it in cubic units (like cm³ or in³). The more cubes that fit inside, the bigger the volume.

In this WebQuest, you will find the volume of prisms.

2. Task

By the end, you will be able to:

  • Name the length, width, and height of a box.
  • Use the formula V = l × w × h to find volume.
  • Find volume even when the sides are fractions (like ½).
  • Solve a real box problem and finish the short quiz below.

3. Process

Do the steps in order. Read each one carefully.

  1. Step 1 — Learn the words

    Volume = the space inside a box. Unit cube = one small cube that is 1 unit on each side. Cubic units = how we count volume.

  2. Step 2 — Learn the formula

    Volume = length × width × height

    First multiply length × width to get the bottom layer. Then multiply by the height to stack the layers.

  3. Step 3 — Play the 3D game

    Open the Volume Vault 3D game. Pack each prism with unit cubes and watch the volume add up.

  4. Step 4 — Try fraction sides

    A box can have a side like ½ unit. The same formula still works: V = l × w × h. Example: 4 × 3 × ½ = 6 cubic units.

  5. Step 5 — Show what you know

    Type your name in the bar above, finish the quiz, then Save as PDF or DOC to turn in.

💡 Tip: Volume always uses cubic units because a box has 3 directions: long, wide, and tall.
