📐 The Area Architect WebQuest

Area of Polygons — measure, build, and prove every shape!

1. Introduction

Welcome, Area Architect! 🏗️ A new park needs floor plans, and you are the math behind the build.

Area is the number of unit squares that fit inside a flat shape. We measure it in square units (like cm² or ft²). In this WebQuest you will find the area of rectangles, triangles, and composite figures (shapes made of smaller shapes).

2. Task

By the end, you will be able to:

  • Find the area of a rectangle (length × width).
  • Find the area of a triangle (½ × base × height).
  • Find the area of a composite figure by adding parts.
  • Solve a real floor-plan problem and finish the short quiz below.

3. Process

Do the steps in order. Read each one carefully.

  1. Step 1 — Learn the words

    Area = space inside a flat shape. Base and height must meet at a right angle (a square corner).

  2. Step 2 — Learn the formulas

    Rectangle: Area = length × width
    Triangle: Area = ½ × base × height
    Composite: cut it into rectangles & triangles, then add

  3. Step 3 — Play the 3D game

    Open the Area Architect 3D game. Build blocks to match the gold target area. Watch how length × width fills the shape.

  4. Step 4 — Try a composite figure

    An L-shaped room splits into two rectangles. Find each area, then add them together.

  5. Step 5 — Show what you know

    Type your name in the bar above, finish the quiz, then Save as PDF or DOC to turn in.

💡 Tip: A triangle is exactly half of a rectangle with the same base and height. That is why we multiply by ½.
