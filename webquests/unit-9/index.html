Chart the Star Map: The Coordinate Plane

Grade 6 Math · Standards 6.NS.C.6 & 6.G.A.3

Introduction

You are a space navigator. Each star sits at an ordered pair like (x, y) on a star map. The map is a coordinate plane with four parts called quadrants. Your job is to read points, plot points, and draw shapes so your ship can reach every star.

Your Task

By the end you will:

  • Name and plot points like (-3, 4) in all four quadrants.
  • Find which quadrant a point is in by its signs.
  • Plot the corners of a shape and find a side length by counting units.
  • Pass a short Check (6 questions) and save it for grading.

Process — Follow the Steps

  1. Learn the words. The x-axis goes left and right. The y-axis goes up and down. They cross at the origin (0, 0).
  2. Read a point. In (x, y), the first number is x (left/right). The second number is y (up/down). Right and up are positive (+). Left and down are negative (-).
  3. Know the quadrants. Quadrant I = (+, +), II = (-, +), III = (-, -), IV = (+, -).
  4. Play the 3D game below to plot stars and practice.
  5. Draw a shape. Plot 4 corners on grid paper and count units to find a side length.
  6. Take the Check at the bottom. Type your name, answer the 6 questions, press Check My Answers, then Save as PDF or DOC.
Tip: To find a side length on the grid, count the units between two corners. If only the sign of x changes, add the two distances from 0.
