📖 6.RP.A.1 — Understanding Ratios

A ratio compares two quantities. Learn to write them, say them, and use them!

⚖️

What Is a Ratio?

A ratio compares two quantities.
Example: 3 cats and 5 dogs → ratio of cats to dogs is 3 to 5.
Order matters — always name the quantities in the same order!

✍️

3 Ways to Write a Ratio

You can write the same ratio three ways:
3 to 5 — words
3:5 — colon notation
3/5 — fraction form
All three mean the same thing!

🔁

Part-to-Part vs Part-to-Whole

Part-to-part: cats to dogs = 3:5
Part-to-whole: cats to all pets = 3:8
Always read carefully which comparison is asked for!

🔤

Ratio Language

"For every 2 wings there is 1 beak."
"The ratio of boys to girls is 4 to 3."
"There are 5 red for every 2 blue."
Use precise language to describe ratios!

🔑 Key Idea: A ratio does NOT have to be written as a fraction. 3:5 and 3 to 5 and 3/5 all represent the same ratio — but only 3/5 is a fraction. A ratio is a comparison!

📊 Three Ways to Write a Ratio

🐦 Example: Bird House — There are 6 wings and 3 beaks.
The ratio of wings to beaks:
6 to 3
Word form
6:3
Colon form
6/3
Fraction form
"For every 2 wings there is 1 beak." (simplified: 2:1)
🍎🍊 Example: Fruit Bowl — 4 apples and 6 oranges (10 fruit total)
Ratio of apples to oranges (part-to-part): 4:6
Ratio of apples to all fruit (part-to-whole): 4:10
Ratio of oranges to apples (reversed!): 6:4 ← different ratio!
⚠️ Watch out! The ratio of A to B is NOT the same as the ratio of B to A. Order always matters!
