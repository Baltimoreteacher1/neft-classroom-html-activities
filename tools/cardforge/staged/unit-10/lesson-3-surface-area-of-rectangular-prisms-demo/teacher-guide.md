# Teacher Guide — Surface Area of Rectangular Prisms

> CardForge package · **DEMO / sample** · status: **draft**

## Overview
Students unfold a rectangular prism into a net, see its six faces, and add the face areas to find total surface area, then use the formula SA = 2(lw + lh + wh).

## Objective & Standard
- **Objective:** I can find the surface area of a rectangular prism.
- **Language objective:** I can explain my work using the words net, face, and surface area.
- **Standard:** 6.G.4
- **Time:** ~45 min

## Materials
- Pencil and practice page
- Optional: a small box to unfold

## Key Vocabulary (ESOL-friendly)
- **Face** _(es: Cara)_ — A flat side of a solid figure. _(Un lado plano de una figura sólida.)_
  - Example: A box has 6 faces
- **Net** _(es: Red / Plantilla)_ — A flat pattern that folds into a solid. _(Un patrón plano que se dobla para formar un sólido.)_
  - Example: Unfold a box and it lies flat as a net
- **Surface area** _(es: Área de superficie)_ — The total area of all the faces of a solid. _(El área total de todas las caras de un sólido.)_
  - Example: Add the area of all 6 faces
- **Rectangular prism** _(es: Prisma rectangular)_ — A box-shaped solid with 6 rectangular faces. _(Un sólido en forma de caja con 6 caras rectangulares.)_
  - Example: A cereal box

## Formulas / Definitions
- SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)  — add the three different face areas, then double
- Cube: SA = 6 × (side × side)

## Modeled Examples (I do)
**Modeled Example 1.** Find the surface area of a prism with length 4, width 3, height 2 units.

   1. Find the three face areas: lw = 4×3 = 12, lh = 4×2 = 8, wh = 3×2 = 6.
   2. Add them: 12 + 8 + 6 = 26.
   3. Double for the opposite faces: 2 × 26 = 52.

   **Answer:** 52 square units

**Modeled Example 2.** Find the surface area of a cube with side 5 units.

   1. One face area = 5 × 5 = 25.
   2. A cube has 6 equal faces: 6 × 25 = 150.

   **Answer:** 150 square units

## Common Misconceptions
- **Forgetting to double, so only 3 faces are counted.** → Every face has a matching opposite face, so multiply the three areas by 2.
- **Finding volume (l×w×h) instead of surface area.** → Surface area adds the face areas; volume fills the inside. Different question, different work.

## Supports
- **ESOL:** Bilingual EN/ES vocab box; Draw and label the net's 6 faces before adding; Sentence frame: 'The surface area is ___ because I added ___ faces.'
- **SPED:** Provide the labeled net for problems 1–2; Allow a calculator; Give the three products and have students add and double
