Day 1

Mean, Median, Mode, and Range · 6.SP.3

Warm-Up

Put these numbers in order from least to greatest: 7, 2, 9, 4, 2. (Answer: 2, 2, 4, 7, 9.) Which number appears most often?

Worked Example

Find the mean, median, mode, and range of the test scores: 80, 85, 90, 85, 70.

  • Order the data: 70, 80, 85, 85, 90.
  • Mean: 70 + 80 + 85 + 85 + 90 = 410, then 410 ÷ 5 = 82.
  • Median: the middle of five numbers is the 3rd one → 85.
  • Mode: 85 appears twice (most often) → 85.
  • Range: 90 − 70 = 20.

Answer: Mean = 82, Median = 85, Mode = 85, Range = 20.

Practice — show your work

  1. Find the mean of 4, 8, 6, 2, 5.
  2. Find the median of 3, 9, 1, 7, 5.
  3. Find the mode of 2, 4, 4, 6, 4, 8.

Day 2

Mean, Median, Mode, and Range · 6.SP.3

Warm-Up

Look back at yesterday's example before you start.

Worked Example

Find the mean, median, mode, and range of reading minutes: 10, 12, 15, 18.

  • Order the data: 10, 12, 15, 18 (already in order).
  • Mean: 10 + 12 + 15 + 18 = 55, then 55 ÷ 4 = 13.75.
  • Median: with four numbers, average the two middles → (12 + 15) ÷ 2 = 13.5.
  • Mode: every number appears once → there is no mode.
  • Range: 18 − 10 = 8.

Answer: Mean = 13.75, Median = 13.5, Mode = none, Range = 8.

Practice — show your work

  1. Find the range of 12, 7, 20, 5, 16.
  2. Test scores: 88, 92, 100, 88, 76, 96. Find the mean, median, mode, and range.
  3. Daily steps: 5000, 7000, 6000, 8000, 6000. Find the mean, median, mode, and range.

Practice — show your work

Challenge (Early Finishers)
