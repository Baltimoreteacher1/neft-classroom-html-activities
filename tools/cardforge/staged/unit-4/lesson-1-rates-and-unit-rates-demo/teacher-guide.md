# Teacher Guide — Rates and Unit Rates

> CardForge package · **DEMO / sample** · status: **draft**

## Overview
Students turn a rate into a unit rate (an amount for exactly 1) and use unit rates to compare options and find the better buy.

## Objective & Standard
- **Objective:** I can find a unit rate and use it to solve and compare.
- **Language objective:** I can explain a unit rate using the words rate, unit rate, and per.
- **Standard:** 6.RP.2
- **Time:** ~45 min

## Materials
- Pencil and practice page
- Optional: calculator

## Warm-Up

## Key Vocabulary (ESOL-friendly)
- **Ratio** _(es: Razón)_ — A comparison of two amounts. _(Una comparación de dos cantidades.)_
  - Example: 3 cups of flour to 2 cups of sugar = 3 : 2
- **Rate** _(es: Tasa)_ — A ratio that compares two different units. _(Una razón que compara dos unidades diferentes.)_
  - Example: 150 miles in 3 hours
- **Unit rate** _(es: Tasa unitaria)_ — A rate for exactly 1 of something. _(Una tasa para exactamente 1 de algo.)_
  - Example: 150 miles in 3 hours = 50 miles per hour
- **Per** _(es: Por)_ — The word that means 'for each one'. _(La palabra que significa 'por cada uno'.)_
  - Example: $0.50 per pencil

## Formulas / Definitions
- unit rate = total amount ÷ number of units
- better buy = the lower price per unit

## Modeled Examples (I do)
**Modeled Example 1.** 12 apples cost $6. Find the price per apple.

   1. A rate is $6 for 12 apples.
   2. Divide to get the price for 1 apple: 6 ÷ 12 = 0.5.
   3. So the unit rate is $0.50 per apple.

   **Answer:** $0.50 per apple

**Modeled Example 2.** A car travels 150 miles in 3 hours. Find the miles per hour.

   1. The rate is 150 miles in 3 hours.
   2. Divide to get miles for 1 hour: 150 ÷ 3 = 50.
   3. The unit rate is 50 miles per hour.

   **Answer:** 50 miles per hour

## Common Misconceptions
- **Dividing the wrong way (number of units ÷ total).** → Unit rate is total ÷ number of units, so the answer is 'amount for 1'.
- **Thinking the bigger package is always the better buy.** → Compare the price per unit, not the total price.

## Supports
- **ESOL:** Bilingual EN/ES vocab box with examples; Sentence frame: 'The unit rate is ___ per ___ because ___.'; Label the units (dollars, pencils) before dividing
- **SPED:** Provide the division set up for the first 2 problems; Allow a calculator; Reduce to problems 1–4 plus the better-buy item
