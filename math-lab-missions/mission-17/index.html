Mission 17 · Unit 7

Rational Numbers

6.NS.C.5 · 6.NS.C.6 · 6.NS.C.7

A marine biologist is studying ocean creatures at different depths. A dolphin swims at -3.5 meters (below sea level). A sea turtle dives to -12¾ meters. A flying fish jumps to +2.25 meters (above sea level). Your team must place these animals on a vertical number line, compare their positions, order them from deepest to highest, and find the distance between the dolphin and the sea turtle.

The Investigation

The Problem: Three ocean animals are at these positions relative to sea level: flying fish at +2.25 m, dolphin at -3.5 m, sea turtle at -12¾ m (which is -12.75 m). Place all three on a vertical number line. Order them from deepest to highest. Then calculate the distance between the dolphin and the sea turtle. Finally: which animal is closest to sea level?

Visual Model: Vertical Number Line

Step-by-Step Investigation Guide

  1. Convert to the same form. Write -12¾ as a decimal: -12.75. Now all three positions are decimals. Guiding question: How do you convert ¾ to a decimal?
  2. Draw the vertical number line. Mark sea level as 0. Place positive numbers above and negative below. Plot all three creatures. Guiding question: Why does -12.75 go farther below 0 than -3.5?
  3. Compare positions. Which is deepest? Which is highest? Use < and > to compare each pair. Guiding question: Is -12.75 greater or less than -3.5? How do you know?
  4. Order from least to greatest. -12.75 < -3.5 < +2.25. Write this ordering. Guiding question: On a vertical number line, does "less than" mean higher or lower?
  5. Find the distance. Distance between dolphin and turtle: |-3.5 - (-12.75)| = |-3.5 + 12.75| = |9.25| = 9.25 m. Guiding question: Why do we use absolute value for distance?
  6. Identify closest to sea level. Compare |+2.25| = 2.25, |-3.5| = 3.5, |-12.75| = 12.75. Flying fish is closest (2.25 m from 0). Guiding question: What does absolute value tell us about position?

ESOL Support: Key Vocabulary

Rational number
Any number that can be written as a fraction: integers, decimals, fractions.
Negative number
A number less than zero. Written with a minus sign: -3.5.
Positive number
A number greater than zero: +2.25 or just 2.25.
Absolute value
The distance from zero. Always positive. |-5| = 5.
Opposite
Same distance from zero, other side. The opposite of -3 is +3.
Sea level
The surface of the ocean. We call this zero (0 meters).
Sentence Frames:

"_____ is deeper than _____ because it is farther below sea level."

"The absolute value of _____ is _____, which means it is _____ meters from sea level."

"I know -12.75 < -3.5 because on the number line, -12.75 is _____."

Multiple Representations

Approach 1: Vertical Number Line

Draw a vertical line with 0 in the middle. Plot each value. The lowest point is the least value. Read the order by going up the line.

Approach 2: Absolute Value Comparison

Find each distance from zero:
|+2.25| = 2.25
|-3.5| = 3.5
|-12.75| = 12.75
The smallest absolute value is closest to sea level.

Approach 3: Real-World Reasoning

Think of an elevator: floor -12 is much lower than floor -3. A positive floor is above ground. You would ride the elevator 9.25 floors from -3.5 to -12.75.

Real-Life Connections

Negative numbers appear in temperatures below zero, bank account overdrafts, elevations below sea level (Death Valley is -86 m), and golf scores under par.
