Mission 13 · Unit 6

Expressions

6.EE.A.1 · 6.EE.A.2 · 6.EE.A.3

The sixth-grade class is running a school store that sells notebooks for $3 each and pens for $1.50 each. There is also a flat $2 daily table rental fee to set up the store. Your team must write expressions for the daily revenue, total costs, and profit. Then evaluate each expression for specific customer scenarios, interpret what each part means in context, and recommend whether the store is worth running on days with different sales volumes.

Team Roles

Facilitator Reads the school store scenario aloud, ensures everyone identifies the quantities before writing expressions, and watches the timer.
Model Builder Writes the expressions using correct notation, labels each part (variable, coefficient, constant, operator), and draws a diagram connecting math to the story.
Precision Checker Evaluates each expression by substituting values, checks the order of operations, and verifies that results make sense in dollars and cents.
Reporter Prepares the defense: states the profit expression, shows two evaluations, and explains what each part of the expression means in context.

Investigation

The Problem

The sixth-grade school store sells notebooks for $3 each and pens for $1.50 each. Every day, there is a $2 table rental fee the store must pay.

Your tasks:

  1. Define variables for the unknown quantities.
  2. Write an expression for the total revenue (money coming in from sales).
  3. Write an expression for the total cost (the daily fee).
  4. Write an expression for the daily profit (revenue minus cost).
  5. Evaluate each expression for three different scenarios.
  6. Identify and label every part of your expressions: variables, coefficients, constants, and operators.

The Three Expressions

Revenue (money in)

3n + 1.5p

Total dollars earned from selling n notebooks and p pens.

Cost (money out)

2

The fixed daily table rental fee -- it does not change with sales.

Profit (what's left)

3n + 1.5p - 2

Revenue minus cost. Positive = the store earned money. Negative = the store lost money.

Step-by-Step Investigation Guide

  1. Identify the quantities and define variables What quantities change each day? What stays the same? Let n = number of notebooks sold and p = number of pens sold. The $3, $1.50, and $2 are fixed values.

    Why do we use variables for notebooks and pens but not for the rental fee?

  2. Write the revenue expression Revenue = (price per notebook)(notebooks sold) + (price per pen)(pens sold) = 3n + 1.5p.

    What does 3n mean in words? What does 1.5p mean? Why do we add them together?

  3. Write the profit expression Profit = Revenue - Cost = 3n + 1.5p - 2. The -2 represents the daily rental fee being subtracted.

    Is the "-2" part of a term, or is it a separate constant? How can you tell?

  4. Label every part of the expression Identify: variables coefficients constants operators and terms.

    How many terms does the profit expression have? Can you name each one?

  5. Evaluate for Scenario A: n = 10, p = 8 Substitute: 3(10) + 1.5(8) - 2. Follow order of operations: multiply first, then add and subtract. Profit = 30 + 12 - 2 = $40.

    Does $40 profit seem reasonable for selling 10 notebooks and 8 pens? How can you check?

  6. Evaluate for Scenario B: n = 0, p = 1 Substitute: 3(0) + 1.5(1) - 2 = 0 + 1.50 - 2 = -$0.50. A negative profit means the store LOST money.

    What does it mean in the real world when profit is negative? How many items must the store sell to at least break even?

  7. Find the break-even point and make a recommendation Find combinations of n and p where 3n + 1.5p - 2 = 0 (break even) or greater than 0 (profit). Write a recommendation: is the store worth running?

    What is the minimum number of notebooks only (p = 0) needed to break even? What about pens only (n = 0)?

In the expression 3n + 1.5p - 2:

  • Terms: 3n, 1.5p, and 2 (there are 3 terms)
  • Variables: n and p (they change)
  • Coefficients: 3 (in front of n) and 1.5 (in front of p)
  • Constant: 2 (it never changes)
  • Operators: + (add revenue from pens) and - (subtract cost)

To break even, profit must equal 0:

3n + 1.5p - 2 = 0, which means 3n + 1.5p = 2

  • If only notebooks (p = 0): 3n = 2, so n = 2/3. You cannot sell 2/3 of a notebook, so you need at least 1 notebook to make a profit.
  • If only pens (n = 0): 1.5p = 2, so p = 4/3. You need at least 2 pens to make a profit.

ESOL Support

Key Vocabulary

Expression: A math phrase with numbers, variables, and operations (no equal sign)
Variable: A letter that stands for a number that can change
Coefficient: The number multiplied by a variable (the "3" in 3n)
Constant: A number that stays the same and has no variable
Term: A single piece of an expression (like 3n or 1.5p or 2)
Evaluate: Substitute numbers for variables and calculate the answer
Revenue: Total money received from selling items
Profit: Money left after subtracting costs from revenue

Sentence Frames

  • "The variable ___ represents ___ because it can change each day."
  • "The coefficient ___ tells me the price of each ___, which is $___."
  • "When I substitute n = ___ and p = ___, the profit is $___ because..."
  • "The expression 3n + 1.5p - 2 means: earn $3 for each ___, plus $1.50 for each ___, minus $2 for ___."

Real-Life Connection

Every business uses expressions like these to calculate profit and loss. A lemonade stand, a restaurant, and even a billion-dollar company all start the same way: Revenue - Costs = Profit. Understanding expressions helps you read financial statements, plan budgets, and make smart decisions about money.

Defense Preparation

Defense Questions

  1. What does each variable in your expression represent? Why did you choose those letters?
  2. Name the coefficient in the term 1.5p. What does it mean in the context of the store?
  3. Show the substitution work for Scenario A (n=10, p=8). What is the profit?
  4. When is the profit negative? What does that mean for the school store?
  5. How did your team find the break-even point? Explain your reasoning.

Sentence Starters for Your Defense
