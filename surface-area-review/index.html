Visual Formulas

Surface Area is the total area of all the outside surfaces (faces) of a 3D solid. To find the surface area, sum up the individual area of every face.

Rectangular Prism

Prisma Rectangular: Tiene 6 caras. Suma las áreas de las 6 caras rectangulares.
SA = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh

(Faces: 2 Bottom/Top, 2 Front/Back, 2 Left/Right)

Cube

Cubo: Tiene 6 caras cuadradas idénticas.
SA = 6s²

(Where s is the side length of one face)

Triangular Prism

Prisma Triangular: Tiene 5 caras (2 bases triangulares idénticas y 3 rectángulos laterales).
SA = (2 × Triangle Base Area) + (3 Lat. Rectangles)

Vocabulary snapshot

Hover over or read the terms below to build a solid geometric foundation.

📦 Net
Red: El plano bidimensional de una figura 3D.

A flat, 2D pattern of all the faces of a 3D solid that can fold up to create the solid.

💎 Face
Cara: Una superficie plana de un sólido.

A flat surface of a 3D solid. Rectangular prisms have 6; triangular prisms have 5.

📐 Height (h)
Altura: La distancia perpendicular entre bases.

The perpendicular distance of the solid or the flat height of its bases.

🐦 Birdhouse Painting

🧩 Rubik's Cube

A giant decorative Rubik's Cube has a side length of 9 inches on every face. What is the total surface area of all six flat square faces combined?

Calcula el área de un cubo con lado = 9. SA = 6 × s²
